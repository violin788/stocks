    <div style="color: rgb(0, 0, 0); text-indent: 27pt;">On August 10, 2020, Motorcar Parts of America, Inc. (the &#8220;Company&#8221;) issued a press release announcing its earnings for the fiscal quarter ended June 30, 2020 which is being furnished as Exhibit 99.1.
      The information contained herein and in the accompanying exhibit shall not be incorporated by reference into any filing of the Company, whether made before or after the date hereof, regardless of any general incorporation language in such filing,
      unless expressly incorporated by specific reference to such filing. The information in this report, including the exhibit hereto, shall not be deemed to be &#8220;filed&#8221; for purposes of Section 18 of the Securities Exchange Act of 1934, as amended, or
      otherwise subject to the liabilities of that section or Sections 11 and 12(a)(2) of the Securities Act of 1933, as amended.</div>
    <div>&#160;</div>
    <div style="text-align: justify; color: rgb(0, 0, 0); text-indent: 27pt;">The attached exhibit includes non-GAAP adjusted gross profit and non-GAAP adjusted gross margin. The Company believes that these supplemental non- GAAP financial measures, when
      presented together with the corresponding GAAP financial measures, provide useful information to investors and management regarding financial and business trends relating to its business. However, non-GAAP financial measures have certain limitations
      in that they do not reflect all of the costs associated with the operations of the Company&#8217;s business as determined in accordance with GAAP. Therefore, investors should consider non-GAAP financial measures as a supplement to, and not as a substitute
      for, or as superior to, measures of financial performance prepared in accordance with GAAP.</div>
    <div>&#160;</div>
    <div style="color: rgb(0, 0, 0); text-indent: 27pt;">The Company makes adjustments to the following items to calculate its non-GAAP financial measures:</div>
    <div>&#160;</div>
    <div style="text-align: justify; color: rgb(0, 0, 0); text-indent: 27pt;"><font style="font-style: italic;">New product line start-up costs and transition expenses. </font>These are start-up costs incurred prior to recognizing sales for the launch of
      new product lines and costs of ramping up production. Transition expenses are costs incurred in connection with the expansion of the Company&#8217;s operations in Mexico. The Company excluded start-up and ramp-up costs, and transition expenses because they
      do not reflect the Company&#8217;s operations on an ongoing basis and excluding such costs enables period-over period comparability.</div>
    <div>&#160;</div>
    <div style="text-align: justify; color: rgb(0, 0, 0); text-indent: 27pt;"><font style="font-style: italic;">Revaluation - cores on customers&#8217; shelves. </font>On a quarterly basis, the Company revalues cores on customers&#8217; shelves, which are included as
      part of contract assets on the balance sheet. The revaluation is in accordance with the Company&#8217;s accounting policies on contract assets. The impact of this revaluation is reflected in cost of goods sold. The Company excluded the revaluation for
      cores on customers&#8217; shelves because the core inventory on the customers&#8217; shelves is not consumed or realized in cash during the Company&#8217;s normal operating cycle, and is not used by management to assess the profitability of its business operations.</div>
    <div>&#160;</div>
    <div style="text-align: justify; color: rgb(0, 0, 0); text-indent: 27pt;"><font style="font-style: italic;">(Income) loss related to under return of cores</font>. The Remanufactured Core value is recorded as a net revenue based upon the estimate of
      Used Cores that will not be returned by the customer for credit. The Company excluded this (income) loss related to under return of cores to be consistent with the Company&#8217;s exclusion for the revaluation of cores on customers&#8217; shelves, as explained
      above.</div>
