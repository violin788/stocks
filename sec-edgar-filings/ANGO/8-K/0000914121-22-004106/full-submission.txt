    <div style="text-align: left; font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 12pt; font-weight: bold;">Item 8.01 &#8211;&#160;Other Events.</div>

    <div><br />

    <div style="text-align: justify; text-indent: 21.75pt; font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 12pt;">On October 6, 2022, AngioDynamics, Inc. (&#8220;AngioDynamics&#8221;) announced that a jury verdict has been rendered in its antitrust action
      against C.R. Bard, Inc. and Bard Access Systems, Inc. (collectively, &#8220;Bard&#8221;) (now part of Becton Dickinson &amp; Co.) in the United States District Court for the Northern District of New York.&#160; Following a two-week trial, the jury found Bard had not
      unlawfully restrained competition in the U.S. market for percutaneously inserted central catheters (&#8220;PICCs&#8221;), a medical device used for vascular access, in the United States.&#160; While AngioDynamics is disappointed with the results, the company remains
      committed to its core value of improving quality of life for patients.&#160; At this time, AngioDynamics is considering an appeal.</div>
