      <div style="text-align: left; font-family: 'Times New Roman'; font-size: 10pt; font-weight: bold;">Item 1.01 &#8211; Entry into a Material Definitive Agreement.</div>

      <div style="font-size: 10pt;"><br/>
      </div>

      <div style="text-align: justify; text-indent: 21.75pt; font-family: 'Times New Roman'; font-size: 10pt;">As previously disclosed, AngioDynamics, Inc. (&#8220;AngioDynamics&#8221; or the &#8220;Company&#8221;) and Becton, Dickinson and Company, C.R. Bard, Inc. and Bard
        Peripheral Vascular Inc. (collectively, &#8220;BD&#8221;) are involved in certain litigation in the United States District Court for the District of Delaware and the United States Court of Appeals for the Federal Circuit (collectively, the &#8220;Actions&#8221;).</div>

      <div style="font-size: 10pt;">&#160;<br/>
      </div>

      <div style="text-align: justify; text-indent: 21.75pt; font-family: 'Times New Roman'; font-size: 10pt;">On March 31, 2024, AngioDynamics and BD entered into a Settlement Agreement (the &#8220;Settlement Agreement&#8221;) to resolve the Actions. Under the terms
        of the Settlement Agreement, BD will grant a license to AngioDynamics under certain of BD&#8217;s port patents and AngioDynamics will grant BD a license under certain of AngioDynamics&#8217; catheter patents.&#160; AngioDynamics will make a one-time lump sum
        payment to BD in the amount of $7 million, $3 million of which will be payable within 5 business days, and the remainder of which will be payable in installments over the next 12 months.&#160; AngioDynamics will also make six minimum annual payments to
        BD of $2.5 million through February, 2029, and potential additional payments if six percent (6%) of annual net sales of AngioDynamics&#8217; port products exceed the minimum payment.&#160; The parties will participate in the pending appeal before the Federal
        Circuit of the case titled C.R. Bard, Inc. and Bard Peripheral Vascular, Inc. v. AngioDynamics, Inc.&#160; (C.A. 15-00218&#8211;JFB; and CAFC appeal No. 23-2056) and a contingent payment of $3 million will be due from AngioDynamics to BD if the Federal
        Circuit reverses or vacates the District Court&#8217;s findings of invalidity with respect to the patent claims at issue in the case. The agreement contains mutual covenants not to sue and releases.</div>

      <div style="text-align: justify; text-indent: 21.75pt; font-family: 'Times New Roman'; font-size: 10pt;"> <br/>
      </div>

      <div style="text-align: justify; text-indent: 21.75pt; font-family: 'Times New Roman'; font-size: 10pt;">The Settlement Agreement was entered into solely for the purpose of settlement and compromise of the Actions, and without any admission of
        misconduct, responsibility, or liability, which were all expressly denied by the Company and BD.</div>
