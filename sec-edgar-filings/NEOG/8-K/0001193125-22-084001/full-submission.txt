 <P STYLE="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman" ALIGN="right"><B>Exhibit 99.1 </B></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE </B></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>CONTACT: Steve Quinlan, Chief

<P STYLE="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;517.372.9200,
squinlan@neogen.com </B></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:16pt; font-family:Times New Roman" ALIGN="center"><B></B><B><I>Neogen Announces Third Quarter Results</I></B><B> </B></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman" ALIGN="center"><I>Solid year-over-year revenue growth showcases continued strength of products and markets </I></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman" ALIGN="center"><I>Closing of announced agreement to merge with 3M&#146;s Food Safety business on track and </I></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman" ALIGN="center"><I>company has made strong progress with integration plans </I></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">LANSING, Mich., March&nbsp;24, 2022 &#151; Neogen Corporation (NASDAQ: NEOG) announced today the results of the third quarter of its 2022 fiscal year, which
ended February 28. Revenues were $128,244,000, a 10% increase compared to the previous year&#146;s third quarter revenues of $116,709,000. This quarter marked the 119th of the past 125 quarters that Neogen has reported revenue increases compared to
the same period in the prior year. Current <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap"><FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">year-to-date</FONT></FONT> revenues were $387,066,000, up 14% compared to revenues of $341,034,000 for the same period a year ago. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">&#147;I am pleased to report solid top line growth for the quarter, with meaningful gains across our Food Safety, Animal Safety, and international
operations,&#148; said John Adent, Neogen&#146;s President and Chief Executive Officer. &#147;Our results for the third quarter demonstrate the resilience of our business, particularly in an inflationary environment and amid continued supply chain
disruptions. We are making significant progress with integration planning in anticipation of the closing of our transaction with 3M&#146;s Food Safety business, a deal that positions us at the forefront of this new era in food security with the
resources, capabilities, and solutions to be a global food security provider.&#148; </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:18pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>Financial and Business Highlights </B></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Net income for the third quarter was $5,443,000, or $0.05 per share, adversely impacted by $10.6&nbsp;million of professional fees incurred in connection with
Neogen&#146;s plan to combine 3M&#146;s Food Safety business with the company&#146;s existing operations, which was announced on December&nbsp;14, 2021. Excluding the 3M deal-related costs, net income for the quarter would have been $13,600,000, or
$0.13 per share, compared to $13,377,000, or $0.12 per share, in the previous year&#146;s third quarter. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Current <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap"><FONT
STYLE="white-space:nowrap">year-to-date</FONT></FONT> net income was $33,348,000, or $0.31 per share; excluding $19.9&nbsp;million in year to date 3M Food Safety deal costs, net income for the year to date would have been $48,807,000, or $0.45 per
share, compared to $45,122,000, or $0.42 per share, for the same period a year ago. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">For each comparative period above, earnings per share amounts have
been adjusted to reflect the company&#146;s <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap"><FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">2-for-1</FONT></FONT> stock split on June&nbsp;4, 2021. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Gross margins were 44.8% of sales in the third quarter of the fiscal year, compared to 46.1% recorded in the same period a year ago. Ongoing supply chain
issues and the current inflationary environment continued to adversely impact raw material costs and freight expenses, which rose $2.2&nbsp;million during the quarter. Additionally, a product mix shift toward Animal Safety products, which generally
have lower gross margins, negatively impacted gross margins for the period. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Excluding the 3M Food Safety related deal costs, operating expenses rose by
6% during the third quarter, primarily due to increased compensation and personnel-related costs, an uptick in travel and other customer facing activities, increased shipping costs and the impact of amortization expense from recent acquisitions.
</P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">&#147;We incurred significant legal and consulting fees during the quarter as we prepared for the 3M Food Safety combination; this spending will continue
through the end of this fiscal year and into next fiscal year as we work toward closing,&#148; said Steve Quinlan, Neogen&#146;s Chief Financial Officer. &#147;At the same time, our team is also continuing to drive growth initiatives across the
company while addressing the current cost pressures in our business.&#148; </P>

<p style="margin-top:1em; margin-bottom:0em; page-break-before:always">
<HR SIZE="3" style="COLOR:#999999" WIDTH="100%" ALIGN="CENTER">

<Center><DIV STYLE="width:8.5in" align="left">
 <P STYLE="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>Results by Segment </B></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><I>Food Safety </I></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Revenues for the company&#146;s Food Safety
segment were $62,756,000 in the third quarter, an increase of 7% compared to $58,424,000 in the prior year&#146;s third quarter. On an organic basis, the segment&#146;s revenue increased 4%. The company&#146;s recently launched products continued to
lead growth as they gained market share in the third quarter. Sales of the new AccuPoint<SUP STYLE="font-size:85%; vertical-align:top">&reg;</SUP> Advanced NG handheld sanitation monitoring system drove a 16% increase in sales of the general
sanitation product line. The Soleris<SUP STYLE="font-size:85%; vertical-align:top">&reg;</SUP> rapid general microbial testing product line recorded a 10% increase in equipment sales in the third quarter, and sales of the company&#146;s innovative
<I>Listeria </I>Right Now<SUP STYLE="font-size:85%; vertical-align:top">&#153;</SUP> <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">60-minute</FONT> test system recorded an increase of 13% over the prior year. Megazyme, the food quality and nutritional analysis
company that the company acquired in December 2020, continued to exceed expectations as it has been integrated into the company&#146;s Food Safety distribution channels. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Additional growth in this segment came from the company&#146;s diagnostics portfolio, with increases in allergen test kits (12%) and culture media (11%). This
growth was partially offset by a decline in dairy drug residue testing, as the company exits sales of <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">non-core</FONT> products within its portfolio. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:18pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><I>Animal Safety </I></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Revenues for the company&#146;s Animal
Safety segment were $65,488,000 in the third quarter of the fiscal year, an increase of 12%, compared to $58,285,000 in the previous year; organic growth was 9%. The growth was driven by strong sales of veterinary instruments, which recorded an
increase of 36% over the previous year&#146;s third quarter, on market share gains and private label business. Animal care products increased by 12%, with strong growth in vitamin injectables and antibiotics. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Biosecurity products sold through the Animal Safety segment increased by 2%, driven by an 11% increase in insect control products and a 2% increase in sales
of cleaners and disinfectants. This growth was partially offset by a 5% decline in sales of rodent control products, primarily due to difficult prior year comparisons. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Neogen&#146;s worldwide genomics business recorded an increase of 10% in the third quarter of the fiscal year, compared to the prior year&#146;s third
quarter, including new sales resulting from Neogen&#146;s acquisition of Genetic Veterinary Sciences, Inc. on December&nbsp;9, 2021. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:18pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>International
Highlights </B></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">International revenues rose by 10% for the quarter. Revenues at Neogen&#146;s U.K. operations increased by 16% (18% in British pounds),
driven by sales of cleaners and disinfectants to the U.K. and Asia. Revenues at Neogen&#146;s Brazilian operations decreased by 2% (flat in Brazilian real) over the third quarter last year, as increased sales of deoxynivalenol (DON) test kits and
Raptor<SUP STYLE="font-size:85%; vertical-align:top">&reg;</SUP> readers were offset by lower sales of dairy drug residue test kits. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">In China, revenues
decreased by 17% (19% in Chinese yuan), adversely affected by the country&#146;s &#147;Zero <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">COVID-19&#148;</FONT> strategy, which has led to large-scale commercial lockdowns and travel bans; additionally, poor
economic conditions for pork producers and difficult comparisons to a strong prior year resulted in lower sales of cleaners and disinfectants. Sales in the Latin American region increased by 1% (4% in Mexican pesos), with a 10% increase in sales of
Food Safety products, offset by lower sales of rodent control products to a large customer. Revenues at Neogen&#146;s Australasia operations increased by 20% (29% in Australian dollars), driven by the continued growth of genomic services in the beef
and sheep markets. </P>

 <P STYLE="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>About Neogen </B></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Neogen Corporation develops and markets comprehensive solutions dedicated to food and animal safety. The company&#146;s Food Safety segment markets dehydrated
culture media and diagnostic test kits to detect foodborne bacteria, natural toxins, food allergens, drug residues, plant diseases, and sanitation concerns. Neogen&#146;s Animal Safety segment is a leader in the development of genomic solutions
along with the manufacturing and distribution of a variety of animal healthcare products, including diagnostics, pharmaceuticals, veterinary instruments, wound care, and disinfectants, as well as rodent and insect control solutions. </P>
