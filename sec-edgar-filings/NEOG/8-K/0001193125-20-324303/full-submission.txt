</TABLE> <P STYLE="margin-top:24pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman" ALIGN="center"><B></B><B><I>Neogen reports second quarter results</I></B><B> </B></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">LANSING, Mich., Dec. 22, 2020 &#151; Neogen Corporation (NASDAQ: NEOG) announced today that revenues for the second quarter of its 2021 fiscal year, which
ended Nov. 30, were $115,000,000, a 7% increase compared to the previous year&#146;s second quarter revenues of $107,803,000. Current <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap"><FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">year-to-date</FONT></FONT> revenues were
$224,325,000, also an increase of 7%, compared to $209,227,000 for the same period a year ago. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Second quarter net income was $15,885,000, or $0.30 per
share, compared to the prior year&#146;s $16,276,000, or $0.31 per share. Current <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap"><FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">year-to-date</FONT></FONT> net income was $31,745,000, or $0.60 per share, compared to $30,928,000,
or $0.59 per share, for the same period a year ago. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">&#147;We are pleased to report growth across our business during the quarter, even as we continue to
fight global market disruptions caused by the resurgent <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">COVID-19</FONT> pandemic. We recorded a strong 13% sales increase in our animal safety segment, while also reporting significant sales increases in our new food
safety product offerings,&#148; said John Adent, Neogen&#146;s president and chief executive officer. &#147;While much uncertainty remains, we are seeing indications that we are on the road to recovery, with successful new product launches, and
strong sales increases across our diverse product portfolio in countries now less affected by COVID, including China and Australia.&#148; </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Neogen&#146;s
gross margin was 46.3% of sales in its second quarter of the current year, compared to 47.3% recorded in the same quarter of the prior fiscal year. The change in margin percentage was the result of a product mix shift towards Animal Safety products,
which have lower margins. Sales of food safety products continued to be adversely affected by the <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">COVID-19</FONT> global pandemic. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">During the quarter, the company incurred nearly $1&nbsp;million in expenses for strategic consulting, due diligence and other professional fees, the result of
acquisition activities that were ultimately not completed; these <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">non-recurring</FONT> expenses reduced earnings by approximately $0.02 per share. Operating income was $19,245,000, or 16.7% of sales, for the second
quarter, compared to $18,272,000, or 16.9%, in the same quarter a year ago. Interest income declined $716,000 from last year&#146;s second quarter, due primarily to the precipitous drop in interest rates on our marketable securities. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">&#147;We continue to proactively address issues associated with the ongoing difficult operating environment, including strengthening our global supply chain
and adequately staffing our worldwide operations,&#148; said Steve Quinlan, Neogen&#146;s chief financial officer. &#147;Although currency headwinds resulted in comparative revenues being reduced by approximately $1.2&nbsp;million during the
quarter, entirely in the Food Safety segment, this is an improvement over the first quarter as the U.S. dollar has recently weakened somewhat against a number of currencies in countries where we do business.&#148; </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Revenues for the company&#146;s Food Safety segment increased 1% during the second quarter compared to the prior year quarter, from $56,854,000 to
$57,534,000. The segment&#146;s performance benefitted from a 13% increase in sales of its Soleris<SUP STYLE="font-size:85%; vertical-align:top">&reg;</SUP> microbial testing system compared to the prior year quarter. In July 2020, Neogen launched
its Soleris Next Generation (NG) system that can rapidly detect a wide variety of microorganisms that threaten the safety and quality of food and other consumer products, and it has gained robust initial market acceptance. The current quarter also
featured a 6% increase in sales of the company&#146;s <I>Listeria</I> Right Now<SUP STYLE="font-size:85%; vertical-align:top">&#153;</SUP> test system, which detects and reports bacteria in environmental samples in under 60 minutes &#151; without
the need to enrich samples. The segment&#146;s sales performance was also aided by new sales from recently acquired businesses in Italy, Argentina, Uruguay, and Chile. These increases were offset by flat sales growth of its natural toxins and food
allergen product lines, and reductions in sales of foodborne pathogen (8%), general sanitation (8%) and culture media (3%) products, all caused in part by
<FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap"><FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">COVID-19-related</FONT></FONT> disruptions in many of the company&#146;s end markets. </P>
</DIV></Center>


<p style="margin-top:1em; margin-bottom:0em; page-break-before:always">
<HR SIZE="3" style="COLOR:#999999" WIDTH="100%" ALIGN="CENTER">


<Center><DIV STYLE="width:8.5in" align="left">
 <P STYLE="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Neogen&#146;s Animal Safety segment reported revenues of $57,466,000 for the second quarter of the 2021
fiscal year, compared to $50,949,000 in the prior year second quarter &#151; an increase of 13%. This increase was driven by continued new sales of cleaners and disinfectants used to fight the spread of
<FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">COVID-19;</FONT> a 24% increase in sales of rodenticides as the U.S. Pacific Northwest continues to fight rodent pressure; and large increases in sales of genomic testing services, insecticides (aided by the July
2020 acquisition of Elanco&#146;s StandGuard<SUP STYLE="font-size:85%; vertical-align:top">&reg;</SUP> product), animal care products, and needles and syringes &#151; when compared to the prior year&#146;s second quarter, as animal protein markets
gained strength. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">International highlights for the current quarter included a 59% increase in sales at Neogen China, including significant increases in
sales of disinfectants and genomic products as the nation recovers from its <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">COVID-19</FONT> and African swine fever outbreaks, and a 13% increase in Mexico and Central America driven by sales increases in cleaners
and disinfectants, rodenticides, detectable needles, and genomics. Neogen Australasia&#146;s quarterly revenue increased 81%, including strong sales gains for genomic and animal safety products, as well as new sales from the March 2020 acquisition
of a former distributor of Neogen&#146;s food safety products. Neogen&#146;s sales in the current quarter also increased 9% at our U.K. operations, led by strength in sales of cleaners and disinfectants, and 5% in India. Revenues declined 22% in
Brazil, as a large, <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">one-time</FONT> sale of insecticides in the prior year quarter did not recur, and as the result of adverse currency impact due to the 25% devaluation of the real against the dollar. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Revenues from Neogen&#146;s worldwide animal genomics business increased 12% in the second quarter of fiscal 2021 compared to the prior year, led by increases
in companion animal genomic testing due to a surge in <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap"><FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">COVID-19-related</FONT></FONT> pet adoptions and continued penetration into the veterinary markets, and testing through beef and
dairy breed associations. Internationally, Neogen&#146;s genomic sales increased in Australia (mainly bovine and canine), China (dairy cattle and porcine as the country recovers from an outbreak in African swine fever), and Latin America (dairy
cattle). Neogen recently launched Igenity<SUP STYLE="font-size:85%; vertical-align:top">&reg;</SUP> Canine Wellness, a preventative care DNA screening tool. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Neogen Corporation develops and markets products dedicated to food and animal safety. The company&#146;s Food Safety Division markets dehydrated culture media
and diagnostic test kits to detect foodborne bacteria, natural toxins, food allergens, drug residues, plant diseases and sanitation concerns. Neogen&#146;s Animal Safety Division is a leader in worldwide biosecurity products, animal genomics
testing, and the manufacturing and distribution of a variety of animal healthcare products, including diagnostics, pharmaceuticals and veterinary instruments. </P>
