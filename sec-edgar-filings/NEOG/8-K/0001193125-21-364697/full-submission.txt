 <P STYLE="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman" ALIGN="right"><B>Exhibit 99.1 </B></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE </B></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>CONTACT: Steve Quinlan, Chief

<P STYLE="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;517.372.9200,
squinlan@neogen.com </B></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman" ALIGN="center"><B><I>Neogen Announces Second Quarter Results </I></B></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman" ALIGN="center"><I>Food and Animal Safety segments record fourth consecutive quarter of double-digit organic sales growth </I></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman" ALIGN="center"><I>Results demonstrate continued momentum ahead of planned combination with 3M&#146;s Food Safety Division </I></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">LANSING, Mich., December&nbsp;21, 2021 &#151; Neogen Corporation (NASDAQ: NEOG) announced today the results of the second quarter of its 2022 fiscal year,
which ended November 30. Revenues were $130,517,000, a 13% increase compared to the previous year&#146;s second quarter revenues of $115,000,000. The company&#146;s Food Safety and Animal Safety segments each recorded double-digit increases in
organic sales in the second quarter, representing the fourth straight quarter of double-digit organic growth for each segment. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">This quarter marked the
118th of the past 124 quarters that Neogen has reported revenue increases compared to the same period in the prior year. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">&#147;Our second quarter and the
past few weeks, in particular, have been an exciting time for Neogen as we enhance our leadership position in the industry and usher in a new era in food security,&#148; said John Adent, Neogen&#146;s President and Chief Executive Officer. &#147;Not
only have we seen the business continue to rebound from the impacts of the <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">COVID-19</FONT> pandemic, but our team has also completed a number of transactions that will help to expand, strengthen and diversify our
product and geographic portfolio; just last week we announced an agreement to combine 3M&#146;s Food Safety business with our existing operations, a transformative transaction that will nearly double the size of our business and significantly
enhance our financial profile when it closes next year. I am also looking forward to seeing the results of our recent acquisitions as the fiscal year continues and we capitalize on tailwinds and global growth trends in sustainability, food safety
and supply chain integrity.&#148; </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:18pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>Financial and Business Highlights </B></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Net income for the second quarter was $10,828,000, or $0.10 per share, compared to $15,885,000, or $0.15 per share, in the previous year&#146;s second quarter.
Current <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap"><FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">year-to-date</FONT></FONT> net income was $27,905,000, or $0.26 per share, compared to $31,745,000, or $0.30 per share, for the same period a year ago. For each comparative
period, earnings per share amounts have been adjusted to reflect the company&#146;s <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap"><FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">2-for-1</FONT></FONT> stock split on June&nbsp;4 of this year. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Net income for both the second quarter and <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap"><FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">year-to-date</FONT></FONT> period was adversely
impacted by significant fees in connection with the recently announced plan to combine 3M&#146;s Food Safety business with Neogen&#146;s existing operations. The company incurred $9.3&nbsp;million in the second quarter for professional fees tied to
due diligence, consulting and legal services related to the 3M deal. These fees contributed to an overall increase of 42% in operating expenses compared to the prior year&#146;s second quarter. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Gross margins were 46.4% of sales in the second quarter of the fiscal year, compared to 46.3% recorded in the same period a year ago, as a favorable product
mix toward higher margin Food Safety products was offset by higher raw material and freight costs, due to ongoing supply chain issues. Excluding the deal costs previously mentioned, operating expenses rose by 14% during the second quarter, primarily
due to increased compensation costs, the result of additional headcount and improved performance, higher travel, customer facing activities, shipping and amortization expense from recent acquisitions. </P>
</DIV></Center>


<p style="margin-top:1em; margin-bottom:0em; page-break-before:always">
<HR SIZE="3" style="COLOR:#999999" WIDTH="100%" ALIGN="CENTER">

<Center><DIV STYLE="width:8.5in" align="left">
 <P STYLE="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">&#147;This was a strong quarter for Neogen despite operating costs being significantly impacted by expenses
related to our announced deal with 3M,&#148; said Steve Quinlan, Neogen&#146;s Chief Financial Officer. &#147;Excluding these deal costs, operating income for the quarter was $21.8&nbsp;million, a 13% increase over the prior year, while net income
would have been $18.1&nbsp;million, or $0.17 per share. Looking ahead, we will continue to drive operating performance while concurrently planning to ensure the successful integration of the 3M business with our existing operations. We will incur
ongoing legal, consulting and other professional fees during the remainder of the fiscal year as we navigate the <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap"><FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">sign-to-close</FONT></FONT> period.&#148; </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:18pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>Results by Segment </B></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><I>Food Safety </I></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Revenues for the company&#146;s Food Safety segment were $67,132,000 in the current quarter, an increase of 17% compared to $57,534,000 in the prior
year&#146;s second quarter. On an organic basis, the segment&#146;s revenue increased 11%, with the remaining contribution from the company&#146;s December 2020 acquisition of Megazyme, a food quality and nutritional analysis business. A number of
Neogen&#146;s recently launched products continued to gain market share during the second quarter, as the Soleris<SUP STYLE="font-size:85%; vertical-align:top">&reg;</SUP> rapid general microbial testing product line recorded a 22% increase, on
strong growth in equipment sales. Sales of the new AccuPoint<SUP STYLE="font-size:85%; vertical-align:top">&reg;</SUP> Advanced NG handheld sanitation monitoring system drove a reader sales increase of 19%, and the company&#146;s innovative
<I>Listeria</I> Right Now<SUP STYLE="font-size:85%; vertical-align:top">&#153;</SUP> <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">60-minute</FONT> test system recorded an increase of 35% over the prior year. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Additional growth in this segment was from the company&#146;s diagnostics portfolio, with increases across the culture media (11%), natural toxins (10%),
allergens (6%) and general sanitation (8%) product lines. Partially offsetting this growth was a 39% decline in sales of drug residue test kits, as competitive pressure in Europe negatively impacted the product line. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:18pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><I>Animal Safety </I></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Revenues for the company&#146;s Animal
Safety segment were $63,385,000 in the second quarter of the fiscal year, an increase of 10%, compared to $57,466,000 in the previous year; organic growth was also 10%. The growth was driven by a 30% increase in sales of veterinary instruments,
including needles and syringes, resulting in large part from recently won private label business. Animal care products increased by 18% as the equine and companion animal markets returned to <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">pre-pandemic</FONT>
demand levels and the ThyroKare<SUP STYLE="font-size:85%; vertical-align:top">&#153;</SUP> supplement line, which relaunched in February 2021, continued to regain market share. The company has also begun to integrate CAPInnoVet, a companion animal
health company specializing in parasiticides acquired in September 2021, into the business. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Biosecurity products sold through the Animal Safety segment
were driven by a 46% increase in insect control products, led by the StandGuard<SUP STYLE="font-size:85%; vertical-align:top">&reg;</SUP> product line, acquired in July 2020, as it continued to gain market share. This growth was offset by a 14%
decline in rodent control products, which were negatively impacted by supply constraints and a <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">non-outbreak</FONT> year. Cleaners and disinfectants also declined 2%, as the prior year&#146;s second-quarter results
included a large <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">non-recurring</FONT> order. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Neogen&#146;s worldwide genomics business recorded an increase of 8% in the
second quarter of the fiscal year, compared to the prior year&#146;s second quarter. The company saw growth across beef and dairy cattle, sheep and poultry markets, partially offset by lower sales of services into the companion animal market, the
result of difficult comparisons due to strong growth in the prior year&#146;s second quarter. </P>

 <P STYLE="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>International Highlights </B></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">International revenues rose by 20% for the quarter; organic growth was 14%. Revenues at Neogen&#146;s U.K. operations increased by 16% (11% in British pounds),
driven by sales of the company&#146;s One Broth, One Plate culture media solution to commercial labs in the U.K. and increased sales of cleaners and disinfectants to Asia. In China, revenues increased by 28% (22% in Chinese yuan), driven by new
sales of Megazyme products into that market and continued strength in genomic services. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Revenues at Neogen&#146;s Brazilian operations decreased by 5%
(6% in Brazilian real) over the second quarter last year, as drought conditions significantly reduced the corn crop size at harvest and cleaner crops reduced demand for aflatoxin test kits. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Sales in the Latin American region rose 13% (10% in Mexican pesos) with new business in natural toxin test kits, environmental sanitation, culture media and
biosecurity products. Revenues at Neogen&#146;s Australasia operations increased by 29% (27% in Australian dollars), driven by growth of genomic services in the beef and sheep markets and increases in sales of diagnostic products. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:18pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>About Neogen </B></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Neogen Corporation develops and markets
comprehensive solutions dedicated to food and animal safety. The company&#146;s Food Safety segment markets dehydrated culture media and diagnostic test kits to detect foodborne bacteria, natural toxins, food allergens, drug residues, plant
diseases, and sanitation concerns. Neogen&#146;s Animal Safety segment is a leader in the development of genomic solutions along with the manufacturing and distribution of a variety of animal healthcare products, including diagnostics,
pharmaceuticals, veterinary instruments, wound care, and disinfectants, as well as rodent and insect control solutions. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:18pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><I>Certain portions of this news
