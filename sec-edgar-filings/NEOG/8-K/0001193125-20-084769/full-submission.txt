<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">LANSING, Mich., March&nbsp;24, 2020 &#151; Neogen Corporation (NASDAQ: NEOG) announced today that its revenues for the third quarter of its 2020 fiscal year,
which ended Feb. 29, were $99,869,000, up 2% from the previous year&#146;s third quarter revenues of $97,700,000. The third quarter was the 112th of the past 117 quarters that Neogen reported revenue increases as compared with the previous year
&#151; including all consecutive quarters in the past 14 years. Current <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap"><FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">year-to-date</FONT></FONT> revenues were $309,096,000, also up 2% compared to $304,424,000 for the same period
a year ago. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Third quarter net income was $12,200,000, compared to the prior year&#146;s $13,073,000. Earnings per share in the current quarter were
$0.23, compared to $0.25 a year ago. Current <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap"><FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">year-to-date</FONT></FONT> net income was $43,128,000, or $0.82 per share, compared to $44,361,000, or $0.85 per share, for the same
period a year ago. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">&#147;Given the dramatic world events of the last few months, I am extremely proud of our Neogen employees who are working tirelessly
to supply the worldwide demand for our products. Our mission matters more today than ever; we are strategically important to the world&#146;s ability to contain and recover from the global threat posed by
<FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">COVID-19,</FONT> and ensuring the safety, quality and quantity of the global food supply,&#148; said John Adent, Neogen&#146;s president and chief executive officer. &#147;While operating results overall were
disappointing for the quarter, we were encouraged by continued strong performance in our genomics services and growth in a number of our food safety product lines. Additionally, in the third quarter we made four strategic acquisitions to enhance our
global footprint. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">&#147;The recent acquisitions of our distributors in Argentina and Uruguay demonstrate our strong belief that the Southern Cone of
South America, with its large beef and dairy populations, holds significant growth potential for us,&#148; continued Adent. &#147;These acquisitions, along with the other recent purchases of our food safety distributors in Australia and Italy,
position us to promote, market and sell Neogen&#146;s comprehensive suite of food safety, animal safety and genomics products around the globe.&#148;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">&#147;The strength of our balance sheet, with no debt, substantial cash reserves and solid cash generation, leaves us well prepared to weather the current

associated with the current environment, including strengthening our global supply chain to secure availability of the raw materials we need for production. We are also focused on maintaining adequate staffing for our worldwide manufacturing
