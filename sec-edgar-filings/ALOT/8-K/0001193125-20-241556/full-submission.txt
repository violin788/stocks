<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>West Warwick, R.I. &#150; September</B><B></B><B>&nbsp;9, 2020</B> &#150; AstroNova, Inc. (NASDAQ: ALOT), a global leader in data visualization
technologies, today announced financial results for the fiscal 2021 second quarter ended August&nbsp;1, 2020. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:18pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B><U>CEO Commentary </U></B></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">&#147;After a slow start to the quarter, we were pleased to see that our largest segment, Product Identification, representing nearly 80% of AstroNova revenue,
made good progress adapting to the new normal by accelerating the transition to digital business practices from our traditional <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">in-person</FONT> sales and marketing approach,&#148; said Greg Woods, AstroNova&#146;s
President and Chief Executive Officer. &#147;This switch, combined with customer enthusiasm for our recently refreshed printer offerings, resulted in a solid <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">pick-up</FONT> in printer sales and supplies in the latter
part of the quarter. Based upon our unique full-solution offerings and our well-established strong position in resilient market segments such as food&nbsp;&amp; beverage, cleaning supplies and medical, we expect this growth trend to continue as we
move through the second half of the year. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">&#147;In our Test&nbsp;&amp; Measurement segment, the combination of the continued 737 MAX grounding and the <FONT
STYLE="white-space:nowrap">COVID-19</FONT> pandemic further depressed revenue in the quarter,&#148; Woods said. &#147;We adjusted by taking significant additional actions to reduce expenses to better align this business with the current macro
environment. These initiatives better position us to emerge stronger with increased profitability as the industry rebounds. </P>

 <P STYLE="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">&#147;While a full rebound in the aerospace industry is likely to take many quarters, we are starting to see
some positive signs. During the second quarter our repair business started to rebound as the number of global flights increased. We believe that this momentum will continue based on the gradual but steady increase in the number of aircraft being
flown. Additionally, many of the final steps required for the Boeing 737 MAX&#146;s return to service authorization have now been completed,&#148; continued Woods. &#147;Meanwhile, we continue to make progress on further increasing our direct to
airline market penetration, especially in the narrow-body segment. For example, we recently announced an exclusive multi-year commitment from a major North American carrier to standardize on our ToughWriter<SUP
STYLE="font-size:85%; vertical-align:top">&reg;</SUP> narrow-format flight deck printers for its Boeing 737 MAX aircraft. These market share gains enhance our ability to grow at a faster rate as the industry recovers. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">&#147;Despite the impacts of the <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">COVID-19</FONT> pandemic, our team around the globe is doing well and has continued to
demonstrate an unwavering commitment to providing our customers with the highest-quality products and services,&#148; Woods said. &#147;Our team&#146;s health and safety is of utmost importance and we deeply appreciate their dedication and adherence
to our rigorous protection protocols, which have kept them healthy and all of our worldwide facilities fully operational. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:18pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B><U>Business Outlook
</U></B></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">&#147;We have taken &#150; and will continue to take &#151; aggressive cost-reduction actions to enable more rapid profit improvement as the
market recovers. We have also strengthened our liquidity position to drive the growth of our Product Identification segment and weather the downturn in the aerospace industry. Based on the strength of Product Identification&#146;s large and
diversified global installed base, and the renewed new printer sales growth driven by our digital transformation, we expect growth in the Product Identification segment to increase beginning in the second half of the fiscal year. Additionally, we
are encouraged by recent demand trends and expect to see total revenues grow sequentially in the third and fourth quarters of this fiscal year,&#148; concluded Woods. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:18pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B><U>Liquidity Position </U></B></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Early in May, AstroNova
received a $4.4&nbsp;million Payroll Protection Program loan under the CARES Act. Additionally, on July&nbsp;30, 2020, the Company entered into an amended and restated credit facility with its current lender, providing increased available credit,
reduced amortization requirements and financial covenants aligned with the current market realities. The Company believes that these two credit facilities have remedied the short-term overhang that the combined 737 MAX and COVID challenges placed on
its prior credit structure. AstroNova also believes that these credit facilities, together with its cost-reductions; tactical business segment actions; the April 2020 decision by the Board of Directors to suspend the Company&#146;s quarterly cash
dividend; as well as improved quarterly cash from operations, position AstroNova to </P>

$9.4&nbsp;million<I>.</I> </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:18pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B><U>Q2 FY 2021 Operating Segment Results </U></B></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>Product Identification</B> segment revenue in the second quarter of fiscal 2021 was $21.6&nbsp;million, compared with $22.1&nbsp;million in the prior-year
period. Segment operating income was $3.1&nbsp;million, or 14.5% of revenue, compared with $2.2&nbsp;million, or 10.0% of revenue, in the prior year, primarily reflecting a reduction in both manufacturing and operating costs. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>Test</B><B></B><B>&nbsp;&amp; Measurement </B>segment revenue in the second quarter of fiscal 2021 was $6.0&nbsp;million, compared with $11.3&nbsp;million
in the same period of fiscal 2020, due to the continued grounding of the Boeing 737 MAX and rapid demand falloff in the aerospace industry related to <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">COVID-19.</FONT> The Test&nbsp;&amp; Measurement segment recorded
an operating loss of $0.4&nbsp;million, or negative 6.8% of revenue, compared with segment operating income of $1.6&nbsp;million, or 13.7% of revenue, in the comparable period of fiscal 2020, a direct result of declines in aerospace printer sales
and adverse mix, despite lower manufacturing and operating costs. <B></B> </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:18pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B><U>Q2 FY 2021 Results Summary </U></B></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Revenue totaled $27.7&nbsp;million, compared with $33.5&nbsp;million in the year-earlier period. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Gross profit was $9.8&nbsp;million, or 35.4% of revenue, compared with $12.0&nbsp;million, or 35.8% of revenue, in the same period of fiscal 2020. The
decrease reflected lower revenue and less favorable product mix in the 2021 period, primarily in the Test&nbsp;&amp; Measurement segment. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Operating
expenses totaled $9.6&nbsp;million, down approximately 11.4% compared with $10.8&nbsp;million in the second quarter fiscal 2020, reflecting the Company&#146;s cost-reduction actions. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Other income included foreign exchange gains from the impact of a strengthening Euro dollar and Danish Kroner on exposures in those currencies. Taxes were
unfavorably impacted by discrete expenses related to foreign jurisdictions and a windfall shortfall on employee stock vestings, compounded by the effect of accounting for improvements in projected earnings for the year from the first to second
quarters. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Net income was $3,000, or $0.00 per diluted share, compared with net income of $951,000, or $0.13 per diluted share, for the second quarter of
fiscal 2020. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Earnings before interest, taxes, depreciation and amortization (EBITDA) were $2.3&nbsp;million, compared with $2.7&nbsp;million in the

<P STYLE="margin-top:18pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>About AstroNova </B></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">AstroNova, Inc. (NASDAQ: ALOT), a global
leader in data visualization technologies since 1969, designs, manufactures, distributes, and services a broad range of products that acquire, store, analyze, and present data in multiple formats. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">The Product Identification segment offers a complete <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">line-up</FONT> of labeling hardware and supplies allowing customers to
mark, track, and enhance the appearance of their products. The segment is comprised of three business units: QuickLabel<SUP STYLE="font-size:85%; vertical-align:top">&reg;</SUP>, the industry leader in tabletop digital color label printing;
TrojanLabel<SUP STYLE="font-size:85%; vertical-align:top">&reg;</SUP>, an innovative leader for professional label presses; and GetLabels<SUP STYLE="font-size:85%; vertical-align:top">&#153;</SUP>, the premier supplier of label materials, inks,
toners, ribbons, and adhesives, all compatible with the major printer brands. Supported by AstroNova&#146;s customer application experts and technology leadership in printing, material science, and high-speed data processing, customers benefit from
an optimized, &#147;total solution&#148; approach. </P>
