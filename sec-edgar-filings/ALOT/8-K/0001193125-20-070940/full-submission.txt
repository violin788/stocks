 <P STYLE="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">AstroNova Reports Q4 FY 2020 Financial Results/2 </P>
<p STYLE="margin-top:0pt;margin-bottom:0pt ; font-size:8pt">&nbsp;</P>
 <P STYLE="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B><U>CEO Commentary </U></B></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">&#147;The impact of the grounding of the 737 MAX and the unexpected production halt announcement in December on our Test and Measurement segment, combined with
ongoing relative weakness for the Product Identification segment in Asia, continued to affect our revenue and margins in the fourth quarter,&#148; said President and CEO Greg Woods. &#147;However, demand growth in regions outside of Asia drove
positive fourth-quarter bookings in our Product Identification segment. We saw an enthusiastic response to our new products at several trade shows in the fall, and we are seeing indications of that customer interest translating into bookings
momentum.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">&#147;As a result of the near-term industry dynamics, most notably the Boeing 737 MAX production halt, we have taken
actions to bring our costs in line with the current market environment,&#148; continued Woods. &#147;We implemented a 5% headcount reduction and other initiatives that we expect to result in an annualized expense savings of $1.5&nbsp;million to
$2.0&nbsp;million. At the same time however, we continue to invest in those strategic initiatives that we believe will drive growth and profitability for AstroNova.&#148; </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:18pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B><U>Business Outlook </U></B></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">&#147;In addition to the
favorable customer reception to our new Product Identification offerings and the resulting solid bookings in the fourth quarter, we have made some additional changes to our sales management structure that we expect will result in more efficient and
effective global sales processes,&#148; added Woods. &#147;In the meantime, we believe our cost-cutting measures position us for a return to profitability as we move forward in fiscal 2021. Longer term, we are bullish about the opportunities made
possible by secular trends in both segments of our business, but the additional delays in the 737 MAX return to service will negatively impact nearer term results. We also are closely monitoring the <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">COVID-19</FONT>
viral outbreak for potential impacts on customer demand and our supply chain. Locally, we have taken actions to further enhance the health and safety of our people as part of the state of emergency that has been declared in Rhode Island.&#148; </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:18pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B><U>Operating Segment Results </U></B></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>Product
Identification</B> segment revenue in the fourth quarter of fiscal 2020 was $20.6&nbsp;million, compared with $23.4&nbsp;million in the prior-year period. Segment operating profit was $0.5&nbsp;million, or 2.5% of revenue, versus segment operating
profit of $2.1&nbsp;million, or 8.9% of revenue, in the prior year. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>Test</B><B></B><B>&nbsp;&amp; Measurement</B> segment revenue in the fourth
quarter of fiscal 2020 was $9.8&nbsp;million compared with $13.8&nbsp;million in the same period of fiscal 2019. Segment operating profit was $0.7&nbsp;million, or 7.6% of revenue, for the fourth quarter of fiscal 2020 compared with segment
operating profit of $3.7&nbsp;million, or 26.7% of revenue, in the comparable period of fiscal 2019. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Hardware revenue was $11.4&nbsp;million, compared
with $15.2&nbsp;million in the prior-year period. Supplies revenue was $16.4&nbsp;million compared with $18.5&nbsp;million in the same period of fiscal 2019. Service/other revenue was $2.7&nbsp;million compared with $3.5&nbsp;million in the same
period a year earlier. </P>

 <P STYLE="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">AstroNova Reports Q4 FY 2020 Financial Results/3 </P>
<p STYLE="margin-top:0pt;margin-bottom:0pt ; font-size:8pt">&nbsp;</P>
 <P STYLE="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B><U>Financial Summary </U></B></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Revenue totaled $30.5&nbsp;million in the fourth quarter of fiscal 2020, versus $37.2&nbsp;million in the same period of fiscal 2019, reflecting lower sales in
the Test&nbsp;&amp; Measurement and Product Identification segments. Full-year fiscal 2020 revenue was $133.4&nbsp;million compared with $136.7&nbsp;million in fiscal 2019. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Gross profit was $10.2&nbsp;million in the fourth quarter of fiscal 2020, or 33.6% of revenue, compared with $14.6&nbsp;million, or 39.2% of revenue, for the
fourth quarter of fiscal 2019. The decrease in gross profit dollars and margin was attributable primarily to lower revenue and less favorable sales mix. Full-year gross profit was $48.8&nbsp;million for fiscal 2020 compared with $54.0&nbsp;million
in the prior year. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Operating expenses were $11.9&nbsp;million in the fourth quarter of fiscal 2020 compared with $11.7&nbsp;million in the same period of
fiscal 2019. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Operating loss was $1.6&nbsp;million compared with operating income of $2.9&nbsp;million, or 7.9% of revenue, in the fourth quarter of
fiscal 2019. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">For full-year fiscal 2020, operating expenses were $46.3&nbsp;million compared with $45.3&nbsp;million in fiscal 2019. Operating income for
the full fiscal year 2020 was $2.4&nbsp;million compared with $8.7&nbsp;million a year ago. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Net loss was $1.3&nbsp;million, or $0.19 per share, in the
fourth quarter of fiscal 2020 versus net income of $2.3&nbsp;million, or $0.32 per diluted share, in the fourth quarter of fiscal 2019. For full-year fiscal 2020, net income was $1.8&nbsp;million, or $0.24 per diluted share, compared with net income
of $5.7&nbsp;million, or $0.81 per diluted share, in fiscal 2019. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Bookings were $36.0&nbsp;million in the fourth quarter of fiscal 2020 compared with
$38.6&nbsp;million in the fourth quarter of fiscal 2019. Fiscal 2020 bookings decreased 4.0% to $136.3&nbsp;million from $141.9&nbsp;million in fiscal 2019. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Backlog at January&nbsp;31, 2020 was $25.2&nbsp;million, as compared to $25.6&nbsp;million at the end of the fiscal 2019 fourth quarter. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:18pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B><U>Board Declares Quarterly Dividend </U></B></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">On

shareholders of record on March&nbsp;26, 2020. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:18pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B><U>Fourth-Quarter Fiscal 2020 Conference Call </U></B></P>
