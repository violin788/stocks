<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;First Quarter Fiscal 2018 Achievements </B></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&#150; &nbsp;&nbsp;Revenue of $24.5 million, up 1.4 percent over the prior year quarter </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&#150; &nbsp;&nbsp;Diluted EPS of $0.07, compared with $0.14 for Q1 fiscal 2017 </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>West Warwick, R.I., May&nbsp;16, 2017</B> &#150; AstroNova, Inc. (NASDAQ: ALOT), a global leader in data visualization technologies, today reported revenue
of $24.5 million for the fiscal first quarter ended April&nbsp;29, 2017, an increase of 1.4 percent from the same period in the prior year. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">&#147;During
the quarter, we completed the TrojanLabel acquisition and executed on our rapid integration plan while continuing to implement our international expansion strategy,&#148; said AstroNova President and Chief Executive Officer Greg Woods.
&#147;International revenue grew 19 percent in the quarter, resulting from the addition of TrojanLabel and continued strong demand in key markets. In recent months, we signed new dealers in China and Latin America and opened a sales office in India.
</P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">&#147;Product Identification revenue increased 12.3 percent in the quarter, reflecting the additional TrojanLabel revenue and the <FONT
STYLE="white-space:nowrap">fourth-quarter</FONT> fiscal 2017 launch of new products such as the QL-800,&#148; Woods continued. &#147;Revenue in our Test&nbsp;&amp; Measurement segment was $5.8 million in the first quarter of 2018, compared with $7.5
