<p style="font-size:10.0pt;margin:0in 0in .0001pt 1.0in;text-indent:-1.0in;"><b><u><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;font-weight:bold;">Item 5.02</font></u></b><font size="1" style="font-size:3.0pt;">&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160; </font><b><u style="font-weight:bold;">Departure of Directors or Principal Officers; Election of Directors; Appointment of Principal Officers</u>.</b></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-indent:.5in;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">On September&nbsp;5, 2019, the board of directors (the &#147;Board&#148;) of Rockwell Medical,&nbsp;Inc. (the &#147;Company&#148;) appointed John P. McLaughlin to the Board as a Class&nbsp;I Director to serve until the Company&#146;s 2022 Annual Meeting of Stockholders and until his successor is duly elected and qualified, effective immediately. Mr.&nbsp;McLaughlin was also appointed as a member of the Audit Committee and Compensation Committee of the Board.</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-indent:.5in;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Mr.&nbsp;McLaughlin, age 67, was Chief Executive Officer of PDL BioPharma,&nbsp;Inc. (&#147;PDL&#148;), a publicly-traded pharmaceutical holding company, from December&nbsp;2008 until December&nbsp;2019. From November&nbsp;2008 to December&nbsp;2008 he served as a Senior Advisor to PDL. He was the Chief Executive Officer and a member of the board of directors of Anesiva,&nbsp;Inc., formerly known as Corgentech,&nbsp;Inc., a publicly-traded biopharmaceutical company, from January&nbsp;2000 to June&nbsp;2008. From December&nbsp;1997 to September&nbsp;1999, Mr.&nbsp;McLaughlin was President of Tularik Inc., a biopharmaceutical company. From September&nbsp;1987 to December&nbsp;1997, Mr.&nbsp;McLaughlin held a number of senior management positions at Genentech,&nbsp;Inc., a biopharmaceutical company, including Executive Vice President. From January&nbsp;1985 to September&nbsp;1987, Mr.&nbsp;McLaughlin was a partner at a Washington, D.C. law firm specializing in food and drug law. Prior to that, Mr.&nbsp;McLaughlin served as counsel to various subcommittees of the United States House of Representatives, where he drafted numerous measures that became Food and Drug Administration laws. Mr.&nbsp;McLaughlin has been&#160; a member of the board of directors of PDL since 2008. He also serves as a member of the boards of directors of Lensar, a private medtech company that develops and commercializes femtosec lasers for the treatment of cataracts, and Noden, a private company that commercializes two hypertension drugs worldwide. Both are largely owned by PDL. Mr.&nbsp;McLaughlin co-founded and served as Chairman of the board of directors of Eyetech Pharmaceuticals,&nbsp;Inc., a publicly-traded biopharmaceutical company subsequently bought by OSI Pharmaceuticals,&nbsp;Inc., co-founded and served as a member of the board of directors of Peak Surgical,&nbsp;Inc., a private medical device company, until it was acquired by Medtronic in 2011, and served as a member of the boards of directors of AxoGen,&nbsp;Inc., a publicly-traded biopharmaceutical company until 2014, Adverum Biotechnologies,&nbsp;Inc., a publicly-traded biopharmaceutical company, until 2016 and Seattle Genetics,&nbsp;Inc., a publicly-traded biopharmaceutical company, until 2016. He received a B.A. from the University of Notre Dame and a J.D. from Catholic University of America.</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-indent:.5in;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">In accordance with the Company&#146;s non-employee director compensation policy, which is described in the Company&#146;s Proxy Statement on Schedule 14A (No. 000-23661), Mr. McLaughlin will receive an annual cash retainer of $60,000 for his service as a director, which will be pro-rated through the Company&#146;s 2020 annual meeting of stockholders. In addition, Mr. McLaughlin was granted an option to purchase 28,372 shares of the Company&#146;s common stock at an exercise price equal to the closing price of the Company&#146;s common stock on The Nasdaq Global Market on September 5, 2019 and 22,559 restricted stock units for his service as a director, as well as for his service as a member of the Audit Committee and Compensation Committee.&#160; The equity awards were made under the Company&#146;s 2018 Long Term Incentive Plan. The restricted stock units and the shares underlying the option will vest and become exercisable on June 6, 2020, subject to Mr. McLaughlin&#146;s continued service to the Company.
