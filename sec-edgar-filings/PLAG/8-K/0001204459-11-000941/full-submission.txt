<P align=center><B></B><B><U>AMERICAN LORAIN CORPORATION REPORTS 2010 FOURTH
QUARTER <BR>AND YEAR</U></B><B><U>-</U></B><B><U>END FINANCIAL
RESULTS</U></B><B> </B></P>
<P align=center><B>Company to Hold Conference Call Friday, April 1, 2011, at
9:00 a.m. ET </B><B>-</B><B> <BR>Accompanying Slide Presentation at
www.americanlorain.com</B></P>
<P align=justify>JUNAN COUNTY, China, March 31, 2011 -- <B>American Lorain
Corporation (NYSE Amex: ALN) </B>("American Lorain" or the "Company"), an
international processed snack foods, convenience foods, and frozen foods company
based in the Shandong Province, China, today announced financial results for its
fourth quarter and year ended December 31, 2010. </P>
<P align=justify><B>Q4 2010 Financial Highlights</B></P>
<UL>
  <LI>Total revenues of $82.3 million, an increase of 30.8% year over year
  <LI>Gross margin improved to 22.7% from 20.9% year-over-year
  <LI>Net income attributable to common stockholders of $8.2 million, up 30.4%
  year-over-year
  <LI>Diluted earnings per share of $0.24 compared to $0.23 in the prior year
  period </LI></UL>
<P align=justify><B>Full</B><B>-</B><B>year 2010 Financial Highlights</B></P>
<UL>
  <LI>Total revenues of $184.2 million, an increase of 25.5% from the prior year

  <LI>Gross margin improved to 22.7% from 22.3% in the prior year
  <LI>Net income attributable to common stockholders was $17.8 million, an
  increase of 23.8% year-over-year
  <LI>Diluted earnings per share of $0.55 compared to $0.55 in the prior year
  <LI>Generated $24.4 million in cash from operations
  <LI>Company achieved 2010 guidance of $182.0 to $190.0 million in revenues and
  $17.8 million to $19.0 million in net income
  <LI>Book value per share of $3.76 </LI></UL>
<P align=justify>American Lorain&#146;s Chairman and CEO, Mr. Si Chen, stated, &#147;We
are very pleased with our growth during the quarter and year ended December 31,
2010. We have continued to benefit from our existing long-term supplier and
customer relationships, and are gaining significant traction in consumer
acceptance of our convenience food products. Despite the various uncertainties
weighing on the global economic environment, we have remained growing and
profitable. American Lorain maintains a growing and diverse network of customers
throughout 26 provinces in China and 42 countries around the world. We continued
to expand our brand name throughout China, and improved sales domestically by
30.5% during 2010. In 2010, 73.3% of the Company's sales were generated
domestically through our network of distribution channels and relations. We also
improved our sales internationally by 13.6% primarily supported by growth in the
Asia-pacific region. Our financial position is strong with approximately $57.4
million in working capital and a continued record of generating free cash flow
while still regularly investing in our business.&#148;</P>

<P align=justify>American Lorain&#146;s sales of chestnuts increased by 13.5% to
$101.2 million, and represented approximately 54.9% of the Company&#146;s total
revenues, as compared with 60.7% over the same period of last year. This growth
was largely due to increased marketing efforts of the Company&#146;s 50+ varieties of
chestnut products such as sweetheart chestnuts and chestnut in syrup, as well as
the increase in chestnut retail prices. Sales of chestnuts were particularly
strong during the fourth quarter, which has historically been the Company&#146;s
strongest quarter due to the seasonality of chestnuts. </P>
<P align=justify>Convenience foods remained American Lorain&#146;s fastest growing
business segment, improving to $56.8 million (up 63.9%) for the year. Products
in American Lorain's convenience foods product line include &#145;ready to cook&#146;
foods such as French fries, 'ready to eat' foods such as snack beans and pickle
vegetables, and 'meals ready to eat' which are microwavable lunch box entrees or
packaged foods with self-heating devices for military personnel. For 2010, this
segment represented 30.8% of the Company&#146;s total sales as compared with 23.6%
over the same period of last year, and the Company expects this segment to
continue to grow the fastest of its three business lines.</P>
<P align=justify>American Lorain's frozen food segment contributed an increase
of 14% to $26.3 million in 2010 from $23.0 million in 2009, representing 14.3%
of the total revenue as compared with 15.7% over the same period of last year.
Sales at the Company&#146;s frozen food segment were improved by several large orders
placed in the fourth quarter. </P>

<P align=justify><B>Balance Sheet Highlights and Financial Position </B></P>
<P align=justify>In December 2010, the Company completed a sale of 3,440,800
shares of its Common Stock to Tongley Investment Ltd. and several accredited
investors in a private placement transaction for proceeds of approximately $9.6
million, before payment of transaction fees and expenses. The Company's use of
this capital was allocated to its chestnut business and primarily used in raw
chestnut procurement during the current chestnut season.</P>
<P align=justify>At December 31, 2010, American Lorain&#146;s cash and cash
equivalents (excluding $2.3 million in restricted cash) were $12.7 million,
working capital was $57.4 million, total debt was $34.7 million, and
stockholders&#146; equity was $129.3 million, compared to $12.1 million, $49.9
million, $35.9 million, and $94.7 million, respectively, at December 31,
2009.</P>
<P align=justify>In January 2010, the Company also announced that it received
the second tranche of a loan from the German bank, DEG, in in the amount of $10
million. The total amount of the loan with DEG is $15 million, and the first
tranche of $5 million was disbursed on December 13, 2010. The loan has a term of
5 years and commencing from the full disbursement, the interest rate has been
fixed at 5.51% per annum. </P>

31, 2010 of $3.76.<B> </B></P>
