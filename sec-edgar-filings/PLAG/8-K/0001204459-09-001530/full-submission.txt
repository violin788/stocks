<P align="center">
<B><font size="5">American Lorain Announces Second Quarter of Fiscal Year 2009
Results</font></B></P>
<P align="justify">
<B><font size="2">JUNAN COUNTY, Shandong, China, August 14, 2009 --</font></B><font size="2"> <U><FONT color="#0000ff">American Lorain</FONT></U> Corporation (OTC Bulletin Board: ALRC &#150; News) ("American Lorain" or the "Company"), an international processed foods company
based in Shandong Province, People's Republic of China ("PRC"), announced its financial results for the second quarter of fiscal year 2009 ended June 30, 2009.
</font> </P>
<P align="justify">
<B><font size="2">Second Quarter 2009 Highlights</font></B></P>
<UL style="text-align:justify;">
<LI>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 5"><font size="2">Net revenue increased 11.3% year-over-year to &#36;24.0 million</font></LI>
<LI>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 5"><font size="2">Gross profit increased 2.0% year-over-year to &#36; 5.3 million</font></LI>
<LI>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 5"><font size="2">Gross margin was 22.1%, compared to 24.1% for the second quarter of 2008</font></LI>
<LI>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 5"><font size="2">Net income decreased 12.4% year-over-year to &#36;1.9 million</font></LI>
<LI>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 5"><font size="2">Diluted earnings per share were &#36; 0.074, compared to &#36;0.084 for the second quarter of 2008</font></LI>
<LI>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 5"><font size="2">Domestic sales totaled &#36;20.8 million and accounted for 87.0% of revenue</font></LI>
<LI>
<p style="margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 5"><font size="2">Increased number of sales agents to 300 that covered 26 provinces in China</font></LI>
</UL>
<P align="justify">
<font size="2">&#147;Although the second quarter is usually a slower quarter for American Lorain as a result of seasonality, the Company still demonstrated double-digit top line growth in the three months ended June 30, 2009,&#148; said Mr. Si Chen, Chairman and
Chief Executive Officer of American Lorain. &#147;2009 is a transitional year for American Lorain. We are currently in the process of adjusting our business model by increasing convenience food sales, building our brand equity through an extensive
promotional campaign, and transitioning our domestic sales to third party channels such as sales agents. Furthermore, we have entered a partnership with Well Luck Company, Inc. in the United States in order to increase our sales to what is for us a
virtually untapped export market. We are confident in American Lorain&#146;s expansion strategy and growth in the second half of the year and will continue to work diligently to deliver quality products to our customers around the world.&#148;</font></P>
<P align="justify">
<B><font size="2">Second Quarter 2009 Results</font></B><font size="2"><BR>
</font>
</P>
<P align="justify">
<font size="2">Net revenue for the second quarter of 2009 increased to &#36;24.0 million, an increase of 11.3% from &#36;21.5 million for the second quarter of 2008. Sales of chestnut products increased 9.6% from the second quarter of 2008 and accounted for 57.8%
of total revenue, compared to 58.8% for the second quarter of 2008. Sales of frozen, canned and bulk food increased 19.3% from the second quarter of
2008 and accounted for 15.8% of total net revenue, compared to 14.8% for the same period of 2008. Sales of convenience food products increased 10.8% and accounted for 26.3% of revenue, compared to 26.5% for the corresponding quarter last year.</font></P>

<A name="page_2"><font size="2"></font></A>

<P align="justify">
<font size="2">American Lorain's revenue growth for the second quarter of 2009 was mainly the result of strong domestic sales, which increased 16.7% year-over year. In comparison, exports decreased 12.3% to &#36;3.2 million for the second quarter of 2009 from
&#36;3.7 million for the second quarter of 2008.</font></P>
<P align="justify">
<font size="2">Gross profit amounted to &#36;5.3 million for the second quarter of 2009, an increase of 2.0% from &#36;5.2 million for the second quarter of 2008. Gross profit margin was 22.1 % for the second quarter of 2009 compared to 24.1% for the corresponding
quarter in 2008. The decrease in gross profit margin was mainly due to increased cost of raw materials.
</font> </P>
<P align="justify">
<font size="2">Total operating expenses for the second quarter of 2009 were &#36;2.1 million, or 8.8% of revenue, compared to &#36;1.7 million, or 7.9% of revenue, in the second quarter of 2008. The increase in operating expenses was mainly due to an increase of
98.1% in selling and marketing expenses due to an expansion of the Company&#146;s sales network to encompass 26 provinces in China for the second quarter of 2009 compared to 19 provinces for the second quarter of 2008, in addition to marketing
expenses incurred by the initiation of a nationwide commercial campaign to be launched around September 2009. The increase was partially offset by a decrease of 15.9% in general and administrative expenses mainly due to the Company transferring
domestic sales to agents. Consequently, operating income for the second quarter of 2009 was &#36;3.2 million, a decrease of 9.4% from &#36; 3.5 million for the second quarter of 2008.</font></P>
<P align="justify">
<font size="2">Earnings before taxes were &#36;2.6 million for the second quarter of 2009, a decrease of 7.6% from &#36;2.8 million for the corresponding quarter of 2008. The Company&#146;s effective tax rate was 23.5% for the three months ended June 30, 2009
compared to 17.8% for the same period last year. The increase in effective tax rate was due to increased income tax rates for the Company&#146;s operating subsidiaries Junan Hongrun and Beijing Lorain in line with tax reforms implemented by the PRC
government.</font></P>
<P align="justify">
<font size="2">Net income for the second quarter of 2009 was &#36;1.9 million, or &#36;0.074 per diluted share, a decrease of 12.4% from &#36;2.1 million, or &#36;0.084 per diluted share, for the second quarter last year. Basic and diluted weighted average number
of shares outstanding was 25,177,640 for the second quarter of 2009. </font> </P>
<P align="justify">
<B><font size="2">Six Month Results</font></B><font size="2"><BR>
<br>
Revenues for the first six months of 2009 were &#36;45.2 million, up 16.0% from revenues of &#36;39.0 million for the first six months of 2008. Gross profit was &#36;10.5 million, up 14.4% from gross profit of &#36;9.1 million for the first six
months of 2008. Gross margin was 23.1%, compared to 23.4% for the first six months of 2008. Operating income was &#36;6.2 million compared to &#36;6.3 million for the first six months of fiscal 2008. Net income was &#36;3.6 million, or &#36;0.14 per
basic and fully diluted share, compared to &#36;3.8 million, or &#36;0.15 per basic and fully diluted share, for the same period a year ago.
</font>
</P>
<P align="justify">
<B><font size="2">Financial Condition</font></B><font size="2"><BR>
<br>
As of June 30, 2009, American Lorain had &#36;6.8 million in cash and cash equivalents, short term bank loans of &#36;32.0 million and &#36;0.6 million in long-term debt. At quarter end, the Company had a
current ratio of 1.6:1 and &#36;29.1 million in working capital. Days sales outstanding for the three months ended June 30, 2009 were 81 days, while days inventory outstanding were 131 days for the same period, compared to 108 days and 98 days
respectively for the three months ended June 30, 2008. As of June 30, 2009, the Company&#146;s inventories totaled &#36;26.9 million, mainly due to an increase in raw materials. Shareholders' equity stood at &#36;72.5 million as of June 30, 2009
compared to &#36; 67.7 million as of December 31, 2008.</font></P>
