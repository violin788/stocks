<P align=center><B>Company to Hold Conference Call Tuesday, May 17, 2011, at

<P align=justify>JUNAN COUNTY, China, May 16, 2011 -- <B>American Lorain
Corporation (NYSE Amex: ALN) </B>("American Lorain" or the "Company"), an
international processed snack foods, convenience foods, and frozen foods company
based in the Shandong Province, China, today announced financial results for its
first quarter ended March 31, 2011. </P>
<P align=justify><B>Q1 2011 Operational Highlights <BR></B>&#149; Each product
segment increased by over 16% in sales year-over-year <BR>&#149; Convenience food
segment continued to grow as a percent of total revenue <BR>&#149; Company&#146;s
international sales continued to improve, increasing 49.2% </P>
<P align=justify><B>Q1 2011 Financial Highlights<BR></B>&#149; Total revenues of
$30.4 million, an increase of 24.0% year over year <BR>&#149; Gross margins decreased
slightly to 22.2%, compared to 23.3% year-over-year and 22.7% at 12/31/2010
<BR>&#149; Net income attributable to common stockholders of $2.4 million, up 30.4%
year-over-year <BR>&#149; Diluted earnings per share of $0.07 <BR>&#149; Operating cash
flow of $3.8 million for the period ended March 31, 2011 </P>

<P align=justify>American Lorain&#146;s Chairman and CEO, Mr. Si Chen, stated, &#147;We
are extremely pleased with the performance of the Company in the first quarter.
We believe the largest contributor to our growth in the coming months will be
the continued expansion of our convenience foods segment, as was the case in the
first quarter. We continue to focus on both diversifying our lines to include a
wider variety of food products and leveraging our known brand name among
different segments, as evidenced by our expansion in the instant rice market.&#148;
</P>
<P align=justify>&#147;Although the first quarter is seasonally one of our weakest
due to the chestnut business, American Lorain&#146;s highly competitive cost
structure, coordinated sales and marketing platform, and continued diverse food
operations allowed us to deliver another solid quarter. Over the past two years,
American Lorain has made significant improvements to the balance sheet and cash
generation. We reported $3.8 million in operating cash flow during the period.
We believe that the Company is appropriately capitalized to grow organically in
the coming months.&#148;<B> </B> </P>

  prior-year period. Gross margin declined slightly to 22.2% for the three
  months ended March 31, 2011, from 23.3% for the prior-year period. The
  decrease is primarily due to a higher percentage of sales from the American
  Lorain&#146;s convenience foods, which typically sell at a slightly lower margin
  than the Company&#146;s chestnut business. However, American Lorain expects that
  its margins will remain relatively stable and in the 20-25% range in the
  coming months.

  18.0% from $3.3 million reported in the prior year period. Operating margin
  for the 2011 first quarter was 12.9% compared with 13.6% in the prior year.
  <LI>The Company had net income attributable to common shareholders for the
  fourth quarter of 2010 of $2.4 million, or $0.07 per diluted share based on
  35.2 million diluted shares outstanding, compared to $1.9 million, or $0.07
  per diluted share based on 26.7 million diluted shares outstanding in the
  prior-year period. The Company&#146;s net margin for the period improved to 8.0%
  from 7.5% in the prior year period, largely because of lower interest
  expenses. </LI></UL>

<P align=justify><B>Outlook for 2011 </B></P>
<P align=justify>Mr. Chen concluded, &#147;Chestnut sales (both domestically and
internationally) have remained strong throughout the first four months of the
year when compared to last. While it is always a challenge to precisely assess
customer demand for our products, we are optimistic about fiscal 2011 based on
what we are seeing thus far. The efforts of our management group in all three business segments are producing greater
efficiencies in both the operating infrastructure and costs control which will
help us as we continue to grow. We are in a very basic business, supplying easy,
ready-to-eat food to individuals across the globe. In tough economic times,
people tend to cook at home by shopping at their local grocer. This is our core
business and, along with our international presence, we should be better
positioned than many of our local competitors in this regard. We are optimistic
about the outlook of our market growth in China and abroad because of growing
demand, improving brand recognition, and balanced supply. We will continue to
execute on the Company's core strategies of driving growth through each of our
business segments.&#148;</P>
