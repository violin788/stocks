<P align=center><B><FONT size=5>American Lorain Corporation Reports

<P align=justify><B>JUNAN COUNTY, China, August 10, 2010 </B>&#150; American Lorain
Corporation (NYSE Amex: ALN) ("American Lorain" or "the Company&#148;), an
international processed snack foods, convenience foods, and frozen foods company
based in Shandong Province, China, today announced its financial results for its
second quarter ended June 30, 2010. </P>
<P align=justify><B>Second Quarter 2010 Highlights </B></P>
<UL>
  <LI>Revenue was $29.2 million, up 21.9% from 2Q2009 with convenience food
  sales improving 62.6% year over year
  <LI>Gross profit was $6.7 million, up 27.0% from 2Q2009 with gross margins of
  23.0%, a 90- basis point improvement over 2Q2009
  <LI>Operating income margin was 15.7% for the quarter, a 255-basis point
  improvement over 2009
  <LI>Net income was $2.7 million, up 44.4% from 2Q 2009 and earnings per
  diluted shares were $0.10 based on 26.8 million shares </LI></UL>

<P align=justify>&#147;Sales of chestnut and convenience food products remain the
driving force of our operating performance,&#148; began Chairman Si Chen of American
Lorain. "Chestnut sales benefitted from a return of export orders in key Asian
markets which helps level the seasonality of chestnut in China in the first half
of the year. We are confident that our new frozen chestnut products will
contribute to revenue in the third quarter and are pleased to report we have
begun shipping our frozen chestnut raw materials to our Lorain branded retail
counters in China, Japan and the Korean market. Convenience food products remain
an exciting growth category for our Company. After a series of new product
launches, including lunch box entr&#233;es, bean products and cold dishes, we are
pleased to report that customers have been purchasing popular items in each

<P align=justify>Total revenue for the second quarter of 2010 ended June 30,
2010 was $29.2 million, up 21.9% from $24.0 million for the quarter ended June
30, 2009. The increase was primarily due to a higher sales contribution from the
convenience food segment, which grew by 62.6% quarter over quarter. In the
second quarter of 2010 revenues from convenience food products were 35.1% of
total revenues for the quarter, up from 28.9% of total revenues for the quarter
ended March 31, 2010. Products in American Lorain&#146;s convenience foods product
line include &#145;ready to eat&#146; meals and snack foods, &#145;ready to heat&#146; lunch box
entrees and &#145;self-heating&#146; meals for military personnel. As of the period ending
June 30<SUP>th</SUP> 2010, American Lorain has developed and launched more than
100 convenience type foods in the Chinese market and is leveraging its
long-standing distributor and retail networks to introduce Lorain&#174;-branded
convenience foods throughout 5,200 retail points in 26 provinces it currently
sells Lorain&#174;-branded snack foods.</P>
<P align=justify>American Lorain&#146;s core product line includes more than 50
varieties of chestnuts and chestnut-based snack. Sales in this category
increased 7.3% from the second quarter 2009 and met management expectations.
American Lorain&#146;s chestnut business accounted for 51% of the Company&#146;s revenues
and is derived from a combination of domestic and international sales of
chestnuts and chestnut-based snack foods. For the quarter, China accounted for
75.4% of chestnut sales. Export markets accounted for the balance of revenues in
which Japan, Korea and the United Kingdom remained the largest export markets
for the Company.</P>
<P align=justify>American Lorain frozen foods are sold in bulk to commercial
customers in export markets and quick service restaurant chains like KFC&#174; and
Pizza Hut&#174; restaurants in China. Sales of frozen foods are sold including frozen
vegetables and select fruits accounted for 14.0% of revenues in the second of
2010. American Lorain only services a select group of commercial accounts and it
is not actively growing this business segment due to its lower margin
profile.</P>
