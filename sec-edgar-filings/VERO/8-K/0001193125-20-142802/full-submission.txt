 </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman" ALIGN="center"><B>Venus Concept Announces First Quarter of 2020 Financial Results </B></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>TORONTO, May</B><B></B><B>&nbsp;14, 2020 (PR Newswire)</B> &#150; Venus Concept Inc. (&#147;Venus Concept&#148; or the &#147;Company&#148;) (NASDAQ: VERO),
a global medical aesthetic technology leader, announced financial results for the three months ended March&nbsp;31, 2020. The Company notes that the financial results provided for the three months ended March&nbsp;31, 2020 give effect to the merger
that closed on November&nbsp;7, 2019 as described below. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:18pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B><U>Management Commentary: </U></B></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">&#147;Our first quarter revenue performance was significantly impacted by the global pandemic caused by <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">COVID-19,&#148;</FONT>
said Domenic Serafino, Chief Executive Officer of Venus Concept. &#147;Approximately 30% of our 2019 sales came from the APAC and European regions which were impacted by the pandemic throughout the first quarter. We also saw a pronounced decline in
system sales, product sales and service revenues in North America and Latin America beginning in March 2020, primarily as a result of mandated government lockdowns or
<FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap"><FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">&#147;shelter-in-place&#148;</FONT></FONT> requirements in these regions. We expect <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">COVID-19</FONT> will continue to significantly negatively affect
customer demand in the second quarter of fiscal 2020 and into the second half of the year.&#148; </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">&#147;During this period of unprecedented disruption, we
remain focused on supporting our global customers and on protecting the health and safety of our employees. We acted quickly in response to the challenges presented by <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">COVID-19</FONT> to implement measures to reduce
costs and preserve capital. We expect our restructuring program, combined with previously announced synergies and cost reductions, to result in cost savings of approximately $38&nbsp;million in 2020 and continuing into 2021. While the near-term
outlook has been challenged by this global pandemic, we continue to believe the long-term opportunity remains extremely compelling for us as a leading player in both the global minimally <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">invasive/non-invasive</FONT>
medical aesthetics market and the minimally invasive surgical hair restoration market.&#148; </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:18pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B><U>First Quarter 2020 Financial Summary: </U></B></P>
<P STYLE="font-size:6pt;margin-top:0pt;margin-bottom:0pt">&nbsp;</P>

<TR style = "page-break-inside:avoid">
<TD WIDTH="5%">&nbsp;</TD>
<TD WIDTH="3%" VALIGN="top" ALIGN="left">&#149;</TD>

<P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Total revenue for the first quarter of 2020 decreased $10.1&nbsp;million, or 41%, to $14.5&nbsp;million, compared to $24.6&nbsp;million for the first quarter
of 2019. Total products and services revenue for the first quarter of 2020 decreased $1.1&nbsp;million, or 13%, to $7.7&nbsp;million, compared to $8.8&nbsp;million for the first quarter of 2019. Total leases revenue decreased $8.9&nbsp;million, or
57%, to $6.8&nbsp;million, compared to $15.7&nbsp;million for the first quarter of 2019. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">The decrease in total revenue, by geography, for the first
quarter of 2020 was driven by a decrease of $6.2&nbsp;million, or 41%, in international revenue and a decrease of $3.9&nbsp;million, or 41%, in the U.S. revenue, compared to the prior year period. The decrease in revenue in the United States was
driven by <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">COVID-19</FONT> related lockdown restrictions and <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap"><FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">shelter-in-place</FONT></FONT> orders imposed by federal and state governments. The
decrease in revenue in international markets is largely due to business disruptions caused by restrictions imposed by the Chinese government due to the <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">COVID-19</FONT> pandemic in January and February followed by
lockdown restrictions and <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap"><FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">shelter-in-place</FONT></FONT> orders imposed subsequently by federal and local governments in other countries and markets in which we operate. In both the
United States and international markets, the business closures and the resultant uncertainty negatively impacted our ability to access and sell into our customary channels. Where accessibility was possible, selling efforts were hampered by target
customer concerns over economic uncertainty. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">The decrease in total revenue, by product category, for the first quarter of 2020 was attributable to a
decrease of $8.9&nbsp;million, or 57%, in lease revenue, a decrease of $2.8&nbsp;million, or 45%, in system revenue, offset partially by an increase of $1.5&nbsp;million, or 125%, in service revenue and an increase of $0.2&nbsp;million, or 14%, in
product revenue. The decrease in lease revenue for the first quarter of 2020 was driven primarily by <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">COVID-19</FONT> related disruptions, lockdown restrictions and <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap"><FONT
STYLE="white-space:nowrap">shelter-in-place</FONT></FONT> orders imposed by federal and local governments. The decrease in system revenue for the first quarter of 2020 was driven by a 48% decrease in revenue from Venus Concept systems, offset
partially by the contribution of revenue from the sale of ARTAS<SUP STYLE="font-size:85%; vertical-align:top">&reg;</SUP> and ARTAS iX<SUP STYLE="font-size:85%; vertical-align:top">&reg;</SUP> systems, products and services. The increase in service
revenue for the first quarter of 2020 was driven by the contribution of service revenue associated with the ARTAS<SUP STYLE="font-size:85%; vertical-align:top">&reg;</SUP> and ARTAS iX<SUP STYLE="font-size:85%; vertical-align:top">&reg;</SUP><SUP
STYLE="font-size:85%; vertical-align:top"> </SUP>systems, offset partially by a 7% decrease in Venus Concept service revenue. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Gross profit for the first
quarter of 2020 decreased $8.8&nbsp;million, or 49%, to $9.3&nbsp;million, compared to $18.1&nbsp;million for the first quarter of 2019. The decrease in gross profit is primarily due to disruptions due to
<FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">COVID-19</FONT> lockdown restrictions and <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap"><FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">shelter-in-place</FONT></FONT> orders imposed by federal and local governments in countries and markets we
operate. Gross margin was 64.0% of revenue for the first quarter of 2020, compared to 73.5% of revenue for the first quarter of 2019. The decrease in gross profit percentage is primarily related excess manufacturing costs related to the ARTAS<SUP
STYLE="font-size:85%; vertical-align:top">&reg;</SUP> system, as a result of lower than expected production, and inventory fair value adjustments recognized on the business combination with Venus Concept Ltd. expensed through cost of goods sold
during the three months ended March&nbsp;31, 2020. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Operating expenses for the first quarter of 2020 increased $32.9&nbsp;million, or 165%, to
$52.9&nbsp;million, compared to $19.9&nbsp;million for the first quarter of 2019. The year-over-year increase in operating expenses was primarily driven by a <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">non-cash,</FONT>
<FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">pre-tax,</FONT> goodwill impairment charge of $27.5&nbsp;million. Excluding the impairment charge, operating expenses for the first quarter of 2020 increased $5.5&nbsp;million, or 27%, driven by an increase of
$5.8&nbsp;million, or 70%, in general and administrative expenses and, to a lesser extent, an increase of $0.6&nbsp;million, or 27%, in research and development expense, offset partially by a decrease of $0.9&nbsp;million, or 10%, in sales and
marketing expenses. The year-over-year increase in general and administrative expenses driven primarily by increased costs related to public company reporting obligations, increased bad debt expense and additional amortization of intangible assets
recognized on the business combination with Venus Concept Ltd. </P>

<P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Adjusted EBITDA <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">is&nbsp;a&nbsp;non-GAAP&nbsp;measure&nbsp;defined</FONT> as net loss income before foreign exchange loss,

performance under U.S. GAAP and should not be considered an alternative to net income or any other performance measures derived in accordance with U.S. GAAP. Accordingly, you should consider Adjusted EBITDA along with other financial performance
measures, including net income, and our financial results presented in accordance with U.S. GAAP. Other companies, including companies in our industry, may calculate Adjusted EBITDA differently or not at all, which reduces its usefulness as a
comparative measure. We understand that although Adjusted EBITDA is frequently used by securities analysts, lenders and others in their evaluation of companies, Adjusted EBITDA has limitations as an analytical tool, and you should not consider it in
isolation, or as a substitute for analysis of our results as reported under U.S. GAAP. Some of these limitations are: Adjusted EBITDA does not reflect our cash expenditures or future requirements for capital expenditures or contractual commitments;
Adjusted EBITDA does not reflect changes in, or cash requirements for, our working capital needs; and although depreciation and amortization <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">are&nbsp;a&nbsp;non-cash&nbsp;charges,&nbsp;the</FONT> assets being
depreciated will often have to be replaced in the future, and Adjusted EBITDA does not reflect any cash requirements for such replacements. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">We believe
that Adjusted EBITDA is a useful measure for analyzing the performance of our core business because it facilitates operating performance comparisons from period to period and company to company by backing out potential differences caused by changes
in foreign exchange rates that impact financial assets and liabilities denominated in currencies other than the U.S. dollar, tax positions (such as the impact on periods or companies of changes in effective tax rates), the age and book depreciation
