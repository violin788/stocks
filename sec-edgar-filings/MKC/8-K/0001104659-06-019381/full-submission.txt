meeting.&#160; Notice is given to a
stockholder when it is personally delivered to him or her, left at his or her
residence or usual place of business, or mailed to him or her at his or her
address as it appears on the records of the Corporation.&#160; Notwithstanding the foregoing provisions,

Voting; Adjournments</u>.&#160; Unless statute
or the Charter provides otherwise, at a meeting of stockholders the presence in
person or by proxy of stockholders entitled to cast a majority of all the votes
entitled to be cast at the meeting constitutes a quorum, and a majority of all
the votes cast at a meeting at which a quorum is present is sufficient to
approve any matter which properly comes before the meeting, except that a
plurality of all the votes cast at a meeting at which a quorum is present is
sufficient to elect a director.&#160; Whether
or not a quorum is present, a meeting of stockholders convened on the date for
which it was called may be adjourned from time to time without further notice
by a majority vote of the stockholders present in person or by proxy to a date
not more than 120 days after the original record date.&#160; Any business which might have been transacted
at the meeting as originally notified may be deferred and transacted at any
such adjourned meeting at which a quorum shall be present.</font></p>

the Charter limits or denies voting rights or provides for a greater or lesser
number of votes per share, each outstanding share of stock, regardless of
class, is entitled to one vote on each matter submitted to a vote at a meeting
of stockholders.&#160; In all elections for
directors, each share of stock may be voted for as many individuals as there
are directors to be elected and for whose election the share is entitled to be
voted.&#160; A stockholder may vote the stock
the stockholder owns of record either in person or by proxy.&#160; A stockholder may sign a writing authorizing
another person to act as proxy.&#160; Signing
may be accomplished by the stockholder or the stockholder&#146;s authorized agent
signing the writing or causing the stockholder&#146;s signature to be affixed to the
writing by any reasonable means, including facsimile signature.&#160; A stockholder may authorize another person to
act as proxy by transmitting, or authorizing the transmission of, a telegram,
cablegram, datagram, or other means of electronic transmission to the person
authorized to act as proxy or to a proxy solicitation firm, proxy support
service organization, or other person authorized by the person who will act as
proxy to receive the transmission.&#160;
Unless a proxy provides otherwise, it is not valid more than 11 months
after its date.&#160; A proxy is revocable by
a stockholder at any time without condition or qualification unless the proxy
states that it is irrevocable and the proxy is coupled with an interest.&#160; A proxy may be made irrevocable for so long
as it is coupled with an interest.&#160; The
interest with which a proxy may be coupled includes an interest in the stock to
be voted under the proxy or another general interest in the Corporation or its
assets or liabilities.</font></p>
