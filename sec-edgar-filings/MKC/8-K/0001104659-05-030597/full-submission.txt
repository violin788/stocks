<p align="right" style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-align:right;text-indent:4.0in;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p align="center" style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-align:center;"><b><u><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;font-weight:bold;">McCORMICK REPORTS&#160; SECOND QUARTER RESULTS AND PROJECTS STRONG </font></u></b></p>

<p align="center" style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-align:center;"><b><u><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;font-weight:bold;">SECOND HALF FOR FISCAL YEAR</font></u></b></p>

<p align="center" style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-align:center;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-indent:.5in;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">SPARKS,
MD, JUNE&nbsp;30 - - - McCormick&nbsp;&amp; Company, Incorporated (NYSE:MKC),
today reported record sales and earnings per share for the second quarter ended
May&nbsp;31, 2005 and projected strong profit growth in the second half of the
fiscal year.&#160; Earnings per share for the
year are projected to be $1.66-$1.70.&#160;
Since the prior guidance of $1.70-$1.74, foreign exchange rates have
become less favorable and the Company has moderated its outlook for industrial
business growth.</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-indent:.5in;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style="font-size:10.0pt;margin:0in 0in .0001pt .5in;text-indent:-.25in;"><font size="3" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:12.0pt;">&#149;</font><font size="1" style="font-size:3.0pt;">&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160; </font><b><font style="font-weight:bold;">Second quarter sales
increased 5% to $629 million, and earnings per share were 31&#162;.</font></b></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt .5in;text-indent:-.25in;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style="font-size:10.0pt;margin:0in 0in .0001pt .5in;text-indent:-.25in;"><font size="3" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:12.0pt;">&#149;</font><font size="1" style="font-size:3.0pt;">&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160; </font><b><font style="font-weight:bold;">Earnings per share in the
second half are projected to grow 16%-20%.</font></b></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt .5in;text-indent:-.25in;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style="font-size:10.0pt;margin:0in 0in .0001pt .5in;text-indent:-.25in;"><font size="3" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:12.0pt;">&#149;</font><font size="1" style="font-size:3.0pt;">&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160; </font><b><font style="font-weight:bold;">The Board of Directors
approved a new program to repurchase $400 million of outstanding shares.</font></b></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt .5in;text-indent:-.25in;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-indent:.5in;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Sales
for the quarter rose 5% to $629 million compared to the second quarter of 2004.
New products, effective marketing programs and incremental sales from the Silvo
business acquired in 2004 drove an increase of 3%.&#160; Favorable foreign exchange rates added 2%.</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-indent:.5in;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-indent:.5in;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Earnings
per share for the second quarter were 31&#162; compared to earnings per share of 30&#162;
reported in the second quarter of 2004.&#160;
Higher operating income added 1&#162; to earnings per share and was the net
result of an increase in sales, lower gross profit margin, and the effect of
income related to the settlement of a class action lawsuit that was recorded in
the second quarter of 2004.&#160; Higher cost
vanilla beans continued to have an impact on gross profit margin in the second
quarter.&#160; Gross profit margin was also
affected by additional operational accounting adjustments related to the
condiment operation in the U.K.&#160; Earnings
per share this period were&#160; impacted by
higher income from the Company&#146;s joint venture in Mexico and lower shares
outstanding.&#160; These positive factors were
offset by higher interest rates and a higher tax rate.</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-indent:.5in;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-indent:.5in;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">During
the quarter, the Company funded $75 million of share repurchases and $22
million of dividends with net cash from operations and increased
borrowings.&#160; Dividend payments increased
12% compared to the second quarter of 2004, and share repurchases rose 11%.</font></p>

<p align="center" style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-align:center;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><b><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;font-weight:bold;">Chairman&#146;s
Comments</font></b></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-indent:.5in;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Robert
J. Lawless, Chairman, President&nbsp;&amp; CEO, commented, &#147;In the second
quarter, we achieved higher sales, a positive product mix and cost reductions
that more than offset the adverse effect of high cost vanilla beans and
operational accounting adjustments.&#160; As
we look to the second half of our fiscal year, we expect strong sales growth,
robust margin improvement and a 16%-20% increase in earnings per share.&#160; Based on a less favorable foreign exchange
outlook and a moderation in projected growth for our industrial business,
however, we have lowered our earnings per share guidance for the full year to
$1.66-$1.70.&#160; For the third quarter, we
are projecting earnings per share of 36&#162;-37&#162;, an increase of 9%-12% compared to
a 33&#162; result in 2004.</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-indent:.5in;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-indent:.5in;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&#147;Second
quarter sales were positively impacted by successful new products, effective
marketing programs, favorable foreign exchange rates and the acquisition of
Silvo.&#160; Earnings were further increased
as we reduced costs across our organization.&#160;
Our consumer business results in the Americas were particularly strong,
and we were pleased to achieve higher sales in our European consumer business
despite difficult market conditions.&#160;
Sales and profits in our industrial business were affected by lower
vanilla prices, some delay in the launch of new products by our customers and
the additional operational accounting adjustments in our U.K. condiment
operation.&#160; These challenges offset
strong sales and underlying margin improvement for the first six months of 2005
and&#160; led to a 1&#162; decline in earnings per
share when compared to 2004.</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-indent:.5in;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-indent:.5in;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&#147;For
the second half, we are projecting significantly improved financial
performance. We expect to grow sales about 4% based on our new product
activity, marketing programs and the addition of Silvo.&#160; Consumer business sales are projected to grow
at a faster pace than industrial business sales during this period.&#160; While the industrial business will continue
to have strong sales in products such as snack seasonings, other new products
in our pipeline have not yet been launched by our customers.&#160;&#160;&#160; During the second half, we will reduce
costs and benefit from a positive product mix, increasing gross profit margin
an estimated 0.5 percentage points for the full fiscal year.&#160; With a focused effort by employees throughout
McCormick, we are on track to achieve our cost savings goal of $25 million.
Together, the benefits of higher sales and margin improvement are projected to
increase earnings per share 16%-20% in the second half.</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-indent:.5in;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-indent:.5in;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&#147;With
the cash generated during the first half of 2005, we repurchased shares and are
nearing completion of the $300 million authorization approved in September&nbsp;2003.&#160; Earlier this week, McCormick&#146;s Board of
Directors approved a new repurchase program to buy back up to $400 million of
outstanding stock.&#160; This program is
expected to be completed by the end of 2007 in the absence of significant
acquisition activity.</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-indent:.5in;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-indent:.5in;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&#147;In
the first half of this fiscal year we faced a number of challenges.&#160; As we begin the second half, many of these
challenges are behind us.&#160; We are
confident that higher sales, improved margins and the benefit of our strong
cash flow will deliver strong financial results and increased value for
McCormick shareholders.&#148;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-indent:.5in;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">For the second quarter,
sales for McCormick&#146;s consumer business rose 9% when compared to 2004.&#160; Higher volume added 5% to sales, including an
increase from the acquisition of Silvo.&#160;
Favorable price and product mix added 2%, and favorable foreign exchange
rates also added 2%.&#160; In the Americas,
sales increased 5% with higher pricing, new product sales and effective
marketing programs.&#160; Consumer sales in
Europe increased 18% for the quarter, with 12% due to the acquisition of Silvo
in November&nbsp;2004 and 5% due to favorable foreign exchange.&#160; Sales from new products and marketing
programs overcame difficult market conditions, primarily in France.&#160; In the Asia/Pacific region, sales declined
1%, despite favorable foreign exchange rates that added 3%.</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-indent:.5in;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Higher
sales and a favorable product mix drove a 19% increase in operating income,
more than offsetting impacts from lower vanilla margins and the operational
accounting adjustments.&#160; This follows an
increase in operating income of 25% during the second quarter of 2004.</font></p>

seasoning sales as well as increased sales to food service distributors.&#160; As in the first quarter, these increases were
partially offset by lower vanilla prices.&#160;

exchange contributing 4%. Steps to eliminate certain lower margin products
began in 2004 and continue to have an impact on sales in 2005. In the
Asia/Pacific region, industrial sales rose 4% led by higher sales to quick
service restaurants, other food processors and food service distributors.&#160; In this region, 2% was added by favorable
foreign exchange during the quarter.</font></p>
