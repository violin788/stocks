<p align="right" style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-align:right;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p align="center" style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-align:center;"><b><u><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;font-weight:bold;">McCORMICK
REPORTS THIRD QUARTER INCREASE IN SALES AND PROFITS</font></u></b></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-indent:.5in;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-indent:.5in;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">SPARKS, MD, SEPTEMBER 28
- - - - McCormick &amp; Company, Incorporated (NYSE:MKC), today reported
increased sales and earnings per share for the third quarter ended August 31,
2005.</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-indent:.5in;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Sales for the quarter
were $623 million, a 1.5% increase from the third quarter of 2004.&#160; Sales from the Silvo business acquired in
2004 and favorable foreign exchange rates were offset in part by lower sales to
industrial customers.</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-indent:.5in;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-indent:.5in;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Earnings per share for
the third quarter were 35&#162; compared to earnings per share of 33&#162; reported in
the third quarter of 2004.&#160; Higher
operating income from the sales increase as well as higher margins added 2&#162; to
earnings per share.&#160; The negative effects
of higher interest rates and an increase in the effective tax rate were offset
by higher income from the Company&#146;s joint venture in Mexico and lower shares
outstanding.</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-indent:.5in;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-indent:.5in;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Through the first three
quarters of 2005, the Company funded $141 million of share repurchases and $65
million of dividends with net cash from operations and increased
borrowings.&#160; Year-to-date, dividend
payments increased 12% compared to 2004 and share repurchases rose 30%.&#160; During this period, the Company has
maintained capital expenditures at the same level as 2004.</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-indent:.5in;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><b><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;font-weight:bold;">Chairman&#146;s Comments</font></b></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-indent:.5in;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-indent:.5in;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Robert J. Lawless,
Chairman, President &amp; CEO, commented, &#147;During the third quarter, we
achieved higher profits with increased sales and income from our consumer
business, the benefit of our cost savings program and improved income from
joint ventures.&#160; As expected, sales to
industrial customers were weak this quarter.&#160;
We were also affected by higher interest rates and an increase in our
tax rate.&#160; Based on our outlook for the
fourth quarter, we continue to expect 2005 fiscal year earnings per share of
$1.58 to $1.62.</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-indent:.5in;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-indent:.5in;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&#147;During 2005, the Company
has been challenged by a high cost inventory of vanilla beans, weakness in
industrial sales, an accounting adjustment and the effects of hurricane
Katrina.&#160; Together, these factors have
interrupted a period of strong sales and profit growth that began in 1999.&#160; As announced early in September, a review of
our industrial business is underway as well as certain actions to improve the
efficiency of our entire supply chain. With these actions, we are confident
that long term, our sales growth, margin improvement and strong cash flow will
once again lead to excellent financial results and increased value for
McCormick shareholders.&#148;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-indent:.5in;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">For the third quarter,
sales for McCormick&#146;s consumer business rose 4% versus the prior year.&#160; This follows a 12% sales increase in the
third quarter of 2004.&#160; In 2005, higher
volume, price and product mix increased sales 3%, due largely to the
acquisition of Silvo in November 2004.&#160;
Favorable foreign exchange rates added 1%.&#160; In the Americas, sales increased 3% due
primarily to favorable pricing and product mix, as well as to favorable foreign
exchange rates that added 1%.&#160; Consumer
sales in Europe rose 8% for the quarter, including an unfavorable 1% from
foreign exchange rates.&#160; Silvo increased
sales in Europe 11%, while difficult market conditions, primarily in France,
had a negative effect.&#160; In the third
quarter, sales declined slightly in the Asia/Pacific region.</font></p>

4%, in line with the higher sales.&#160; This
follows an increase in operating income of 25% during the third quarter of
2004.</font></p>

<p style="font-size:10.0pt;margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-indent:.5in;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">For the third quarter of
2005, sales for McCormick&#146;s industrial business decreased 1% when compared to
2004.&#160; This followed a 9% sales increase
in the third quarter of 2004.&#160; In the
third quarter of 2005, lower vanilla prices reduced sales 3%</font><b><i><font style="font-style:italic;font-weight:bold;"> </font></i></b>&#160;and favorable foreign exchange added 1%.&#160; During this period, the Company experienced
volatility in demand with certain customers as well as pricing pressure due to
competitive activity. These factors affected industrial sales in the Americas
which declined 1%, despite a 1% increase from favorable foreign exchange
rates.&#160; In Europe, sales decreased 8%,
with a 1% negative impact from foreign exchange rates.&#160; In addition to volatility and competitive
pressure, sales in this region were also affected by the elimination of certain
lower margin products. Sales in the Asia/Pacific region rose 15% driven by
increases in both China and Australia.&#160;
In this region, 4% was added by favorable foreign exchange rates during
the quarter.</p>
