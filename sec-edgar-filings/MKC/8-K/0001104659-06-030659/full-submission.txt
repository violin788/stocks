<p align="center" style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-align:center;"><b><u><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;font-weight:bold;">McCORMICK COMPLETES ACQUISITION OF REMAINING SHARE OF </font></u></b></p>

<p align="center" style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-align:center;"><b><u><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;font-weight:bold;">DESSERT PRODUCTS INTERNATIONAL </font></u></b></p>

<p align="center" style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-align:center;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p align="center" style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-align:center;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-indent:.5in;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">SPARKS, MD, MAY 2 - - -
McCormick &amp; Company, Incorporated (NYSE:MKC) today announced that it has
completed the acquisition of the remaining 49% share of Dessert Products
International, S.A.S. (DPI) from Hero A.G. in exchange for its 50% interest in
the Signature Brands, LLC joint venture.&#160;
The initial agreement for these transactions was first announced on
February 17, 2006.&#160; The Company had
acquired a 51% interest in the DPI business as part of the acquisition of the
Ducros business in August 2000.&#160; As a
result of this transaction, McCormick now has 100% ownership of DPI, which
markets the Vahine brand in France and other European countries.</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-indent:.5in;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-indent:.5in;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Robert J. Lawless,
Chairman, President &amp; CEO of McCormick, stated, &#147;This action is another
step in McCormick&#146;s plan to simplify its business and focus on those areas with
strong growth potential.&#160; Vahine is the
leading brand of dessert aids in France and other European countries.&#160; Since acquiring a 51% interest in this
business in 2000, product innovation and expanded distribution have increased
sales and net income more than 50%.&#148;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-indent:.5in;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-indent:.5in;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">As previously announced,
the impact of this exchange on McCormick&#146;s ongoing sales and net income is
expected to be neutral.&#160; However, in the
second quarter, the Company expects to record a net gain after-tax of
approximately $27 million that will increase 2006 earnings per share approximately
20&#162;.&#160; The Company has increased its
projected earnings per share for 2006 from a range of $1.21-$1.24 to a range of
$1.41-$1.44.&#160; This range still includes
the impact of special charges in connection with the previously announced
restructuring plan which are projected to be 42&#162;, as well as 11&#162; of stock-based
compensation expense that the Company began to record in 2006.</font></p>
