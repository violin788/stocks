<div>

<p align="right" style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-align:right;"><b><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;font-weight:bold;">Exhibit 99.1</font></b></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE</font></p>

<p align="center" style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-align:center;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p align="center" style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-align:center;"><b><u><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;font-weight:bold;">McCORMICK REPORTS FIRST QUARTER RESULTS AND</font></u></b></p>

<p align="center" style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-align:center;"><b><u><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;font-weight:bold;">REAFFIRMS FISCAL YEAR GOALS</font></u></b></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-indent:.5in;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-indent:.5in;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-indent:.5in;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">SPARKS, MD, MARCH 22 - -
- - McCormick &amp; Company, Incorporated (NYSE:MKC), today reported sales and
earnings per share for the first quarter ended February 28, 2005.</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-indent:.5in;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Sales for the quarter
rose 5% to $604 million compared to the first quarter of 2004.&#160; Higher volume, pricing and product mix
contributed 3% of the increase, of which 2% was due to the acquisition of
Silvo.&#160; Favorable foreign exchange rates
added 2%.</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-indent:.5in;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-indent:.5in;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Earnings per share for
the first quarter were 26&#162;, compared to earnings per share of 27&#162; reported in
the first quarter of 2004.&#160; Despite
higher sales, a strong performance by the Company&#146;s joint venture in Mexico, and
lower shares outstanding, earnings declined as a result of a lower gross profit
margin, higher interest rates and a higher tax rate.&#160; In addition, special charges in 2005 reduced
earnings per share by 1&#162;.</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-indent:.5in;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-indent:.5in;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Gross profit margin for
the first quarter of 2005 was significantly impacted by vanilla.&#160; The Company purchased a strategic supply of
vanilla beans in 2003 to ensure an ongoing supply of quality product for
customers and manage the cost of this raw material.&#160; However, a larger than expected crop has
caused vanilla bean costs to drop rapidly and as projected, the Company is
experiencing significant margin pressure, particularly in the industrial
business.&#160; This situation is expected to
have a significant impact through the first half of 2005.&#160; The performance of the industrial business in
Europe also had a negative impact on first quarter gross profit margin.</font></p>

repurchases and $22 million of dividends.&#160;
Dividend payments increased 13% compared to the first quarter of
2004.&#160; The $45 million spent for share
repurchase compares to $13 million of repurchases in the prior year.</font></p>

Despite our first quarter results, our growth targets for the year

in 2004 and will continue to have an impact on sales in 2005. In the

service restaurants as well as other food processors.&#160; In this region, 2% was added by favorable
