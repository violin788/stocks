<p align="center" style="margin:0pt 0pt 12.0pt;text-align:center;"><b><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;font-weight:bold;">&nbsp;</font></b></p>

<p align="center" style="font-family:Times New Roman;font-size:10.0pt;margin:0pt 0pt 12.0pt;text-align:center;"><b><u><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;font-weight:bold;">ARTICLE I</font></u>.</b></p>

<p style="font-family:Times New Roman;font-size:10.0pt;margin:0pt 0pt 12.0pt;text-indent:36.0pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">1.&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160; </font><font size="1" style="font-size:6.5pt;position:relative;top:-3.0pt;">1</font><u>Principal Office</u>.&#160; The principal office shall be at 18 Loveton

<p style="margin:0pt 0pt 12.0pt;text-indent:72.0pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">The Corporation may also have offices at such other
places as the Board of Directors may from time to time appoint, or the business
of the Corporation may require.</font></p>

<p style="font-family:Times New Roman;font-size:10.0pt;margin:0pt 0pt 12.0pt;text-indent:36.0pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">2.&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160; </font><font size="1" style="font-size:6.5pt;position:relative;top:-3.0pt;">2</font><u>Seal</u>.&#160; The seal of the Corporation shall be in

<p align="center" style="margin:0pt 0pt .0001pt;text-align:center;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">McCormick &amp;

<p align="center" style="font-family:Times New Roman;font-size:10.0pt;margin:0pt 0pt 12.0pt;text-align:center;"><b><u><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;font-weight:bold;">ARTICLE II</font></u>.</b></p>

<p align="center" style="font-family:Times New Roman;font-size:10.0pt;margin:0pt 0pt 12.0pt;text-align:center;"><u><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Stockholders&#146; Meetings</font></u>.</p>

<p style="font-family:Times New Roman;font-size:10.0pt;margin:0pt 0pt 12.0pt;text-indent:36.0pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">3.&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160; </font><font size="1" style="font-size:6.5pt;position:relative;top:-3.0pt;">3</font><u>Place of Meeting</u>.&#160; All meetings of the stockholders shall be
held at the time and place determined by the Board of Directors of the
Corporation.</p>

<p style="font-family:Times New Roman;font-size:10.0pt;margin:0pt 0pt 12.0pt;text-indent:36.0pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">4.&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160; </font><font size="1" style="font-size:6.5pt;position:relative;top:-3.0pt;">4</font><u>Annual Meeting</u>.&#160; An annual meeting for the election of
Directors and for the transaction of such other business as may be properly
brought before the meeting shall be held on the first Wednesday in April of
every year beginning with the year 2008.</p>

<p style="font-family:Times New Roman;font-size:10.0pt;margin:0pt 0pt 12.0pt;text-indent:36.0pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">5.&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160; </font><font size="1" style="font-size:6.5pt;position:relative;top:-3.0pt;">5</font><u>Notice of Annual Meetings;
Waiver of Notice</u>.&#160; Not less than ten
nor more than 90 days before each stockholders&#146; meeting, the Secretary shall
give written notice of the meeting to each stockholder entitled to vote at the
meeting and each other stockholder entitled to notice of the meeting.&#160; The notice shall state the time and place of
the meeting and, if the meeting is a special meeting or notice of the purpose
is required by statute, the purpose of the meeting.&#160; Notice is given to a stockholder when it is
personally delivered to him or her, left at his or her residence or usual place
of business, or mailed to him or her at his or her address as it appears on the
records of the Corporation.&#160;
Notwithstanding the foregoing provisions, each person who is entitled to
notice waives notice if he or she before or after the meeting signs a waiver of
the notice which is filed with the records of stockholders&#146; meetings, or is
present at the meeting in person or by proxy.</p>

<p style="font-family:Times New Roman;font-size:10.0pt;margin:4.5000pt 0pt 12.0pt;text-indent:36.0pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">6.&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160; </font><font size="1" style="font-size:6.5pt;position:relative;top:-3.0pt;">6</font><u>Quorum; Voting; Adjournments</u>.&#160; Unless statute or the Charter provides
otherwise, at a meeting of stockholders the presence in person or by proxy of
stockholders entitled to cast a majority of all the votes entitled to be cast
at the meeting constitutes a quorum, and a majority of all the votes cast at a
meeting at which a quorum is present is sufficient to approve any matter which
properly comes before the meeting, except as provided in the next
sentence.&#160; Except as provided in Section
21 of these By-Laws or as otherwise required by law or the Charter, each Director
shall be elected by the vote of a majority of the votes cast with respect to
the Director at any meeting for the election of Directors at which a quorum is
present, provided that if the number of nominees exceeds the number of
Directors to be elected, the Directors shall be elected by the vote of a
plurality at the meeting at which a quorum is present.&#160; Whether or not a quorum is present, a meeting
of stockholders convened on the date for which it was called may be adjourned
from time to time without further notice by a majority vote of the stockholders
present in person or by proxy to a date not more than 120 days after the
original record date.&#160; Any business which
might have been transacted at the meeting as originally notified may be
deferred and transacted at any such adjourned meeting at which a quorum shall
be present.</p>

<p style="font-family:Times New Roman;font-size:10.0pt;margin:0pt 0pt 12.0pt;text-indent:36.0pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">7.&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160; </font><font size="1" style="font-size:6.5pt;position:relative;top:-3.0pt;">7</font><u>General Right to Vote;
Proxies</u>.&#160; Except where the Charter
limits or denies voting rights or provides for a greater or lesser number of
votes per share, each outstanding share of stock, regardless of class, is
entitled to one vote on each matter submitted to a vote at a meeting of
stockholders.&#160; In all elections for
directors, each share of stock may be voted for as many individuals as there
are directors to be elected and for whose election the share is entitled to be
voted.&#160; A stockholder may vote the stock
the stockholder owns of record either in person or by proxy.&#160; A stockholder may sign a writing authorizing
another person to act as proxy.&#160; Signing
may be accomplished by the stockholder or the stockholder&#146;s authorized agent
signing the writing or causing the stockholder&#146;s signature to be affixed to the
writing by any reasonable means, including facsimile signature.&#160; A stockholder may authorize another person to
act as proxy by transmitting, or authorizing the transmission of, a telegram,
cablegram, datagram, or other means of electronic transmission to the person
authorized to act as proxy or to a proxy solicitation firm, proxy support
service organization, or other person authorized by the person who will act as proxy
to receive the transmission.&#160; Unless a
proxy provides otherwise, it is not valid more than 11 months after its
date.&#160; A proxy is revocable by a
stockholder at any time without condition or qualification unless the proxy
states that it is irrevocable and the proxy is coupled with an interest.&#160; A proxy may be made irrevocable for so long
as it is coupled with an interest.&#160; The
interest with which a proxy may be coupled includes an interest in the stock to
be voted under the proxy or another general interest in the Corporation or its
assets or liabilities.</p>

<p style="font-family:Times New Roman;font-size:10.0pt;margin:0pt 0pt 12.0pt;text-indent:36.0pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">8.&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160; </font><font size="1" style="font-size:6.5pt;position:relative;top:-3.0pt;">8</font><u>List of Stockholders</u>.&#160; A complete record of stockholders entitled to
vote at the ensuing election, arranged in alphabetical order, with the
residence of each and the number of voting shares held by each shall be
prepared by the Secretary and filed in the office of the Secretary, at least
ten days before every election.</p>


 <p style="margin:24.0pt 0pt .0001pt;text-align:center;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">2</font></p> <br><hr size="3" width="100%" noshade color="#010101" align="center">

<p style="font-family:Times New Roman;font-size:10.0pt;margin:4.5000pt 0pt 12.0pt;text-indent:36.0pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">9.&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160; </font><font size="1" style="font-size:6.5pt;position:relative;top:-3.0pt;">9</font><u>Special Meetings</u>.&#160; At any time in the interval between annual
meetings, a special meeting of the stockholders may be called by the Chairman
of the Board or the President or by a majority of the Board of Directors by
vote at a meeting or in writing (addressed to the Secretary of the Corporation)
with or without a meeting.&#160; Special
meetings of the stockholders shall be called by the Secretary at the request of
stockholders only as may be required by law and, if the request is made on or
after October 1, 1996, only if requested by stockholders entitled to cast 50%
of the votes entitled to be cast at the meeting.&#160; A request for a special meeting shall state
the purpose of the meeting and the matters proposed to be acted on at it.&#160; The Secretary shall inform the stockholders
who make the request of the reasonably estimated costs of preparing and mailing
a notice of the meeting and, on payment of these costs to the Corporation,
notify each stockholder entitled to notice of the meeting.&#160; Unless requested by stockholders entitled to
cast a majority of all the votes entitled to be cast at the meeting, a special
meeting need not be called to consider any matter which is substantially the
same as a matter voted on at any special meeting of stockholders held in the
preceding 12 months.&#160; Business
transaction at all special meetings shall be confined to the objects stated in
the call.</p>

<p style="font-family:Times New Roman;font-size:10.0pt;margin:0pt 0pt 12.0pt;text-indent:36.0pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">10.&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160; </font><font size="1" style="font-size:6.5pt;position:relative;top:-3.0pt;">10</font><u>Conduct of Business and
Voting</u>.&#160; At all meetings of
stockholders, unless the voting is conducted by inspectors, the proxies and
ballots shall be received, and all questions touching the qualifications of
voters and the validity of proxies, the acceptance or rejection of votes and
procedures for the conduct of business not otherwise specified by these

certification of the election results.&#160;

the Board&#146;s decision.&#160; A Director who is
an employee of the Corporation shall cease to be a Director concurrent with his
termination, resignation or retirement from active employment; provided
however, that the Chairman of the Board of Directors may continue to serve
until the next Annual Meeting of Stockholders following his retirement from
active employment.&#160; Non-employee
directors shall be ineligible for election or re-election to the Board of
Directors after reaching age 70.&#160; The
Board of Directors shall keep minutes of its meetings and a full account of its
transactions.&#160; The number of Directors

decreased to such number (not less than six, nor, unless this Section has been
amended by the Board, more than 20) as the Board of Directors may deem proper
or expedient, but such action shall not affect the tenure of any Director.</p>

<p align="center" style="font-family:Times New Roman;font-size:10.0pt;margin:0pt 0pt 12.0pt;text-align:center;"><b><u><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;font-weight:bold;">ARTICLE VII</font></u>.</b></p>

<p style="margin:0pt 0pt 12.0pt;text-indent:36.0pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">39.&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160; <u>Fiscal
Year</u>.&#160; The fiscal year of the
Corporation shall commence on whatever date is determined as most practical by
the Board of Directors, and shall end twelve months thereafter.</font></p>

<p align="center" style="font-family:Times New Roman;font-size:10.0pt;margin:0pt 0pt 12.0pt;text-align:center;"><b><u><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;font-weight:bold;">ARTICLE VIII</font></u>.</b></p>

<p style="margin:0pt 0pt 12.0pt;text-indent:36.0pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">40.&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160; <u>Dividends</u>.&#160; Dividends upon the capital stock of the
Corporation, subject to the provisions of the certificate of incorporation, if
any, may be declared by the Board of Directors at any regular or special
meeting, pursuant to law.&#160; Dividends may
be paid in cash, in property, or in shares of the capital stock.&#160; Before payment of any dividend, there may be
set aside out of any funds of the Corporation available for dividends such sum
or sums as the Directors from time to time, in their absolute discretion, think
proper as a reserve fund to meet contingencies, or for equalizing dividends; or
for repairing or maintaining any property of the Corporation; or for such other
purposes as the Directors shall think conducive to the interests of the
Corporation.</font></p>

<p align="center" style="font-family:Times New Roman;font-size:10.0pt;margin:0pt 0pt 12.0pt;text-align:center;"><b><u><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;font-weight:bold;">ARTICLE IX</font></u>.</b></p>

<p align="center" style="margin:0pt 0pt 12.0pt;text-align:center;"><u><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Stock</font></u></p>

<p style="font-family:Times New Roman;font-size:10.0pt;margin:0pt 0pt 12.0pt;text-indent:36.0pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">41.&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160; </font><font size="1" style="font-size:6.5pt;position:relative;top:-3.0pt;">25</font><u>Certificates/Book Entry
Registration</u>.&#160; Shares of the capital
stock of the Corporation may be evidenced by electronic book entry, or by
certificate.&#160; Upon request of a
stockholder, the Corporation shall issue a numbered certificate to the
stockholder which describes the number and class of shares owned by the
stockholder.&#160; Said certificate shall be
signed and sealed by such officers and in such manner as may be authorized and
directed by the Board of Directors.</p>

<p style="font-family:Times New Roman;font-size:10.0pt;margin:0pt 0pt 12.0pt;text-indent:36.0pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">42.&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160; </font><font size="1" style="font-size:6.5pt;position:relative;top:-3.0pt;">26</font><u>Transfer of Shares</u>.&#160; Shares of stock shall be transferable on the
books of the Corporation only by the holder thereof in person, or by his duly
authorized attorney, and, in the case of stock evidenced by a certificate, by
endorsement and surrender of the certificate.</p>

<p style="font-family:Times New Roman;font-size:10.0pt;margin:4.5000pt 0pt 12.0pt;text-indent:36.0pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">43.&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160; </font><font size="1" style="font-size:6.5pt;position:relative;top:-3.0pt;">21</font><u>Closing Books of the
Corporation Against Transfer of Stock; Record Dates</u>.&#160; The Board of Directors may fix a time not
exceeding twenty (20) days preceding the date of any meeting of stockholders,
any dividend payment date, or any date for the allotment of rights, during
which the books of the Corporation shall be closed against the transfer of
stock.&#160; In lieu of providing for the
closing of the books against transfer of stock as aforesaid, the Board of
Directors may fix in advance a time not exceeding ninety (90) days preceding
any dividend date, or any date for the allotment of rights, as record date for
the determination of the stockholders entitled to receive such dividend or
rights, as the case may be, and, in that event, only stockholders of record on
such date shall be entitled to receive such dividend or rights, as the case may
be.&#160; Except as set forth in Paragraph 7
of these By-Laws, the Board of Directors may fix in advance a time not
exceeding ninety (90) days preceding any meeting of stockholders as record date
for the determination of stockholders entitled to vote at a stockholders&#146;
meeting to be called by the Board of Directors.</p>

<p style="margin:0pt 0pt 12.0pt;text-indent:36.0pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">44.&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160; <u>Mutilated,
Lost or Destroyed Certificates</u>.&#160; The
holder of any mutilated certificate shall immediately notify the Corporation,
and the Board of Directors may, in its discretion, cause one or more
certificates, for the same number of shares in the aggregate, to be issued to
such holder upon the surrender of the mutilated certificate.&#160; Any person claiming a certificate of stock to
be lost or destroyed shall make an affidavit or affirmation of that fact.&#160; The Board of Directors, in its sole
discretion, and subject to such terms and conditions as the Board of Directors
may determine, may issue new stock certificate or certificates in place of the
lost, mutilated, or destroyed certificate or certificates.</font></p>

<p style="margin:0pt 0pt 12.0pt;text-indent:36.0pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">45.&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160; <u>Registered
Stockholders</u>.&#160; The Corporation shall
be entitled to treat the holder of record of any share or shares as the holder
in fact thereof, and accordingly shall not be bound to recognize any equitable,
or other claim, or interest, in such shares on the part of any other person,
whether or not it shall have express or other notice thereof, save as expressly
provided by the laws of Maryland.</font></p>

<p align="center" style="font-family:Times New Roman;font-size:10.0pt;margin:0pt 0pt 12.0pt;text-align:center;"><b><u><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;font-weight:bold;">ARTICLE X</font></u>.</b></p>

<p align="center" style="font-family:Times New Roman;font-size:10.0pt;margin:0pt 0pt 12.0pt;text-align:center;"><u><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Sundry Provisions</font></u>.</p>

<p style="font-family:Times New Roman;font-size:10.0pt;margin:0pt 0pt 12.0pt;text-indent:36.0pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">46.&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160; </font><font size="1" style="font-size:6.5pt;position:relative;top:-3.0pt;">22</font><u>Notices</u>.&#160; Whenever under the provisions of these
By-Laws notice is required to be given to any director, officer or stockholder,
it shall not be construed to require personal notice, but such notice may be
given in writing, by mail, by depositing the same in the post office or
letterbox in a postage paid sealed wrapper, addressed to such director, officer
of stockholder at such address as appears on the books of the Corporation, or
in default of other address, to such director, officer, or stockholder at the
General Post Office in the City of Baltimore, Maryland, and such notice shall
be deemed to be given at the time when the same shall be thus mailed.&#160; Any director, officer or stockholder may
waive any notice required to be given under these By-Laws.</p>

officers who are not directors to such further extent as shall be authorized by
the Board of Directors and be consistent with law.&#160; The foregoing shall not limit the authority
of the Corporation to indemnify other employees and agents consistent with law.</font></p>

<p style="margin:0pt 0pt 12.0pt;text-indent:72.0pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">(b)&#160;&#160; The
provision of paragraph (a) shall apply to all proceedings arising (i) after the
time of adoption of this by-law amendment (the &#147;effective date&#148;) in connection
with any facts and circumstances occurring after the effective date; (ii) after
the effective date in connection with any facts or circumstances occurring
before the effective date; and (iii) prior to the effective date, whether
before or after July 1, 1981, to the extent necessary or appropriate to make
any indemnification provisions of the Corporation consistent with applicable
indemnification provisions of the General Laws of the State of Maryland.&#160; This by-law shall not limit any rights of any
person with respect to facts and circumstances occurring or proceedings arising
prior to the effective date to the extent such rights are consistent with law
applicable to the time in question.</font></p>

<p align="center" style="margin:0pt 0pt 12.0pt;text-align:center;"><b><font size="4" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:14.0pt;font-weight:bold;">McCORMICK

GORDON M. STETZ TO BECOME EXECUTIVE VP &amp; CFO</font></b></p>

<p style="margin:0pt 0pt 12.0pt;text-indent:36.0pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">SPARKS, MD, JULY 2
- --- Robert J. Lawless, Chairman and Chief Executive Officer of McCormick &amp;
Company, Inc., today announced the retirement of Francis A. Contino, Executive
Vice President - Strategic Planning and Chief Financial Officer, effective July&nbsp;1,
2008.&#160;&#160; Mr. Lawless also announced the
appointment of Gordon M. Stetz, who is currently Vice President - Finance for
the Company&#146;s European businesses, as Executive Vice President and Chief
Financial Officer, effective November 1, 2007.</font></p>

<p style="margin:0pt 0pt 12.0pt;text-indent:36.0pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Mr. Contino joined
McCormick in June of 1998 as Executive Vice President and Chief Financial
Officer and a member of the Corporate Board of Directors.&#160; Prior to joining McCormick, Mr. Contino was
Managing Partner of the Baltimore office of Ernst &amp; Young.&#160; Mr. Contino will retain his responsibilities
as Executive Vice President - Strategic Planning until his retirement at the
end of June 2008.&#160; He will work closely
with Mr. Stetz over the next several months to facilitate an orderly
transition.</font></p>

<p style="margin:0pt 0pt 12.0pt;text-indent:36.0pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Mr. Stetz joined
McCormick in 1987.&#160; Prior to his
appointment as chief financial officer for the Company&#146;s European businesses,
Mr. Stetz served in a number of leadership positions within the Company&#146;s
financial organization, including the role of chief financial officer of the
Company&#146;s largest business unit, the U.S. Consumer Products Division, as well
as other key roles in the Company&#146;s mergers and acquisitions and financial
planning activities.</font></p>

<p style="margin:0pt 0pt 12.0pt;text-indent:36.0pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Commented Bob
Lawless, &#147;Fran&#146;s vision regarding the implementation of SAP and his financial
leadership have been instrumental in moving the Company forward.&#160; He leaves a strong financial team and a
foundation for continued success.&#160; We are
grateful for his leadership and wish him well in his upcoming retirement.</font></p>

<p style="margin:0pt 0pt 12.0pt;text-indent:36.0pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Many factors
contribute to the success of our great Company, and one of them is an orderly
succession plan.&#160; This current
announcement is another step to ensure an orderly transition of leadership
responsibilities. Gordon has long been recognized as a leader and a strategic
thinker, and he has been a key contributor in the success of the Company&#146;s U.S.
Consumer and European businesses.&#160; He
will bring these same strengths and leadership to his new role.&#160; Gordon and Fran will work closely together
over the next several months to ensure a smooth transition and continued focus
on our strong financial success.&#148;</font></p>
