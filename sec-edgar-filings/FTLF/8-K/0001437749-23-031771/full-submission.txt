<p style="font-family:Times New Roman;font-size:10pt;font-variant:normal;margin:0pt;"><b><i>MRC Integration and Operational Improvements</i></b></p>

<p style="font-family:'Times New Roman';font-size:10pt;font-variant:normal;margin:0pt;">&nbsp;</p>

<p style="font-family:Times New Roman;font-size:10pt;font-variant:normal;margin:0pt;">The Company continues to focus on improving cash flow and profitability at Mimi&#8217;s Rock Corp (&#8220;MRC&#8221;). Since acquiring MRC on February 28, 2023, we have focused primarily on improving profitability through cost reductions. Specifically, we have eliminated over $1.5 million of annual non-advertising SG&amp;A expense (all numbers in this press release are reported in USD and not CAD).</p>

<p style="font-family:'Times New Roman';font-size:10pt;font-variant:normal;margin:0pt;">&nbsp;</p>

<p style="font-family:Times New Roman;font-size:10pt;font-variant:normal;margin:0pt;">Separately, we continue to evaluate the effectiveness of MRC&#8217;s advertising spend. As previously reported, during the second quarter of 2023 we reduced MRC&#8217;s advertising expense by 20% compared to the second quarter of 2022 with no material impact on revenue.</p>

<p style="font-family:'Times New Roman';font-size:10pt;font-variant:normal;margin:0pt;">&nbsp;</p>

<p style="font-family:Times New Roman;font-size:10pt;font-variant:normal;margin:0pt;">We experimented with further reductions during the third quarter of 2023, reducing advertising expense by approximately 30% compared to the same quarter in 2022. Although the additional reduction in advertising expenditure resulted in approximately 8% lower revenue, it increased contribution (defined as gross profit minus advertising expense) by approximately 15%. In other words, through these actions, the Company is increasing its profitability by eliminating unprofitable revenue.</p>

<p style="font-family:'Times New Roman';font-size:10pt;font-variant:normal;margin:0pt;">&nbsp;</p>

<p style="font-family:Times New Roman;font-size:10pt;font-variant:normal;margin:0pt;">As a result of our efforts, MRC advertising expense during the third quarter of 2023 was 16.6% of MRC revenue, compared to 21.9% during the same quarter in the prior year, a quarterly impact of over $0.5 million or an annualized impact of approximately $2.1 million. With these changes, MRC&#8217;s profitability has increased significantly since the acquisition, accounting for approximately 69% of the Company&#8217;s reported net income for the third quarter of 2023.</p>

<p style="font-family:Times New Roman;font-size:10pt;font-variant:normal;margin:0pt;"><b><i>Legacy FitLife Performance</i></b></p>

<p style="font-family:'Times New Roman';font-size:10pt;font-variant:normal;margin:0pt;">&nbsp;</p>

<p style="font-family:Times New Roman;font-size:10pt;font-variant:normal;margin:0pt;">During the third quarter of 2023, legacy FitLife revenue declined 19%, driven by a 27% decline in wholesale revenue partially offset by an 8% increase in online revenue.</p>

<p style="font-family:'Times New Roman';font-size:10pt;font-variant:normal;margin:0pt;">&nbsp;</p>

<p style="font-family:Times New Roman;font-size:10pt;font-variant:normal;margin:0pt;">Due to the size and timing of wholesale orders, wholesale revenue recognized by the Company during a quarter is not necessarily reflective of the retail demand for the Company&#8217;s products. During the quarter ended September 30, 2023, based on data shared by our wholesale partners, the retail movement of the Company&#8217;s products through wholesale channels declined approximately 14%. The Company is exploring new international wholesale opportunities for some of its legacy FitLife brands that are expected to at least partially offset the declines being experienced in the U.S.</p>

<p style="font-family:'Times New Roman';font-size:10pt;font-variant:normal;margin:0pt;">&nbsp;</p>

<p style="font-family:Times New Roman;font-size:10pt;font-variant:normal;margin:0pt;">While still positive, the growth rate for legacy FitLife&#8217;s online revenue declined over the first eight months of the year. The declines were driven primarily by a combination of fewer consumer sessions as well as underperformance of certain brands and products. Although session counts have declined, indicating lower traffic on our online listings, conversion rates have generally increased during the year, partially offsetting the decline in sessions.</p>

<p style="font-family:'Times New Roman';font-size:10pt;font-variant:normal;margin:0pt;">&nbsp;</p>

<p style="font-family:Times New Roman;font-size:10pt;font-variant:normal;margin:0pt;">The year-over-year growth rate was lowest during August before improving sequentially in September and again in October. Thus far in November, the year-over-year growth rate has continued to show sequential improvement. During the third quarter of 2023, subscriptions accounted for approximately 23% of legacy FitLife&#8217;s online sales volumes.</p>

<p style="font-family:Times New Roman;font-size:10pt;font-variant:normal;margin:0pt;"><b><i>Acquisition of MusclePharm Assets</i></b></p>

<p style="font-family:'Times New Roman';font-size:10pt;font-variant:normal;margin:0pt;">&nbsp;</p>

<p style="font-family:Times New Roman;font-size:10pt;font-variant:normal;margin:0pt;">As previously reported, the Company successfully closed the acquisition of substantially all of the assets of MusclePharm Corporation (&#8220;MusclePharm&#8221;), which primarily consisted of intangible assets. None of the liabilities of MusclePharm were assumed other than&nbsp;<i>de</i>&nbsp;<i>minimus</i>&nbsp;cure costs relating to certain assumed contracts.&nbsp; Total consideration for the acquisition was approximately&nbsp;$18.5 million&nbsp;cash.&nbsp; Of this amount,&nbsp;$10.0 million&nbsp;was funded using proceeds from a new term loan provided by&nbsp;First Citizens Bank, with the remainder funded from FitLife&#8217;s available cash balances.</p>

<p style="font-family:'Times New Roman';font-size:10pt;font-variant:normal;margin:0pt;">&nbsp;</p>

<p style="font-family:Times New Roman;font-size:10pt;font-variant:normal;margin:0pt;">Since closing the acquisition, management has begun engaging in discussions with several of MusclePharm&#8217;s previous customers in an effort to restore wholesale distribution of the brand. The Company is also in the process of re-launching some historically successful MusclePharm products.</p>

<p style="font-family:'Times New Roman';font-size:10pt;font-variant:normal;margin:0pt;">&nbsp;</p>

<p style="font-family:Times New Roman;font-size:10pt;font-variant:normal;margin:0pt;">Online sales of MusclePharm products are expected to be limited in the fourth quarter while third-party resellers deplete their inventory. As these resellers run out of product, the Company will become the primary seller of MusclePharm products online. We expect online revenue from MusclePharm products to exceed $5 million in 2024.</p>

<p style="font-family:'Times New Roman';font-size:10pt;font-variant:normal;margin:0pt;">&nbsp;</p>

<p style="font-family:Times New Roman;font-size:10pt;font-variant:normal;margin:0pt;">Dayton Judd, FitLife&#8217;s Chairman and CEO, commented,&nbsp;&#8220;Although the third and fourth quarters of each year are traditionally the slowest for the Company, I am pleased with our overall performance. While recent legacy FitLife performance has not met expectations, the weakness has been largely offset by strong financial performance at MRC. Going forward, I am optimistic that legacy FitLife performance will improve, and I am excited about the continued improvements at MRC as well as the potential of the MusclePharm brand to create additional stockholder value.&#8221;</p>
