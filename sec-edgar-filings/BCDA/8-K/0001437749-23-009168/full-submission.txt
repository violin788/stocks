<p style="font-family:Times New Roman;font-size:10pt;font-variant:normal;text-align:center;margin:0pt;"><b>BioCardia Announces Issuance of Two Patents Related to Technology That Guides Interventional Therapies</b></p>

<p style="font-family:'Times New Roman';font-size:10pt;font-variant:normal;margin:0pt;">&nbsp;</p>

<p style="font-family:Times New Roman;font-size:10pt;font-variant:normal;margin:0pt;"><b>SUNNYVALE, Calif.</b>&nbsp;&#8211;&nbsp;<b>April 4, 2023</b> &#8211; BioCardia<u>&#174;</u><u>, Inc.</u>&nbsp;[Nasdaq: BCDA], a developer of cellular and cell-derived therapeutics for the treatment of cardiovascular and pulmonary diseases, today announced the issuance of two patent grants related to enabling technologies for delivery of its investigational autologous and allogeneic cell therapies.</p>

<p style="font-family:'Times New Roman';font-size:10pt;font-variant:normal;margin:0pt;">&nbsp;</p>

<p style="font-family:Times New Roman;font-size:10pt;font-variant:normal;margin:0pt;">The United States Patent Office issued BioCardia Patent Number 11,716,859, entitled &#8220;Multi-Directional Steerable Catheter,&#8221; with a patent term that will expire in 2035. The patent claims a fundamental design for steerable introducer sheaths, such as those used for BioCardia&#8217;s autologous and allogeneic cell therapy procedures, and for transseptal procedures for the treatment of cardiac arrhythmias. The design enables the tensioning elements in the catheter to rotate around the catheter shaft, allowing consistent catheter performance in any direction. This design is intended to enable smooth navigation and prevent &#8220;whip,&#8221; when a catheter in the heart suddenly jumps from one orientation to another due to the build-up of mechanical forces in the device. This patented design is incorporated in the Company&#8217;s FDA-cleared Morph DNA&#8482; product, a 5 French sheath equivalent, and in the Company&#8217;s FDA-cleared Avance&#8482; product, an 8.5 French introducer sheath indicated for transseptal procedures.</p>

<p style="font-family:'Times New Roman';font-size:10pt;font-variant:normal;margin:0pt;">&nbsp;</p>

<p style="font-family:Times New Roman;font-size:10pt;font-variant:normal;margin:0pt;">The Indian Patent Office granted the Company Patent Number 424579, entitled &#8220;Steerable Endoluminal Devices and Methods for Use,&#8221; with a patent term that will expire in late 2031.&nbsp;&nbsp;The patent claims a fundamental design for steerable introducer sheaths. The design is for a coil with a braid disposed coaxially about the coil, all embedded within the wall of an introducer sheath. The coil enables a robust, kink-resistant design with enhanced column support, while the braid in the catheter shaft provides for excellent torque transmission. This patent design feature has demonstrated excellent performance in the Company&#8217;s Morph Access Pro&#8482; product family and has been used to treat approximately 10,000 patients to-date, ranging from a two-year-old girl to a 90-year-old man.</p>

<p style="font-family:'Times New Roman';font-size:10pt;font-variant:normal;margin:0pt;">&nbsp;</p>

<p style="font-family:Times New Roman;font-size:10pt;font-variant:normal;margin:0pt;">&#8220;This positive experience with the Morph DNA, Avance, and Morph AccessPro underlies our understanding of catheter navigation that informs the delivery of our higher-value biotherapeutic interventions, where we utilize steerable guide sheaths in every procedure,&#8221; said BioCardia CEO Peter Altman, PhD. &#8220;The acquisitions of Baylis by Boston Scientific for $1.5 billion and the acquisition of the Acutus sheath portfolio by Medtronic for $87 million last year were focused on enabling transseptal access devices, like these, that enable ablation therapies to treat cardiac arrhythmias. These acquisitions show that these steerable catheter assets are nontrivial to develop and that the intellectual property that underlies these assets has the potential to enable large market opportunities and be quite valuable.&#8221;</p>
