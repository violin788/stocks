<p style="font-family:Times New Roman;font-size:10pt;font-variant:normal;text-align:center;margin:0pt;"><b>BioCardia Announces First Patient Treated in CardiAMP Cell Therapy Trial for Chronic Myocardial Ischemia</b></p>

<p style="font-family:'Times New Roman';font-size:10pt;font-variant:normal;margin:0pt;">&nbsp;</p>

<p style="font-family:'Times New Roman';font-size:10pt;font-variant:normal;margin:0pt;">&nbsp;</p>

<p style="font-family:Times New Roman;font-size:10pt;font-variant:normal;margin:0pt;"><b>SAN CARLOS, Calif.</b>&nbsp;&#8211;<b> October 21, 2021 </b>&#8211;<b> </b><u>BioCardia<sup style="vertical-align:top;line-height:120%;font-size:pt">&#174;</sup><u>, Inc</u>.</u>&nbsp;[Nasdaq: BCDA] a company focused on developing cellular and cell derived therapeutics for the treatment of cardiovascular and pulmonary diseases,&nbsp;today announced the treatment of the first patient in its Phase III pivotal CardiAMP Cell Therapy Chronic Myocardial Ischemia Trial for patients with no option chronic myocardial ischemia with refractory angina. These patients experience frequent angina (chest pain) attacks that are uncontrolled by optimal drug therapy but are not suitable candidates for stent placement or bypass surgery, leaving them with no therapeutic options.</p>

<p style="font-family:'Times New Roman';font-size:10pt;font-variant:normal;margin:0pt;">&nbsp;</p>

<p style="font-family:Times New Roman;font-size:10pt;font-variant:normal;margin:0pt;">The first patient was treated with the investigational CardiAMP autologous cell therapy at the University of Wisconsin in Madison, by Interventional Cardiologist and Associate Professor of Medicine, Dr. Amish Raval and supported by Dr. Peiman Hematti, Bone Marrow Transplantation Hematologist and Professor of Medicine.</p>

<p style="font-family:'Times New Roman';font-size:10pt;font-variant:normal;margin:0pt;">&nbsp;</p>

<p style="font-family:Times New Roman;font-size:10pt;font-variant:normal;margin:0pt;">The CardiAMP Cell Therapy Chronic Myocardial Ischemia Trial is expected to enroll up to 343 patients at up to 40 centers. The primary endpoint will evaluate improvement in exercise tolerance at six months following the study procedure. The trial is intended to have an adaptive statistical analysis plan with an initial assessment for efficacy when 100 patients reach their primary endpoint, although aspects of the statistical analysis plan remain the subject of study considerations with the FDA. The co-National Principal Investigators for the trial are Thomas Povsic, M.D. of Duke University, Durham, NC, and Timothy Henry, M.D. of The Christ Hospital, Cincinnati, OH.</p>

<p style="font-family:'Times New Roman';font-size:10pt;font-variant:normal;margin:0pt;">&nbsp;</p>

<p style="font-family:Times New Roman;font-size:10pt;font-variant:normal;margin:0pt;">CardiAMP Cell Therapy uses a patient&#8217;s own (autologous) bone marrow cells delivered to the heart in a minimally-invasive, catheter-based procedure to potentially stimulate the body&#8217;s natural healing response. The therapy incorporates a pre-procedural screening assay to identify those patients who are likely responders to the therapy, a first for a cardiac cell therapy. Patients eligible&nbsp;to undergo the cell therapy procedure receive cells delivered with a system that has been shown in published literature to present the lowest risk to patients for biotherapeutic delivery<font style="font-family:Times New Roman;font-size:8pt;"><sup style="vertical-align:top;line-height:120%;font-size:pt">1</sup></font> and to be three to six times more efficient at delivering cells to the heart muscle than other methods<font style="font-family:Times New Roman;font-size:8pt;"><sup style="vertical-align:top;line-height:120%;font-size:pt">2</sup></font>. This approach allows the patient to be discharged from the hospital the morning after the study procedure.</p>

<p style="font-family:Times New Roman;font-size:10pt;font-variant:normal;margin:0pt;"><b>About Chronic Myocardial Ischemia with Refractory Angina</b></p>

<p style="font-family:'Times New Roman';font-size:10pt;font-variant:normal;margin:0pt;">&nbsp;</p>

<p style="font-family:Times New Roman;font-size:10pt;font-variant:normal;margin:0pt;">Despite improvements in revascularization techniques, there is a growing population of patients with chronic angina who have severely limiting symptoms but are not candidates for further revascularization. Recent studies suggest that five to 15 percent of patients undergoing cardiac catheterization have significant disease that cannot be treated with revascularization.<font style="font-family:Times New Roman;font-size:8pt;"><sup style="vertical-align:top;line-height:120%;font-size:pt">3,4</sup></font> It is estimated that between 600,000 and 1.8 million U.S. patients suffer from refractory angina, with approximately 75,000 new cases diagnosed each year.<font style="font-family:Times New Roman;font-size:8pt;"><sup style="vertical-align:top;line-height:120%;font-size:pt">5</sup></font></p>

<p style="font-family:Times New Roman;font-size:10pt;font-variant:normal;margin:0pt;">These patients experience poor perceived health status and psychological distress<sup style="vertical-align:top;line-height:120%;font-size:pt">6</sup> have significant impairments in quality of life,<font style="font-family:Times New Roman;font-size:8pt;"><sup style="vertical-align:top;line-height:120%;font-size:pt">7</sup></font> and represent a financial burden to the healthcare system.<font style="font-family:Times New Roman;font-size:8pt;"><sup style="vertical-align:top;line-height:120%;font-size:pt">8</sup></font> Ranolazine and enhanced external counter pulsation are the only contemporary therapies developed for the treatment of this condition; however, recent studies have highlighted the limitations of Ranolazine,<font style="font-family:Times New Roman;font-size:8pt;"><sup style="vertical-align:top;line-height:120%;font-size:pt">9</sup></font> while enhanced counter pulsation has been associated with minimal reduction in angina.<font style="font-family:Times New Roman;font-size:8pt;"><sup style="vertical-align:top;line-height:120%;font-size:pt">10</sup></font></p>

<p style="font-family:Times New Roman;font-size:10pt;font-variant:normal;margin:0pt;"><b>About BioCardia</b><sup style="vertical-align:top;line-height:120%;font-size:pt">&#174;</sup></p>

<p style="font-family:'Times New Roman';font-size:10pt;font-variant:normal;margin:0pt;">&nbsp;</p>

<p style="font-family:Times New Roman;font-size:10pt;font-variant:normal;margin:0pt;"><u>BioCardia, Inc.</u>, headquartered in San Carlos, California, is a developer of two biotherapeutic platforms &#8211; the CardiAMP&nbsp;autologous bone marrow derived mononuclear cell therapy for cardiovascular indications, and the NK1R+ allogenic bone marrow derived mesenchymal stem cell therapies for cardiovascular and pulmonary diseases.&nbsp; These platforms underly four product candidates, each with the potential to meaningfully benefit millions of patients. Three of these investigational therapies are enabled by the Company&#8217;s proprietary biotherapeutic delivery platforms, which the Company also selectively licenses to other biotherapeutic development firms.</p>
