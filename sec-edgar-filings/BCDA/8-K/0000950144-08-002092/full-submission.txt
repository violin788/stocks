<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 12pt"><B>Item&nbsp;1.01. Entry into a Material Definitive Agreement.</B>
</DIV>


<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On December&nbsp;19, 2007, clickNsettle.com, Inc. (the &#147;Company&#148;) entered into a Stock Purchase
Agreement, as amended January&nbsp;31, 2008 (the &#147;Purchase Agreement&#148;), with the purchasers identified
on Exhibit&nbsp;A to the Purchase Agreement, a small group of investors led by Dr.&nbsp;Phillip Frost (the
&#147;Purchasers&#148;). Pursuant to the terms of the Purchase Agreement, the Company agreed to sell to the
Purchasers an aggregate number of shares equal to 51% of the post-reverse stock split outstanding
shares of the common stock of the Company on a fully diluted basis for a purchase price equal to
approximately the net cash and cash equivalents of the Company on the closing date of the
transaction after deducting any and all liabilities existing as of the closing date of the
transaction, including the costs and expenses of the transaction.
</DIV>

<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Purchase Agreement required the Company to seek stockholder approval to (i)&nbsp;conduct a
one-for-ten reverse stock split and (ii)&nbsp;amend its Certificate of Incorporation to increase the
aggregate number of shares of all classes of capital stock that the Company may issue from
305,000,000 to 800,000,000, consisting of 750,000,000 shares of common stock, par value $0.001 per
share, and 50,000,000 shares of preferred stock, par value $0.001 per share. The Board of Directors
of the Company approved the foregoing actions on December&nbsp;19, 2007 and stockholders owning a
majority of the Company&#146;s shares approved the foregoing actions on January&nbsp;25, 2008.
</DIV>

<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On March&nbsp;13, 2008, the one-for-ten reverse stock split was effected and the increase in the
number of authorized shares became effective upon the filing of the Company&#146;s Amended and Restated
Certificate of Incorporation with the Delaware Secretary of State. As a result of the reverse stock
split, as of March&nbsp;13, 2008, the trading symbol on the OTCBB for the Company&#146;s post-reverse stock
split common stock, par value $0.001 per share (the &#147;New Common Stock&#148;), changed to &#147;CKST&#148; and the
CUSIP number for the New Common Stock changed to 18682E304. Stockholders of record on March&nbsp;13,
2008 will receive a transmittal letter from the Company&#146;s transfer agent indicating how they can
exchange their current pre-reverse stock split share certificates for new post-reverse stock split
share certificates.
</DIV>

<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The descriptions of the Purchase Agreement in this report are summaries that do not purport to
be complete and are qualified in their entirety by reference to the Purchase Agreement which is
filed as an exhibit hereto and incorporated herein by this reference.
</DIV>
<!-- link2 "Item&nbsp;3.02. Unregistered Sales of Equity Securities" -->

<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 12pt"><B>Item&nbsp;3.02. Unregistered Sales of Equity Securities</B>
</DIV>


<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The information disclosed under Item&nbsp;1.01 of this report is incorporated by reference into
this Item&nbsp;3.02.
</DIV>

<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On September&nbsp;26, 2007, the Company sold 44,921,052 shares of its pre-reverse stock split
common stock, par value $0.001 per share (the &#147;Old Shares&#148;), to investors led by Glenn L. Halpryn
of Miami, Florida, and Steven Jerry Glauser of Denver, Colorado (the &#147;Investors&#148;) for an aggregate
offering price of $1,567,000.
</DIV>

<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On March&nbsp;18, 2008, pursuant to the Purchase Agreement, the Company sold 5,762,448 shares of
New Common Stock (the &#147;New Shares&#148;) to the Purchasers for an aggregate offering price of
$1,338,100. At the time of their sale, the New Shares represented 51% of the outstanding shares
of New Common Stock on a fully diluted basis.
</DIV>

<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;There were no underwriting discounts or commissions for either sale.
</DIV>

<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Both the Old Shares and the New Shares were issued pursuant to the private placement exemption
provided by Section&nbsp;4(2) of the Securities Act of 1933 (the &#147;1933 Act&#148;). The Old Shares and the New
Shares are deemed to be &#147;restricted securities&#148; as defined in Rule&nbsp;144 under the 1933 Act and the
certificates evidencing the Old Shares and the New Shares bear a legend stating the restrictions on
resale. Each purchaser of these restricted securities is an &#147;accredited investor&#148; as that term is
defined in Rule 501(a) of Regulation&nbsp;D of the 1933 Act.
</DIV>

<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Company has not made any other unregistered sales of equity securities during the past
three years.
</DIV>

<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 12pt"><B>clickNsettle.com, Inc. Closes Sale of 51% of the Company to Investor Group Led by Dr.&nbsp;Phillip Frost</B>
</DIV>

<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">MIAMI, FL &#151; March&nbsp;18, 2008 &#151; clickNsettle.com, Inc. (CKST.OTC.BB) today announced that it
closed a Stock Purchase Agreement with a small group of investors, led by Dr.&nbsp;Phillip Frost.
</DIV>

<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">In accordance with the agreement, on March&nbsp;13, 2008, clickNsettle.com, Inc. implemented a
one-for-ten reverse stock split and amended its Certificate of Incorporation to increase the aggregate
number of shares of all classes of capital stock that it may issue. As a result of the reverse
stock split, as of March&nbsp;13, 2008, the trading symbol for the Company&#146;s shares on the
Over-the-Counter Bulletin Board (OTCBB)&nbsp;changed to &#147;CKST&#148; and the CUSIP number for shares changed
to 18682E304. Stockholders of record on March&nbsp;13, 2008 will receive a transmittal letter from the
Company&#146;s transfer agent indicating how they can exchange their current pre-reverse stock split
share certificates for new post-reverse stock split share certificates.
</DIV>

<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">Pursuant to the agreement, the Company sold to the investor group an aggregate of 5,762,448
post-reverse split unregistered shares, or approximately 51% of the post-reverse split outstanding
shares on a fully-diluted basis. The aggregate purchase price for the
shares was $1,338,100, an
amount equal to approximately net cash and cash equivalents of the Company on the closing date
after deducting liabilities and costs, including costs and expenses of this transaction.
</DIV>

<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">Dr.&nbsp;Frost is the Chairman and Chief Executive Officer of Opko Health, Inc., a specialty healthcare
company focused on the treatment, diagnosis and prevention of ophthalmic diseases. Previously, Dr.
Frost founded IVAX Corporation and served as its Chairman of the Board of Directors and Chief
Executive Officer from 1987 until its sale to Teva Pharmaceuticals Industries Ltd., in January
2006. Dr.&nbsp;Frost has served as Vice Chairman of the Board of Directors of Teva since the completion
of the acquisition of IVAX.
</DIV>

<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt"><U>About clickNsettle.com, Inc.</U>:
</DIV>

<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">clickNsettle.com, Inc. is a &#147;shell company&#148; with no operating assets or business. The Company
provided alternative dispute resolution services until the sale of its operating business in 2005.
Since 2005 the Company has not had an operating business. The Company&#146;s plan of business is to
identify and evaluate a merger or other business combination with an operating company. No
assurance can be given as to when or if the Company will be successful in doing so.
</DIV>
