 <p align="justify">MARTINSVILLE, Va., June  08, 2023  (GLOBE NEWSWIRE) -- Hooker Furnishings Corporation (NASDAQ-GS: HOFT), a global leader in the design, production, and marketing of home furnishings for nearly a century, today reported operating results for its fiscal 2024 first quarter ended April 30, 2023.<br ></p>  <p align="justify"><strong>Fiscal 2024 First Quarter overview:</strong></p>  <ul><li style="text-align:justify;"><strong>Consolidated net sales for the quarter were $121.8 million, a $25.5 million, or 17.3%, decrease compared to a year ago. An expected revenue reduction was driven by a 32.5% sales decrease in the Home Meridian (HMI) segment and, to a lesser extent, a 14.8% decrease in first quarter year-over-year Domestic Upholstery sales. Net sales in the Hooker Branded segment remained relatively unchanged.</strong></li></ul>  <ul><li style="text-align:justify;"><strong>Consolidated net income was $1.5 million, or $0.13 per diluted share for the quarter, compared to $3.2 million, or $0.26 per diluted share, in the prior year period. Consolidated operating income for the quarter was $2 million compared to $3.9 million in the same quarter a year ago.</strong></li></ul>  <ul><li style="text-align:justify;"><strong>The Company strengthened its financial position during the quarter by generating $22.4 million in cash from operating activities and funded $4.5 million in capital expenditures and the continued development of our ERP system, $4.3 million in share repurchases, and $2.4 million in cash dividends.</strong></li></ul>  <ul><li style="text-align:justify;"><strong>Inventory levels decreased by $23 million during the quarter, well on the way toward a goal of reducing inventories by $30 million before fiscal year-end.</strong></li></ul>  <ul><li style="text-align:justify;"><strong>The Hooker Branded and Domestic Upholstery segments and All Other were profitable for the quarter. Home Meridian&#8217;s operating loss of $2.1 million, resulting from lower sales volume, improved by $1 million compared to the same period a year ago.</strong></li></ul>  <ul><li style="text-align:justify;"><strong>The grand opening of a nearly 120,000-square-foot new Hooker Legacy Showroom in the Showplace Building at the Spring High Point Market generated a 93% increase in year-over-year retailer traffic, with successful product launches across all brands.</strong><br ></li></ul>  <p align="justify"><strong><u>Management Commentary</u></strong></p>  <p align="justify">&#8220;Considering the softer retail environment, economic uncertainties and our recent exit from the Accentrics Home (ACH) line, we&#8217;re pleased to have exceeded internal and external expectations for sales and earnings this quarter,&#8221; said Jeremy Hoff, chief executive officer and director of Hooker Furnishings. &#8220;Our liquidation of ACH inventories and other obsolete inventories at HMI is about 80% complete as of May end, which is helping us reduce our domestic warehousing footprint and make progress towards getting profitability back on track at HMI. We generated $22.4 million in cash during the quarter, and we are continuing to build cash currently as we further reduce inventories.&#160;Recent cash levels have increased by&#160;about $15 million since the end of our first quarter.&#8221;</p>  <p align="justify">&#8220;Our new Hooker Legacy Showroom Grand Opening at the April High Point Market achieved what we intended, as we nearly doubled our attendance from a year ago and attracted new customers,&#8221; Hoff said. &#8220;Many of our strategic organic growth initiatives that will enable us to broaden our total addressable market and visibility are tied to the new showroom and the Hooker Legacy Brands,&#8221; he added. One of those strategic growth initiatives, the High Point Market launch of the &#8216;M&#8217; domestically produced upholstery and imported occasional furniture brand, &#8220;surpassed our expectations,&#8221; Hoff said
