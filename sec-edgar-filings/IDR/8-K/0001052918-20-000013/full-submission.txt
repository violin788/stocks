<P align=center style='font:18pt Times New Roman;margin:0'><B>New Jersey Mining Company Releases President&#8217;s Letter to Shareholders</B></P>
<P style='font:11pt Times New Roman;margin:0'>&nbsp;</P>
<P align=justify style='font:11pt Times New Roman;margin:0'>COEUR D'ALENE, Idaho, January 13, 2020 (ACCESSWIRE) -- New Jersey Mining Company (CSE/OTCQB: NJMC) (&#8220;NJMC&#8221; or the &#8220;Company&#8221;) today distributed the following Letter to Shareholders from NJMC President &amp; CEO John Swallow:</P>
<P align=justify style='font:11pt Times New Roman;margin:0'>&nbsp;</P>
<P align=justify style='font:11pt Times New Roman;margin:0'>Amongst the many (self-prescribed) pearls of wisdom contained within my iPhone journal, I came across an appropriate quote to introduce this years&#8217; high-level stakeholder discussion. As Arthur Shopenhauer (fittingly) once said, &#8220;The task is not so much to see what no one has yet seen, but to think what nobody yet has thought about that which everybody sees&#8221;. This quote describes our approach to business as well as the seemingly overlooked influence of society&#8217;s generational turnings toward more passive portfolio management.</P>
<P align=justify style='font:11pt Times New Roman;margin:0'>&nbsp;</P>
<P align=justify style='font:11pt Times New Roman;margin:0'>As the curtain rises on the new year we admirably reflect on the fact that it was considerably more difficult to get to where we are than to get to where we are going. In this new era of the virtual marketplace and fleeting brand loyalty, it seems apropos that NJMC supplies a commodity that has been in demand for over 2,000 years. It is our continued belief that the subtle trend toward $1,500/oz gold is telegraphing that the next chapters of the economy are being drafted while few recognize that some of its authors are already producing content. </P>
<P align=justify style='font:11pt Times New Roman;margin:0'>&nbsp;</P>
<P align=justify style='font:11pt Times New Roman;margin:0'>As a local Idaho company, we believe strongly in our boots on the ground management style and community-based business approach. With the location of our mine on a year-round Idaho highway and the mill just off Interstate 90, we enjoy a greatly understated jurisdictional and geographical advantage not frequently encountered within the marketplace. We believe the potential rewards from a global increase in the demand for gold will gravitate toward those firms pre-positioned to where the market is going and not already affected by someone else&#8217;s exit strategy. Furthermore, we recognize the clock is always ticking for those without a source of non-dilutive cash flow and/or permitting in more remote and capital-intensive locations. Our specific set of skills and geologic endowment has allowed us to stay under the radar while consolidating the under-developed and forgotten (Murray) Gold Belt portion of the world-class Coeur d&#8217;Alene Mining District.</P>
<P align=justify style='font:11pt Times New Roman;margin:0'>&nbsp;</P>
<P align=justify style='font:11pt Times New Roman;margin:0'>Being somewhat agnostic to industry cycles, much of our prior focus was on base building, balance sheet improvement and becoming a producer &#8211;&nbsp;which proved prescient as gold prices moved higher and the potential for deep-rooted mineralization at the Golden Chest began to quietly draw outside attention. Last year we invested in newer equipment and overall operating processes which resulted in gradually lowering costs per ounce, increased mine production and record throughput at the mill. From modeling to exploration and from mining to milling, our focus was toward moderate growth and a February-inspired across the board goal of &#8220;getting better at what we already do&#8221;. </P>
<HR style='border:0;height:0;width:0;margin:14pt 0 0 0'><P align=center style='font:10pt Times New Roman;margin:0'><FONT style=font-size:10pt>New Jersey Mining Company &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</FONT><FONT style='font:10pt Wingdings'>&#159;</FONT><FONT style=font-size:10pt> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;201 N. 3<SUP>rd</SUP> Street &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</FONT><FONT style='font:10pt Wingdings'>&#159;</FONT><FONT style=font-size:10pt> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Coeur d&#8217;Alene, Idaho 83814</FONT></P>
<HR style='page-break-after:always;border:0;height:3pt;background-color:#909090;margin:8pt 0'><P style=line-height:0;margin:0></P>
<P align=justify style='font:11pt Times New Roman;margin:0'>In regard to production-based exploration, most of our efforts focused on the upcoming open pit expansion and future underground development. Within the MGB, as we improve the quality of our overall district base, energy has naturally moved away from lesser land packages &#8211;&nbsp;thus the Crown Point option will not be part of our portfolio in the new year. The combination of its ownership structure, limited potential and permitting delays ran contrary to our unofficial rule and initial justification for considering the project. Our holdings in that area are reflected in the wholly owned Potosi patented and un-patented land package that lies adjacent to and on-strike to the south. From both a macro and micro level, continued evaluation of strategic mining and/or milling opportunities is ongoing.</P>
<P align=justify style='font:11pt Times New Roman;margin:0'>&nbsp;</P>
<P align=justify style='font:11pt Times New Roman;margin:0'>In conclusion, as Henry Ford once said, &#8220;<FONT style=color:#222222>You can't build a reputation on what you are going to do&#8221;, thus said, it is fairly obvious that we feel </FONT>the experience gained from being an Idaho Company and becoming proficient in &#8220;just about everything related to gold production&#8221; has resulted in a defined advantage for our company. And while the potential for &#8220;movement&#8221; relating to our Montana and central Idaho holdings exists, the subject of this particular commentary is closer to home. To set the stage for the year ahead we are providing a high-level bullet-point synopsis relating to our (largely) proprietary district base &#8211; a direct reflection of our discussions and beliefs &#8211; in an effort to share with our stakeholders &#8220;why we like it&#8221;.</P>
<P align=justify style='font:11pt Times New Roman;margin:0'>&nbsp;</P>
<P align=justify style='font:11pt Times New Roman;margin:0'>Sincerely,</P>
<P align=justify style='font:11pt Times New Roman;margin:0'>John Swallow</P>
<P align=justify style='font:11pt Times New Roman;margin:0'>&nbsp;</P>
<P align=justify style='font:11pt Times New Roman;margin:0'><B>Murray Gold Belt (MGB)</B></P>
<P align=justify style='font:11pt Times New Roman;margin:0'>&nbsp;</P>
<P align=justify style='font:11pt Times New Roman;margin:0;margin-left:36pt'><KBD style='position:absolute;font:11pt Times New Roman;margin-left:-18pt'>&#45;</KBD>The world&#8217;s great mining districts are zoned (gold zone, silver zone, copper zone, etc.), with the MGB being the gold zone related to the silver zone of the world-class Coeur d&#8217;Alene Mining District (CMD).&nbsp;</P>
<P align=justify style='font:11pt Times New Roman;margin:0;margin-left:36pt'><KBD style='position:absolute;font:11pt Times New Roman;margin-left:-18pt'>&#45;</KBD>Mines of the CMD and the MGB can be classified as orogenic mineral deposits.&nbsp;</P>
<P align=justify style='font:11pt Times New Roman;margin:0;margin-left:36pt'><KBD style='position:absolute;font:11pt Times New Roman;margin-left:-18pt'>&#45;</KBD>Orogenic deposits and their associated vein systems often set-up in mineralized shoots/localized areas of economic mineralization.&nbsp;</P>
<P align=justify style='font:11pt Times New Roman;margin:0;margin-left:36pt'><KBD style='position:absolute;font:11pt Times New Roman;margin-left:-18pt'>&#45;</KBD>Golden Chest vein system has six separate mineralized shoots along 1,200 meters of strike-length. Vertical limits of the six mineralized shoots have not been identified and all remain open at depth.&nbsp;</P>
<P align=justify style='font:11pt Times New Roman;margin:0;margin-left:36pt'><KBD style='position:absolute;font:11pt Times New Roman;margin-left:-18pt'>&#45;</KBD>A recent graduate thesis on the Golden Chest Mine indicates depth of mineralization to be from 3km to 10.5km below the ground surface.&nbsp;</P>
<P align=justify style='font:11pt Times New Roman;margin:0;margin-left:36pt'><KBD style='position:absolute;font:11pt Times New Roman;margin-left:-18pt'>&#45;</KBD>The MGB has numerous gold prospects and old mines, most of which have not received any sort of modern exploration. A number of these prospects share similar characteristics to the Golden Chest mine (a target-rich environment).&nbsp;</P>
<P align=justify style='font:11pt Times New Roman;margin:0'>&nbsp;</P>
<P align=justify style='font:11pt Times New Roman;margin:0'><B>By the Numbers</B></P>
<P align=justify style='font:11pt Times New Roman;margin:0'>&nbsp;</P>
<P align=justify style='font:11pt Times New Roman;margin:0;margin-left:36pt'><KBD style='position:absolute;font:11pt Times New Roman;margin-left:-18pt'>&#45;</KBD>Current mining (Skookum shoot and adjoining mineralization) is only 200 meters out of the 1,200 meters of overall strike.&nbsp;</P>
<P align=justify style='font:11pt Times New Roman;margin:0;margin-left:36pt'><KBD style='position:absolute;font:11pt Times New Roman;margin-left:-18pt'>&#45;</KBD>Deepest drilling (GC 12-121: 2.5 m (true thickness) assaying 4.35 ppm Au) at the Golden Chest intercepts the Idaho Fault around the 650 elevation, 260 meters below current underground production.&nbsp;</P>
<P align=justify style='font:11pt Times New Roman;margin:0;margin-left:36pt'><KBD style='position:absolute;font:11pt Times New Roman;margin-left:-18pt'>&#45;</KBD>Average true-thickness of gold mineralization from drill intercepts is 19.4 gram/meters.&#160; &nbsp;</P>
<P align=justify style='font:11pt Times New Roman;margin:0;margin-left:36pt'><KBD style='position:absolute;font:11pt Times New Roman;margin-left:-18pt'>&#45;</KBD>Conservative 1:2 strike-length to vertical-depth ratio, indicates the potential for 2,400 meters of vertical mineralization and the potential for the Golden Chest to host a multi-million-ounce deposit.&nbsp;</P>
