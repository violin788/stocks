(NYSE American: WWR) (&#8220;WWR&#8221; or &#8220;Westwater&#8221;) for $1.36 per share due to the Board of Directors (the &#8220;Board&#8221;) of Westwater&#8217;s continued unwillingness to constructively engage in discussions around a potential combination. The proposal to build a new domestic critical minerals company represented a share price premium of approximately 73% to the last unaffected trading day for both companies prior to the business combination proposal announcement on December 22, 2022.</p> <p style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: times new roman; MARGIN: 0px; text-align:justify;">&nbsp;</p> <p style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: times new roman; MARGIN: 0px 0px 0px 0in; text-align:justify;">&#8220;We are disappointed that the Westwater Board has refused to even discuss the potential combination of our two companies and ignored our proposal that would deliver immediate and long-term value to all Westwater shareholders,&#8221; said John Swallow, Idaho Strategic President and CEO. &#8220;Instead of engaging in constructive dialogue, the Westwater Board let this unique and value-generative opportunity to potentially create a company with a clean share structure, a low float, high insider ownership, a significant cash position and several tier 1 critical minerals projects within the United States go unexplored.&#8221;</p> <p style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: times new roman; MARGIN: 0px; text-align:justify;">&nbsp;</p> <p style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: times new roman; MARGIN: 0px 0px 0px 0in; text-align:justify;">Mr. Swallow continued, &#8220;After careful assessment with our advisors, we determined that given the lack of meaningful engagement from Westwater, Idaho Strategic&#8217;s best path forward at this time is to execute on our own &#8216;production-based&#8217; strategy while delivering long-term value for our stakeholders. Our gold operations are hitting stride and the dynamic nature of the REE industry seems to be changing almost daily. With our positioning in the REE industry and actual mining and development experience, our ability to drive results even through periods of increased inflation and global uncertainty, in addition to providing exposure to a decarbonized future is relevant now more than ever. With over 11,000 acres of Rare Earth Element landholdings, the Company is the second largest REE landholder in the U.S. The Company&#8217;s Diamond Creek and Roberts REE properties are included the U.S. national REE inventory as listed in USGS, IGS and DOE publications. IDR&#8217;s Lemhi Pass Thorium-REE Project is recognized by the USGS and IGS as containing the largest concentration of thorium resources in the country. With over 7,000 acres of patented and unpatented land, the Company has the largest private land position in the area following its consolidation of the Murray Gold Belt for the first time in over 100 years.</p> <p style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: times new roman; MARGIN: 0px; text-align:justify;">&nbsp;</p> <p style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: times new roman; MARGIN: 0px 0px 0px 0in; text-align:justify;">With an impressive mix of mining and business experience, IDR maintains a long-standing &#8220;We Live Here&#8221; approach to corporate culture, land management, and historic preservation.
