<div style="font-family: &quot;Times New Roman&quot;; font-size: 10pt; font-variant: normal; margin: 0pt; text-align: justify;"><i><span style="text-decoration: underline;">Clinical Trial and REQORSA Manufacturing Updates</span></i></div>

<div style="font-family: &quot;Times New Roman&quot;; font-size: 10pt; font-variant: normal; margin: 0pt; text-align: justify;">&#xa0;</div>

<div style="font-family: &quot;Times New Roman&quot;; font-size: 10pt; font-variant: normal; margin: 0pt; text-align: justify;"><i>Acclaim-1 </i></div>

<div style="font-family: &quot;Times New Roman&quot;; font-size: 10pt; font-variant: normal; margin: 0pt; text-indent: 0pt; text-align: justify;">&#xa0;</div>

<div style="font-family: &quot;Times New Roman&quot;; font-size: 10pt; font-variant: normal; margin: 0pt; text-indent: 0pt; text-align: justify;">Acclaim-1 is an open-label, multi-center Phase 1/2 clinical trial evaluating REQORSA<sup style="vertical-align:top;line-height:120%;">&#174;</sup><sup style="vertical-align:top;line-height:120%;"> </sup>Immunogene Therapy (generic name: quaratusugene ozeplasmid), the Company&#8217;s lead oncology drug candidate, in combination with AstraZeneca&#8217;s Tagrisso<sup style="vertical-align:top;line-height:120%;">&#174;</sup> in patients with late-stage Non-Small Cell Lung Cancer (&#8220;NSCLC&#8221;) that has activating epidermal growth factor receptor (&#8220;EGFR&#8221;) mutations and progression after treatment with Tagrisso.&#xa0; The Acclaim-1 study has three portions - a Phase 1 dose escalation portion, a Phase 2 expansion portion, and a Phase 2 randomized portion. On May 25, 2023, the Safety Review Committee (&#8220;SRC&#8221;) for the Acclaim-1 clinical trial approved advancement to the Phase 2 expansion portion of the trial following the completion of the Phase 1 portion of the trial. Based on full safety data, the SRC determined that the recommended Phase 2 dose of REQORSA will be 0.12 mg/kg. This was the highest dose level delivered in the Phase 1 portion and is twice the highest dose level delivered in the Company&#8217;s prior clinical trial combining REQORSA with Tarceva<sup style="vertical-align:top;line-height:120%;">&#174;</sup> for the treatment of late-stage lung cancer. We are ready to begin the Phase 2 expansion portion of the trial. We expect we will dose the first patient in the third quarter of 2023 and are awaiting patient dosing due in part to additional time required in connection with our transition to a new lipid nanoparticle (&#8220;LNP&#8221;) third-party contract development and manufacturing organization (&#8220;LNP CDMO&#8221;) and manufacture of final drug product.</div>

<div style="font-family: &quot;Times New Roman&quot;; font-size: 10pt; font-variant: normal; margin: 0pt; text-indent: 36pt; text-align: justify;">&#xa0;</div>

<div style="font-family: &quot;Times New Roman&quot;; font-size: 10pt; font-variant: normal; margin: 0pt; text-indent: 0pt; text-align: justify;"><i>Acclaim-2 </i></div>

<div style="font-family: &quot;Times New Roman&quot;; font-size: 10pt; font-variant: normal; margin: 0pt; text-indent: 0pt; text-align: justify;">&#xa0;</div>

<div style="font-family: &quot;Times New Roman&quot;; font-size: 10pt; font-variant: normal; margin: 0pt; text-indent: 0pt; text-align: justify;">Acclaim-2 is an open-label, multi-center Phase 1/2 clinical trial evaluating REQORSA in combination with Merck &amp; Co.&#8217;s Keytruda<sup style="vertical-align:top;line-height:120%;">&#174;</sup> in patients with late-stage NSCLC whose disease has progressed after treatment with Keytruda. The Acclaim-2 study has three portions - a Phase 1 dose escalation portion, a Phase 2 expansion portion, and a Phase 2 randomized portion. We&#xa0;currently are enrolling and treating patients in the Phase 1 dose escalation portion of Acclaim-2.&#xa0;We expect enrollment in the dose escalation portion of&#xa0;the study to be completed by the first quarter of 2024 due in part to slower than anticipated patient enrollment due to competition from other immunotherapy lung cancer studies, additional time required in connection with our transition to the new LNP CDMO and manufacture of final drug product as well as the addition of two more patients to the cohort.&#xa0;</div>

<div style="font-family: &quot;Times New Roman&quot;; font-size: 10pt; font-variant: normal; margin: 0pt; text-align: justify;">&#xa0;</div>

<div style="font-family: &quot;Times New Roman&quot;; font-size: 10pt; font-variant: normal; margin: 0pt; text-align: justify;"><i>Acclaim-3 </i></div>

<div style="font-family: &quot;Times New Roman&quot;; font-size: 10pt; font-variant: normal; margin: 0pt; text-align: justify;">&#xa0;</div>

<div style="font-family: &quot;Times New Roman&quot;; font-size: 10pt; font-variant: normal; margin: 0pt; text-align: justify;">Acclaim-3 uses a combination of REQORSA and Genentech, Inc.&#8217;s Tecentriq<sup style="vertical-align:top;line-height:120%;">&#174;</sup> as maintenance therapy in patients with extensive stage small cell lung cancer (&#8220;ES-SCLC&#8221;) who did not develop tumor progression after receiving Tecentriq and chemotherapy as initial standard treatment. The Acclaim-3 study has two portions - a Phase 1 dose escalation portion and a Phase 2 expansion portion. In November 2022, we filed with the FDA our protocol for our Phase 1/2 Acclaim-3 clinical trial. We expect to enroll the first patient in Acclaim-3 in the third quarter of 2023 due in part to additional time required in connection with our transition to the new LNP CDMO and manufacture of final drug product.&#xa0; Patients will be treated with REQORSA and Tecentriq until disease progression or unacceptable toxicity is experienced.</div>

<div style="font-family: &quot;Times New Roman&quot;; font-size: 10pt; font-variant: normal; margin: 0pt; text-indent: 36pt; text-align: justify;">&#xa0;</div>

<div style="font-family: &quot;Times New Roman&quot;; font-size: 10pt; font-variant: normal; margin: 0pt; text-indent: 0pt; text-align: justify;">We currently estimate that we have enough REQORSA to treat all of our current patients in our ongoing trials through approximately September 2023. We plan to have produced another batch of REQORSA by this time, but if we do not, then our clinical trials would be delayed.</div>

<div style="font-family: &quot;Times New Roman&quot;; font-size: 10pt; font-variant: normal; margin: 0pt; text-indent: 36pt; text-align: justify;">&#xa0;</div>

<div style="font-family: &quot;Times New Roman&quot;; font-size: 10pt; font-variant: normal; margin: 0pt; text-indent: 0pt; text-align: justify;"><i>REQORSA 1.0 vs. REQORSA 2.0</i></div>

<div style="font-family: &quot;Times New Roman&quot;; font-size: 10pt; font-variant: normal; margin: 0pt; text-align: justify;">&#xa0;</div>

<div style="font-family: &quot;Times New Roman&quot;; font-size: 10pt; font-variant: normal; margin: 0pt; text-align: justify;">The REQORSA product that we currently are utilizing in our clinical trials is referred to by us as REQORSA 1.0 because it is based on the original processes for the manufacture of REQORSA that were transferred from the Company&#8217;s academic collaboration partner to CDMOs starting over 10 years ago.&#xa0;As we move toward larger scale clinical trials and commercialization, we are making multiple process improvements primarily to better manage scalability, stability, shelf life and consistency and are in the process of transitioning to large scale, financially secure CDMOs with commercialization experience and deep expertise to implement these changes.&#xa0;We are also consolidating our CDMOs into two if not one to achieve greater efficiency.&#xa0;We refer to this next generation REQORSA as REQORSA 2.0.&#xa0;As technologies in the gene therapy space are rapidly evolving, we are continually evaluating them and strategizing as to their incorporation in our pipeline.&#xa0;</div>

<p style="font-family: &quot;Times New Roman&quot;; font-size: 10pt; font-variant: normal; margin: 0pt 0pt 0pt 8pt; text-indent: 27pt; text-align: justify;">Genprex, Inc. (the &#8220;<b>Company</b>&#8221;) believes that the granting of equity and cash compensation to its members of the Board of Directors (the &#8220;<b>Board</b>,&#8221; and members of the Board, the &#8220;<b>Directors</b>&#8221;) represents an effective tool to attract, retain and reward Directors who are not employees of the Company (the &#8220;<b>Outside Directors</b>&#8221;). This Amended and Restated Outside Director Compensation Policy (the &#8220;<b>Policy</b>&#8221;) is intended to formalize the Company&#8217;s policy regarding cash compensation and grants of equity to its Outside Directors. Unless otherwise defined herein, capitalized terms used in this Policy will have the meaning given such term in the Company&#8217;s 2018 Equity Incentive Plan (the &#8220;<b>Plan</b>&#8221;). Each Outside Director will be solely responsible for any tax obligations incurred by such Outside Director as a result of the equity and cash payments such Outside Director receives under this Policy.</p>
