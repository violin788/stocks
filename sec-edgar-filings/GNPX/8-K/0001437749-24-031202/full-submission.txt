<div style="font-family: &quot;Times New Roman&quot;; font-size: 10pt; font-variant: normal; margin: 0pt;"><b>Item 8.01</b>&#160;<b>Other Events.</b></div>

<div style="font-family: &quot;Times New Roman&quot;; font-size: 10pt; font-variant: normal; margin: 0pt; text-align: justify;">&#160;</div>

<div style="font-family: &quot;Times New Roman&quot;; font-size: 10pt; font-variant: normal; margin: 0pt; text-align: justify;">On October 15, 2024, Genprex, Inc. (&#8220;Genprex&#8221; or the &#8220;Company&#8221;) issued a press release in which it announced that Genprex has completed the 0.09 mg/kg dose group&#160;of the Phase 1 dose escalation portion of the Acclaim-3 clinical trial of Reqorsa&#174; Gene Therapy (quaratusugene ozeplasmid) in combination with Genentech&#8217;s Tecentriq&#174; (atezolizumab) as maintenance therapy for patients with extensive stage small cell lung cancer (&#8220;ES-SCLC&#8221;) who developed tumor progression after receiving Tecentriq and chemotherapy as initial standard treatment. In addition, the Safety Review Committee (&#8220;SRC&#8221;) has approved escalation to the highest dose group of 0.12 mg/kg.&#160;&#160;The SRC is comprised of three physicians who are principal investigators in the trial. The SRC may recommend that the trial continue at the same dose or at a lower dose, that it escalate to a higher dose, or that the study be terminated altogether due to safety concerns.</div>

<div style="font-family: &quot;Times New Roman&quot;; font-size: 10pt; font-variant: normal; margin: 0pt; text-align: justify;">&#160;</div>

<div style="font-family: &quot;Times New Roman&quot;; font-size: 10pt; font-variant: normal; margin: 0pt; text-align: justify;">In the Phase 1 dose escalation portion of the Acclaim-3 clinical trial, patients are treated with REQORSA and Tecentriq until disease progression or unacceptable toxicity is experienced. The primary endpoint of the Phase 1 escalation portion is to determine the maximum tolerated dose&#160;or recommended Phase 2 dose.&#160; There were no dose limiting toxicities&#160;in this dose group and the SRC recommended moving up to the highest dose group planned in the trial.&#160;&#160;The Phase 1 portion of the trial has two dose groups: 0.09 mg/kg, which has been completed, and 0.12 mg/kg which will now be enrolled. After the Phase 1 portion is complete, the Phase 2 expansion portion will enroll 50 patients at 10 to 15 U.S sites. Patients will be treated with REQORSA and Tecentriq until disease progression or unacceptable toxicity is experienced. The primary endpoint of the Phase 2 portion is to determine the 18-week progression-free survival rate from the time of the start of maintenance therapy with REQORSA and Tecentriq in patients with ES-SCLC. Patients will also be followed for survival. A Phase 2 futility analysis will be performed after the 25th patient enrolled and treated reaches 18 weeks of follow up. The Company continues to anticipate completion of enrollment in the Phase 1 dose escalation portion of the trial and to start the Phase 2 expansion portion in the second half of 2024, dependent on the number of patients needed to be enrolled in the 0.12 mg/kg dose group.</div>

<div style="font-family: &quot;Times New Roman&quot;; font-size: 10pt; font-variant: normal; margin: 0pt; text-align: justify;">&#160;</div>

<div style="font-family: &quot;Times New Roman&quot;; font-size: 10pt; font-variant: normal; margin: 0pt; text-align: justify;">The Acclaim-3 clinical trial has received U.S. Food and Drug Administration (&#8220;FDA&#8221;) Fast Track Designation for this patient population&#160;and Acclaim-3 has also received an FDA Orphan Drug Designation.&#160;&#160;</div>
