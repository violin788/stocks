<div style="font-family: &quot;Times New Roman&quot;; font-size: 10pt; font-variant: normal; margin: 0pt;"><b>Item 8.01</b>&#160;<b>Other Events.</b></div>

<div style="font-family: &quot;Times New Roman&quot;; font-size: 10pt; font-variant: normal; margin: 0pt; text-align: justify;">&#160;</div>

<div style="font-family: &quot;Times New Roman&quot;; font-size: 10pt; font-variant: normal; margin: 0pt; text-align: justify;">On December 16, 2024, Genprex, Inc. (&#8220;Genprex&#8221; or the &#8220;Company&#8221;) issued a press release in which it announced that Genprex has completed the Phase 1 dose escalation portion of the Acclaim-3 clinical trial of Reqorsa&#174; Gene Therapy (quaratusugene ozeplasmid) in combination with Genentech&#8217;s Tecentriq&#174; (atezolizumab) as maintenance therapy for patients with extensive stage small cell lung cancer (&#8220;ES-SCLC&#8221;) who developed tumor progression after receiving Tecentriq and chemotherapy as initial standard treatment. In addition, the Safety Review Committee (&#8220;SRC&#8221;) has approved the opening of the Phase 2 expansion portion of the trial.&#160; Based on full safety data, which showed no dose limiting toxicities, the SRC determined that the Recommended Phase 2 Dose of REQORSA will be 0.12 mg/kg. This was the highest dose level delivered in the Phase 1 portion of the trial. The SRC also recommended the trial advance to the Phase 2 expansion portion of the study, which the Company has now opened for enrollment.&#160; The Company also noted that it will be submitting the results of the Phase 1 portion of the study to a clinical meeting and anticipates data presentation in 2025.</div>

<div style="font-family: &quot;Times New Roman&quot;; font-size: 10pt; font-variant: normal; margin: 0pt; text-align: justify;">&#160;</div>

<div style="font-family: &quot;Times New Roman&quot;; font-size: 10pt; font-variant: normal; margin: 0pt; text-align: justify;">The Phase 1 dose escalation portion of the trial had two dose groups: 0.09 mg/kg and 0.12 mg/kg. The Company anticipates that the Phase 2 expansion portion will enroll approximately 50 patients at approximately 10 to 15 U.S sites. Patients will be treated with REQORSA and Tecentriq until disease progression or unacceptable toxicity is experienced. The primary endpoint of the Phase 2 portion is to determine the 18-week progression-free survival rate from the time of the start of maintenance therapy with REQORSA and Tecentriq in patients with ES-SCLC. Patients will also be followed for survival. A Phase 2 futility analysis will be performed after the 25th patient enrolled and treated reaches 18 weeks of follow up.</div>
