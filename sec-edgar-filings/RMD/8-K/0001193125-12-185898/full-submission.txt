 </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12px;margin-bottom:0px" ALIGN="center"><FONT STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2"><B>RESMED INC. ANNOUNCES RECORD FINANCIAL RESULTS FOR THE </B></FONT></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:0px;margin-bottom:0px" ALIGN="center"><FONT STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2"><B>QUARTER ENDED AND NINE MONTHS ENDED MARCH 31, 2012 </B></FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT
STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2">SAN DIEGO, California, April&nbsp;26, 2012 &#150; ResMed Inc. (NYSE: RMD) today announced record revenue and income for the quarter ended March&nbsp;31, 2012. Revenue for the quarter ended March&nbsp;31,
2012 was $349.1 million, an 11% increase (a 13% increase on a constant currency basis) over the quarter ended March&nbsp;31, 2011. For the quarter ended March&nbsp;31, 2012, income from operations was $76.5 million and net income was $64.6 million,
an increase of 20% and 21%, respectively, compared to the quarter ended March&nbsp;31, 2011. Diluted earnings per share for the quarter ended March&nbsp;31, 2012 were $0.44, an increase of 29% compared to the quarter ended March&nbsp;31, 2011.
</FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2">SG&amp;A expenses were $101.0 million for the quarter ended March&nbsp;31, 2012, an increase of $8.4 million, or 9% (a 10% increase on a
constant currency basis) compared to the quarter ended March&nbsp;31, 2011. SG&amp;A costs were 28.9% of revenue in the quarter ended March&nbsp;31, 2012, compared to 29.5% for the quarter ended March&nbsp;31, 2011. </FONT></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2">R&amp;D expenses were $28.4 million for the quarter ended March&nbsp;31, 2012, or 8.1% of revenue. R&amp;D expenses increased by 22% (an 18% increase on
a constant currency basis) compared to the quarter ended March&nbsp;31, 2011. R&amp;D expenses were negatively impacted by the appreciation of the Australian dollar against the U.S. dollar. </FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT
STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2">The company amortized acquired intangibles of $3.6 million ($2.6 million, net of tax) during the quarter ended March&nbsp;31, 2012. Stock-based compensation costs incurred during the quarter ended
March&nbsp;31, 2012 of $7.9 million ($5.2 million, net of tax) consisted of expenses associated with stock options, restricted stock units, and our employee stock purchase plan. </FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT
STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2">For the nine months ended March&nbsp;31, 2012, revenue was $996.6 million, an increase of 11% over the nine months ended March&nbsp;31, 2011 (a 10% increase on a constant currency basis). For the nine
months ended March&nbsp;31, 2012, income from operations and net income were $204.7 million and $178.0 million, an increase of 2% and 6%, respectively, compared to the nine months ended March&nbsp;31, 2011. Diluted earnings per share for the nine
months ended March&nbsp;31, 2012 were $1.18 per diluted share, an increase of 10% compared to the nine months ended March&nbsp;31, 2011. </FONT></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2">Inventory, at $183.5 million, decreased by $17.3 million compared to June&nbsp;30, 2011. Accounts receivable days sales outstanding, at 67 days,
decreased by 2 days compared to June&nbsp;30, 2011. </FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2">Peter C. Farrell, Ph.D, chairman and chief executive officer, commented, &#147;On a
regional basis, revenue in the Americas increased by 18% to $189.9 million over the prior year quarter. Revenues outside the Americas were $159.2 million, an increase of 4% over the prior year&#146;s quarter (a 7% increase on a constant currency
basis). Our global operating profit for the March quarter was $76.5 million and cash flow from operations was a solid $86.0 million, demonstrating excellent operating performance. During the quarter, we also repurchased 2.3&nbsp;million shares, at a
cost of $67.0 million. </FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2">&#147;If we focus on individual products, it is clear that mask growth remains exceptionally strong as demand for our
interface offerings remains robust, especially in the Americas. We are also very pleased to see America&#146;s flow generator sales return to double digit growth at 14% this quarter, reflecting continued demand for our S9&#153; line of flow
generators, as we roll out the final products on this platform. This is especially the case for the S9 VPAP&#153; line of products. </FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2">&#147;We
also just announced the release of our new patient compliance management solution, EasyCare Online. This innovative new tool, which will be used by sleep labs, home medical equipment suppliers and other health care providers, aggregates usage and
efficacy data from sleep-disordered breathing patients on CPAP therapy. Simple to use, while saving time and money, EasyCare Online employs a secure, cloud-based system which provides compliance data to give a real point of differentiation for our
customers; this will be particularly important in today&#146;s compliance-driven environment. Just as importantly, EasyCare Online will optimize patients&#146; therapy and health. </FONT></P>
