</TABLE> <P STYLE="margin-top:24pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman" ALIGN="center"><B>ResMed Inc. Announces Results for the First Quarter of Fiscal Year 2018 </B></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman" ALIGN="center"><B><I>Revenue increased 13% to $523.7&nbsp;million; up 11% on a constant currency basis </I></B></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman" ALIGN="center"><B><I>GAAP diluted earnings per share of $0.60; <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">non-GAAP</FONT> diluted earnings per share of $0.66
</I></B></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman" ALIGN="center"><B><I>Operating cash flow of $94.0&nbsp;million in the first quarter </I></B></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>SAN DIEGO, October</B><B></B><B>&nbsp;26, 2017</B> &#150; ResMed Inc. (NYSE: RMD, ASX: RMD) today announced results for its quarter ended
September&nbsp;30, 2017. Revenue for the quarter was $523.7&nbsp;million, a 13&nbsp;percent increase compared to the same period of the prior year. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">&#147;We started the fiscal year with strong 11&nbsp;percent constant currency revenue growth led by our masks, <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap"><FONT
STYLE="white-space:nowrap"><FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">software-as-a-service</FONT></FONT></FONT> solutions, and devices,&#148; said Mick Farrell, ResMed&#146;s chief executive officer. &#147;Our new masks have performed well around the world,
device sales are solid, and operating profit returned to double-digit growth in the quarter.&#148; </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Farrell concluded, &#147;We are achieving share gains
with new product innovation and delivering sustainable operating profit growth. We continue to pioneer products, services and solutions that improve patient outcomes, create efficiencies for our homecare providers, and help physicians and payers
better manage chronic disease and lower long-term healthcare costs.&#148; </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:18pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B><I>Analysis of first quarter results </I></B></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">First quarter revenue in the Americas, excluding Brightree, was $296.6&nbsp;million, an 11&nbsp;percent increase over the same period of the prior year.
Brightree revenue for the first quarter was $38.1&nbsp;million, an increase of 15&nbsp;percent compared to the same period of the prior year. Revenue in combined EMEA and APAC was $189.0&nbsp;million, an increase of 11&nbsp;percent on a constant
currency basis, compared to the same period of the prior year. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Gross margin in the first quarter was 58.4&nbsp;percent, higher than the prior year&#146;s
quarter gross margin of 57.8&nbsp;percent. The improvement in gross margin compared to prior year was due to manufacturing and procurement efficiencies and the prior year Astral field safety notification expenses, which were partially offset by
declines in average selling prices. </P>

 <P STYLE="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Income from operations for the quarter was $112.6&nbsp;million, a 20&nbsp;percent increase compared with the
quarter ended September&nbsp;30, 2016. <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">Non-GAAP</FONT> income from operations for the quarter was $124.3&nbsp;million, a 12&nbsp;percent increase compared to the same period of the prior year. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Selling, general and administrative expenses were $143.9&nbsp;million, a 12&nbsp;percent increase over the same period in the prior year, or a 10&nbsp;percent
increase on a constant currency basis. SG&amp;A expenses improved to 27.5&nbsp;percent of revenue in the quarter, compared with 27.7&nbsp;percent reported in the quarter ended September&nbsp;30, 2016.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Research and development expenses were $37.4&nbsp;million, or 7.1&nbsp;percent of revenue. R&amp;D expenses increased by 9&nbsp;percent compared with the same
period last year, or a 6&nbsp;percent increase on a constant currency basis. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Amortization of acquired intangible assets was $11.8&nbsp;million during the
quarter, which is consistent with the same period last year. Stock-based compensation costs incurred during the quarter of $11.9&nbsp;million consisted of expenses associated with employee equity grants, and our employee stock purchase plan. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Net income for the quarter was $86.1&nbsp;million, a 13&nbsp;percent increase compared to the same period of the prior year.
<FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">Non-GAAP</FONT> net income was $94.1&nbsp;million, a 7&nbsp;percent increase compared to the prior year. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><FONT
STYLE="white-space:nowrap">Non-GAAP</FONT> measures adjust for amortization of acquired intangibles and the Astral battery field safety notification expenses. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">GAAP diluted earnings per share for the quarter increased by 11&nbsp;percent to $0.60. <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">Non-GAAP</FONT> diluted earnings per
share of $0.66 were 6&nbsp;percent higher compared with the same period of the prior year. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Cash flow from operations for the quarter was
$94.0&nbsp;million compared to net income in the current quarter of $86.1&nbsp;million. During the quarter we paid $49.7&nbsp;million in dividends and repaid $60.0&nbsp;million of our outstanding debt. </P>
