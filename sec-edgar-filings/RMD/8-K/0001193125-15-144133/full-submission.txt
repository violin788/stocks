 <P STYLE="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:11pt; font-family:Arial" ALIGN="right"><B>Exhibit 99.1 </B></P> <P STYLE="font-size:12pt;margin-top:0pt;margin-bottom:0pt">&nbsp;</P>

 </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:11pt; font-family:Arial" ALIGN="center"><B>ResMed Inc. Announces Results for the Third Quarter of Fiscal Year 2015 </B></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:11pt; font-family:Arial" ALIGN="center"><B><I>Revenue increased 6% to $422 million; up 13% on a constant currency basis </I></B></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:11pt; font-family:Arial" ALIGN="center"><B><I>GAAP diluted earnings per share of $0.64, or non-GAAP earnings per share of $0.65 </I></B></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:11pt; font-family:Arial" ALIGN="center"><B><I>Operating cash flow of $90.9 million </I></B></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:11pt; font-family:Arial" ALIGN="center"><B><I>Company repurchased 300,000 shares in the third quarter </I></B></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:18pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:11pt; font-family:Arial"><B>San Diego, April&nbsp;23, 2015</B> &#150; ResMed Inc. (NYSE: RMD) today announced results for its quarter ended March&nbsp;31, 2015. Revenue for the quarter was
$422.5 million, a 6 percent increase compared to the quarter ended March&nbsp;31, 2014 (a 13 percent increase on a constant currency basis). Net income was $91.0 million, an increase of 1 percent compared to the quarter ended March&nbsp;31, 2014.
Diluted earnings per share for the quarter were $0.64, an increase of 2 percent compared to the quarter ended March&nbsp;31, 2014. Non-GAAP diluted earnings per share for the quarter were $0.65, a 2 percent increase compared to the quarter ended
March&nbsp;31, 2014. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:11pt; font-family:Arial">&#147;We are pleased to report excellent progress with our new product launches, as we continued to see double-digit revenue growth,&#148;
said Mick Farrell, ResMed&#146;s chief executive officer. &#147;Strong demand for our new flow generator launches in the U.S. and continued growth in our Asia Pacific and Europe regions drove our results in the quarter. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:11pt; font-family:Arial">&#147;We saw strong customer adoption of our range of new flow generators: the Astral, AirSense&#153; 10 and the recently launched AirCurve&#153; 10 series of
cloud-connected bilevel devices. This quarter, we continued to build our informatics capabilities with the acquisition of Jaysec, a provider of cloud-based software solutions for the home medical equipment industry, as well as the integration of our
AirView patient management system with leading informatics partners.&#148; Farrell concluded, &#147;We are executing on our long-term strategy: growing our core sleep disordered breathing market and investing in future opportunities in chronic
obstructive pulmonary disease as well as cardio-respiratory conditions. Our innovative products and solutions improve patient outcomes, lower healthcare costs, and reduce the impact of chronic disease.&#148; </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:18pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:11pt; font-family:Arial"><B><I>Analysis of third quarter results </I></B></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:11pt; font-family:Arial">In the third quarter of fiscal year
2015, revenue in the Americas was $250.9 million, a 16 percent increase over the prior year&#146;s quarter. Revenue in combined Europe and Asia Pacific was $171.6 million, a 6 percent decrease compared to the quarter ended March&nbsp;31, 2014 (a 9
percent increase on a constant currency basis). </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:11pt; font-family:Arial">Gross margin in the third quarter was 59.5 percent, lower than the prior year, mainly due to declines in average
selling prices, an unfavorable product and geographic mix, and an unfavorable impact from foreign exchange rate movements. </P>

<p Style='page-break-before:always'>
<HR  SIZE="3" style="COLOR:#999999" WIDTH="100%" ALIGN="CENTER">

 <P STYLE="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:11pt; font-family:Arial">Selling, general and administrative expenses were $116.3 million for the quarter, a 1 percent increase (a 10 percent
increase on a constant currency basis) over the quarter ended March&nbsp;31, 2014. SG&amp;A expenses improved to 27.5 percent of revenue in the quarter, compared to 28.9 percent in the quarter ended March&nbsp;31, 2014. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:11pt; font-family:Arial">Research and development expenses were $27.0 million for the quarter, or 6.4 percent of revenue. R&amp;D expenses decreased by 8 percent (a 4 percent increase on a
constant currency basis) compared to the quarter ended March&nbsp;31, 2014. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:11pt; font-family:Arial">Operating profit for the quarter was $105.9 million and cash flow from operations was
$90.9 million. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:11pt; font-family:Arial">Amortization of acquired intangible assets was $2.2 million ($1.6 million, net of tax) during the quarter. Stock-based compensation costs incurred
during the quarter of $11.7 million ($8.2 million, net of tax) consisted of expenses associated with employee equity grants, and the company&#146;s employee stock purchase plan. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:11pt; font-family:Arial"><B><I>Share repurchase program</I></B> </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:11pt; font-family:Arial"><B><I></I></B>During the quarter, the
company repurchased 300,000 shares at a cost of $20.3 million, as part of its ongoing capital management program. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:11pt; font-family:Arial"><B><I>Dividend program </I></B> </P>

2015. The dividend will be paid in U.S. currency to holders of ResMed&#146;s common stock trading on the New York Stock Exchange. Holders of Chess Depositary Instruments trading on the Australian Securities Exchange will receive an equivalent amount
