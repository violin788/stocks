 </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman" ALIGN="center"><B>RESMED INC. ANNOUNCES RECORD FINANCIAL RESULTS FOR THE </B></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman" ALIGN="center"><B>QUARTER ENDED SEPTEMBER 30, 2013 </B></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">SAN
DIEGO, October&nbsp;24, 2013 - ResMed Inc. (NYSE: RMD) today announced record results for the quarter ended September&nbsp;30, 2013. Revenue for the quarter ended September&nbsp;30, 2013 was $357.7 million, a 5% increase (a 4% increase on a constant
currency basis) over the quarter ended September&nbsp;30, 2012. For the quarter ended September&nbsp;30, 2013, net income was $80.9 million, an increase of 14% compared to the quarter ended September&nbsp;30, 2012. Diluted earnings per share for the
quarter ended September&nbsp;30, 2013 were $0.56, a 14% increase compared to the quarter ended September&nbsp;30, 2012. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">SG&amp;A expenses were $101.3
million for the quarter ended September&nbsp;30, 2013, an increase of $3.0 million, or 3% (a 4% increase on a constant currency basis) compared to the quarter ended September&nbsp;30, 2012. SG&amp;A costs were 28.3% of revenue in the quarter ended
September&nbsp;30, 2013, compared to 28.9% for the quarter ended September&nbsp;30, 2012. SG&amp;A expenses were favorably impacted by the depreciation of the Australian dollar against the U.S. dollar. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">R&amp;D expenses were $27.4 million for the quarter ended September&nbsp;30, 2013, or 7.7% of revenue. R&amp;D expenses increased by 1% (a 9% increase on a
constant currency basis) compared to the quarter ended September&nbsp;30, 2012. R&amp;D expenses were favorably impacted by the depreciation of the Australian dollar against the U.S. dollar. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Amortization of acquired intangibles totalled $2.4 million ($1.8 million, net of tax) during the quarter ended September&nbsp;30, 2013. Stock-based
compensation costs incurred during the quarter ended September&nbsp;30, 2013 of $10.8 million ($8.8 million, net of tax) consisted of expenses associated with stock options, restricted stock units, and our employee stock purchase plan. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Mick Farrell, chief executive officer, commented, &#147;In the first quarter of fiscal 2014, revenue in the Americas increased by 4% over the prior
year&#146;s quarter, to $201.5 million. Revenue outside the Americas was $156.2 million, a 5% increase on a constant currency basis, over the prior year&#146;s quarter. Global revenue growth was driven primarily by sales of higher valued flow
generators, like our S9 AutoSet&#153; and S9&#153; bilevels, while mask spares and accessories did particularly well this quarter. Operating profit for the September quarter was $96.9 million, cash flow from operations was $90.4 million, while gross
margin increased to 63.7%, all demonstrating excellent operating performance. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">&#147;We continued to face a tough competitive product cycle this quarter.
We have only just released our new mask products, but we were encouraged by their early sales. The Quattro&#153; Air is on its way to being one of the best mask launches ever. The Swift&#153; FX Nano, which was already selling well in Europe, was
just launched in the U.S. in September with good reception. We have a rich pipeline of products scheduled for introduction over the course of this fiscal year, providing us with plenty of opportunities for robust growth ahead. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">&#147;The opportunities in sleep and respiratory care remain attractive; we continue to provide our customers with the right solutions to better manage their
businesses while providing high quality products to improve compliance and patient care. We are focused on continuing to improve the quality of life for patients who suffer from sleep-disordered breathing and its related co-morbidities, such as
cardiology and diabetes; respiratory disorders, including chronic obstructive pulmonary disease; preventing disease progression; and saving both in-patient and out-patient costs.&#148; </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Additionally, the board of directors has today declared a quarterly dividend of $0.25 per share, which will have a record date of November&nbsp;19, 2013, and
be payable on December&nbsp;18, 2013. The dividend will be paid in U.S. currency to holders of ResMed&#146;s common stock trading on the New York Stock Exchange (NYSE). Holders of Chess Depositary Instruments (CDIs) trading on the Australian
Securities Exchange, will receive an equivalent amount in Australian currency, based on the exchange rate on the record date, and reflecting the 10:1 ratio between CDIs and NYSE shares. Because the two exchanges have different settlement and
