<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>SAN DIEGO &#150; April</B><B></B><B>&nbsp;26, 2018</B> &#150; ResMed (NYSE: RMD, ASX: RMD) today announced results for its quarter ended March&nbsp;31,
2018. Revenue for the quarter was $591.6&nbsp;million, a 15&nbsp;percent increase compared to the same period of the prior year. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">&#147;We had a strong
quarter, with solid revenue growth at the top line, and operating leverage improving the bottom line,&#148; said ResMed CEO Mick Farrell. &#147;Our cloud-connected medical device strategy is succeeding; the major markets for our sleep business are
adopting remote monitoring systems, which plays to the strength of our offerings. Our mask business performed well across all markets, and our cloud-based <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap"><FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap"><FONT
STYLE="white-space:nowrap">software-as-a-service</FONT></FONT></FONT> business also grew rapidly in Q3.&#148; </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">&#147;We believe we are
well-positioned,&#148; Farrell said. &#147;We continue to innovate. We are improving our existing products and solutions, and have a robust pipeline for the future.&#148; </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:18pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B><I>Analysis of third quarter results </I></B></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Third quarter
revenue in the United States, Canada and Latin America, excluding Brightree, was $317.5&nbsp;million, a 7&nbsp;percent increase over the same period of the prior year. Brightree revenue for the third quarter was $39.9&nbsp;million, an increase of
14&nbsp;percent compared to the same period of the prior year. Revenue in combined Europe, Asia and other markets was $234.2&nbsp;million, an increase of 16&nbsp;percent on a constant currency basis, compared to the same period of the prior year.
</P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Gross margin in the third quarter was 58.2&nbsp;percent, lower than the prior year&#146;s quarter gross margin of 58.3&nbsp;percent, mainly due to
declines in average selling prices, which were partially offset by manufacturing and procurement efficiencies. </P>

<p Style='page-break-before:always'>

 <P STYLE="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Income from operations for the quarter was $136.4&nbsp;million, a 27&nbsp;percent increase compared with the
quarter ended March&nbsp;31, 2017. <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">Non-GAAP</FONT> income from operations for the quarter was $159.0&nbsp;million, a 25&nbsp;percent increase compared to the same period of the prior year. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Selling, general and administrative expenses were $147.9&nbsp;million, a 7&nbsp;percent increase over the same period in the prior year, or a 3&nbsp;percent
increase on a constant currency basis. SG&amp;A expenses improved to 25.0&nbsp;percent of revenue in the quarter, compared with 26.8&nbsp;percent reported in the quarter ended March&nbsp;31, 2017. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Research and development expenses were $37.4&nbsp;million, or 6.3&nbsp;percent of revenue. R&amp;D expenses increased by 7&nbsp;percent compared with the same
period last year, or a 4&nbsp;percent increase on a constant currency basis. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Amortization of acquired intangible assets was $11.7&nbsp;million during the
quarter, which is consistent with the same period last year. Stock-based compensation costs incurred during the quarter of $12.0&nbsp;million consisted of expenses associated with employee equity grants and ResMed&#146;s employee stock purchase
plan. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Net income for the quarter was $110.1&nbsp;million, a 25&nbsp;percent increase compared to the same period of the prior year. <FONT
STYLE="white-space:nowrap">Non-GAAP</FONT> net income was $132.5&nbsp;million, a 32&nbsp;percent increase compared to the prior year. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><FONT
STYLE="white-space:nowrap">Non-GAAP</FONT> measures adjust for amortization of acquired intangibles, the impact of U.S. tax reform on income tax expense, restructuring expenses, litigation settlement expenses, acquisition-related expenses and the
Astral battery field safety notification expenses. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">GAAP diluted earnings per share for the quarter increased by 23&nbsp;percent to $0.76. <FONT
STYLE="white-space:nowrap">Non-GAAP</FONT> diluted earnings per share of $0.92 were 30&nbsp;percent higher compared with the same period of the prior year. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Cash flow from operations for the quarter was $149.1&nbsp;million compared to net income in the current quarter of $110.1&nbsp;million. During the quarter
ResMed paid $50.0&nbsp;million in dividends. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:18pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B><I>Impact of U.S. tax reform on income tax expense </I></B></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">On December&nbsp;22, 2017, &#147;H.R.1,&#148; originally known as the Tax Cuts and Jobs Act, was enacted into law (&#147;U.S. tax reform&#148;). ASC&nbsp;740
<I>Income Taxes</I> requires companies to recognize the effect of any tax laws during the period in which they are enacted. Accordingly, during the quarter ended December&nbsp;31, 2017, ResMed performed preliminary calculations which have been
refined during the current quarter. Based on these refinements and additional guidance from the U.S. Internal Revenue Service, ResMed recognized additional income tax expense of $5.6&nbsp;million during the three months ended March&nbsp;31, 2018,
for a total income tax expense of $132.2&nbsp;million during the nine months ended March&nbsp;31, 2018. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">The U.S. tax reform significantly revises the
U.S. corporate income tax by, among other things, imposing a <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">one-time</FONT> transition tax on unremitted foreign earnings, lowering the corporate income tax rate from 35&nbsp;percent to 21&nbsp;percent and
implementing a territorial tax system in regard to foreign earnings. The <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">one-time</FONT> transition tax on unremitted foreign earnings results in additional income tax expense of $5.6&nbsp;million for the three
months ended March&nbsp;31, 2018, and $125.5&nbsp;million for the nine months ended March&nbsp;31, 2018. ResMed recorded $11.4&nbsp;million in income taxes payable and $114.1&nbsp;million as long-term income taxes payable for the transition tax. The
lower corporate tax rate reduces net deferred tax assets by $6.7&nbsp;million and increases income tax expense by $6.7&nbsp;million for the nine months ended March&nbsp;31, 2018. The amounts recorded as part of U.S. tax reform should be considered
provisional and may continue to be revised through ResMed&#146;s quarter ending December&nbsp;31, 2018. </P>

 <P STYLE="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B><I>Australian Taxation Office audit update </I></B></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">In connection with the audit by the Australian Taxation Office (&#147;ATO&#148;) for the tax years 2009 through 2013, ResMed received Notices of Amended
Assessments in March 2018. Based on these assessments, the ATO is asserting that ResMed owes $151.7&nbsp;million in additional income tax and $38.4&nbsp;million in accrued interest. In April 2018, ResMed agreed to a payment arrangement with the ATO
to pay $75.9&nbsp;million, 50&nbsp;percent of the additional assessed tax, with the remaining amounts only due if it is unsuccessful in defending its position. ResMed does not agree with the ATO&#146;s assessments and intends to pursue
administrative and legal steps to defend its position. ResMed continues to believe it will be successful in defending its position and therefore has not recognized any income tax expense in relation to these assessments. At March&nbsp;31, 2018,
ResMed recorded a liability of $75.9&nbsp;million in short-term income taxes payable, which was offset by an equivalent asset recorded in other long-term assets. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:18pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B><I>Debt refinancing </I></B></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">On April&nbsp;16, 2018, ResMed
entered into a new unsecured syndicated facility (&#147;Facility&#148;) that provides for an $800&nbsp;million five-year revolving Credit Facility and a $200&nbsp;million five-year Term Loan. The proceeds from the initial funding of the Term Loan
were used to repay a portion of the outstanding balance of the Credit Facility. As a result of the refinancing, ResMed has classified the outstanding balance at March&nbsp;31, 2018, as long-term debt. </P>
