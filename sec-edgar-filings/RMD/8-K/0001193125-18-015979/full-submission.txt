<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman" ALIGN="center"><I>Revenue increased 13% to $601.3&nbsp;million; up 11% on a constant currency basis </I></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman" ALIGN="center"><I>GAAP-diluted earnings per share of $0.07 impacted by <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">one-time</FONT> tax charges; </I></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman" ALIGN="center"><I><FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">non-GAAP-diluted</FONT> earnings per share of $1.00 </I></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman" ALIGN="center"><I>Operating cash flow of $132.6&nbsp;million in the second quarter </I></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>SAN DIEGO, January</B><B></B><B>&nbsp;22, 2018</B> &#150; ResMed Inc. (NYSE: RMD, ASX: RMD) today announced results for its quarter ended December&nbsp;31,
2017. Revenue for the quarter was $601.3&nbsp;million, a 13&nbsp;percent increase compared to the same period of the prior year. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">&#147;We had a strong
quarter with double-digit revenue and operating profit growth&#148; said Mick Farrell, ResMed&#146;s chief executive officer. &#147;Our masks have performed well around the world, device sales are solid, and our cloud-based software continues to
grow rapidly. Our operating excellence initiatives are achieving leverage in the business with more runway ahead.&#148; </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Farrell concluded, &#147;We
recently announced our first ResMed branded portable oxygen concentrator, called Mobi, which will launch in the current quarter. Mobi exemplifies patient-centered innovation and our commitment to pioneer products and create value with services and
solutions that improve patients&#146; quality of life, reduce the impact of chronic disease and save healthcare costs globally.&#148; </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:18pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B><I>Analysis of
second quarter results </I></B></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Second quarter revenue in United States, Canada and Latin America, excluding Brightree, was $329.2&nbsp;million, a
12&nbsp;percent increase over the same period of the prior year. Brightree revenue for the second quarter was $38.7&nbsp;million, an increase of 14&nbsp;percent compared to the same period of the prior year. Revenue in Europe, Asia and other markets
was $233.4&nbsp;million, an increase of 8&nbsp;percent on a constant currency basis, compared to the same period of the prior year. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Gross margin in the
second quarter was 58.2&nbsp;percent, lower than the prior year&#146;s quarter gross margin of 58.3&nbsp;percent mainly due to declines in average selling prices, which were partially offset by manufacturing and procurement efficiencies. </P>

<p Style='page-break-before:always'>
<HR  SIZE="3" style="COLOR:#999999" WIDTH="100%" ALIGN="CENTER">

 <P STYLE="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Income from operations for the quarter was $146.0&nbsp;million, a 51&nbsp;percent increase compared with the
quarter ended December&nbsp;31, 2016. <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">Non-GAAP</FONT> income from operations for the quarter was $157.3&nbsp;million, a 20&nbsp;percent increase compared to the same period of the prior year. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Selling, general and administrative expenses were $151.8&nbsp;million, a 9&nbsp;percent increase over the same period in the prior year, or a 6&nbsp;percent
increase on a constant currency basis. SG&amp;A expenses improved to 25.2&nbsp;percent of revenue in the quarter, compared with 26.3&nbsp;percent reported in the quarter ended December&nbsp;31, 2016. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Research and development expenses were $40.6&nbsp;million, or 6.8&nbsp;percent of revenue. R&amp;D expenses increased by 6&nbsp;percent compared with the same
period last year, or a 4&nbsp;percent increase on a constant currency basis. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Amortization of acquired intangible assets was $11.3&nbsp;million during the
quarter, which is consistent with the same period last year. Stock-based compensation costs incurred during the quarter of $12.0&nbsp;million consisted of expenses associated with employee equity grants and our employee stock purchase plan. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Net income for the quarter was $9.5&nbsp;million, an 88&nbsp;percent decrease compared to the same period of the prior year.
<FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">Non-GAAP</FONT> net income was $143.8&nbsp;million, a 39&nbsp;percent increase compared to the prior year. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><FONT
STYLE="white-space:nowrap">Non-GAAP</FONT> measures adjust for amortization of acquired intangibles, impact of U.S. tax reform on income tax expense, restructuring expenses, litigation settlement expenses, acquisition related expenses and the Astral
battery field safety notification expenses. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">GAAP diluted earnings per share for the quarter decreased by 87&nbsp;percent to $0.07. <FONT
STYLE="white-space:nowrap">Non-GAAP</FONT> diluted earnings per share of $1.00 were 37&nbsp;percent higher compared with the same period of the prior year. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Cash flow from operations for the quarter was $132.6&nbsp;million compared to net income in the current quarter of $9.5&nbsp;million. During the quarter we
paid $49.9&nbsp;million in dividends. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:18pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B><I>Impact of U.S. tax reform on income tax expense </I></B></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">On December&nbsp;22, 2017 &#147;H.R.1&#148;, originally known as the Tax Cuts and Jobs Act, was enacted into law (&#147;U.S. tax reform&#148;). ASC&nbsp;740
<I>Income Taxes</I> requires companies to recognize the effect of any tax laws during the period in which they are enacted. Accordingly, the company performed preliminary calculations and based on these, recognized additional income tax expense of
$126.6&nbsp;million for the three and six months ended December&nbsp;31, 2017. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">The U.S. tax reform significantly revises the U.S. corporate income tax
by, among other things, imposing a <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">one-time</FONT> transition tax on unremitted foreign earnings, lowering the corporate income tax rate from 35&nbsp;percent to 21&nbsp;percent and implementing a territorial tax
system in regard to foreign earnings. The <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">one-time</FONT> transition tax on unremitted foreign earnings results in additional income tax expense of $119.9&nbsp;million, of which $10.5&nbsp;million has been recorded
as income taxes payable and $109.4&nbsp;million has been recorded as long-term income taxes payable. The lower corporate tax rate results in a reduction of net deferred tax assets and an increase in income tax expense of $6.7&nbsp;million. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:18pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B><I>Share repurchase program </I></B></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">During the quarter, the
company repurchased 100,000 shares at a cost of $8.5&nbsp;million, as part of its ongoing capital management program. </P>
