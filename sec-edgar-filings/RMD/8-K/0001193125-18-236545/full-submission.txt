<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>SAN DIEGO, August</B><B></B><B>&nbsp;2, 2018</B> &#150; ResMed Inc. (NYSE: RMD, ASX: RMD) today announced results for its quarter ended June&nbsp;30, 2018.
Revenue for the quarter was $623.6&nbsp;million, a 12&nbsp;percent increase compared to the same period of the prior year. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">&#147;We closed out the year
with strong performance across all aspects of our business, from solid <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">top-line</FONT> revenue growth&#151;driven by geographically balanced results across our entire portfolio of offerings&#151;to continued
improvements in operating leverage, which has resulted in double-digit bottom-line growth,&#148; said Mick Farrell, ResMed&#146;s chief executive officer. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">&#147;We continue to advance our cloud-connected medical device strategy and are growing our cloud-based <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap"><FONT
STYLE="white-space:nowrap"><FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">software-as-a-service</FONT></FONT></FONT> business. Our clinical studies and research efforts are being recognized and the opportunity to increase awareness of sleep-related breathing
disorders and improve patient quality of life is bigger than ever.&#148; </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Farrell concluded, &#147;We believe we are well-positioned as we enter fiscal
year 2019. We continue to innovate, we are improving our existing portfolio of products and offerings, and we have a robust pipeline to drive future growth.&#148; </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:18pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B><I>Analysis of fourth quarter results </I></B></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Fourth quarter
revenue in U.S., Canada and Latin America, excluding Brightree, was $346.7&nbsp;million, a 10&nbsp;percent increase over the same period of the prior year. Brightree revenue for the fourth quarter was $40.4&nbsp;million, an increase of
12&nbsp;percent compared to the same period of the prior year. Revenue in combined Europe, Asia and other markets was $236.5&nbsp;million, an increase of 9&nbsp;percent on a constant currency basis, compared to the same period of the prior year.
</P>

<Center><DIV STYLE="width:8.5in" align="left">
 <P STYLE="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Gross margin in the fourth quarter was 58.1&nbsp;percent, lower than the prior year&#146;s quarter gross
margin of 58.2&nbsp;percent mainly due to declines in average selling prices, which were partially offset by manufacturing and procurement efficiencies. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Income from operations for the quarter was $146.9&nbsp;million, a 15&nbsp;percent increase compared with the quarter ended June&nbsp;30, 2017. <FONT
STYLE="white-space:nowrap">Non-GAAP</FONT> income from operations for the quarter was $166.0&nbsp;million, a 19&nbsp;percent increase compared to the same period of the prior year. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Selling, general and administrative expenses were $156.8&nbsp;million, a 6&nbsp;percent increase over the same period in the prior year, or a 3&nbsp;percent
increase on a constant currency basis. SG&amp;A expenses improved to 25.1&nbsp;percent of revenue in the quarter, compared with 26.6&nbsp;percent reported in the quarter ended June&nbsp;30, 2017. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Research and development expenses were $39.7&nbsp;million, or 6.4&nbsp;percent of revenue. R&amp;D expenses increased by 8&nbsp;percent compared with the same
period last year, or a 6&nbsp;percent increase on a constant currency basis. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Amortization of acquired intangible assets was $11.6&nbsp;million during the
quarter, which is consistent with the same period last year. Stock-based compensation costs incurred during the quarter of $12.5&nbsp;million consisted of expenses associated with employee equity grants and our employee stock purchase plan. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Net income for the quarter was $109.8&nbsp;million, an 8&nbsp;percent increase compared to the same period of the prior year.
<FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">Non-GAAP</FONT> net income was $136.3&nbsp;million, a 24&nbsp;percent increase compared to the prior year. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><FONT
STYLE="white-space:nowrap">Non-GAAP</FONT> measures adjust for amortization of acquired intangibles, impact of U.S. tax reform on income tax expense, restructuring expenses and impact of foreign tax credit adjustments on income tax expense. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">GAAP diluted earnings per share for the quarter increased by 7&nbsp;percent to $0.76. <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">Non-GAAP</FONT> diluted earnings per
share of $0.95 were 23&nbsp;percent higher compared with the same period of the prior year. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Cash flow from operations for the quarter was
$129.4&nbsp;million compared to net income in the current quarter of $109.8&nbsp;million. During the quarter we paid $50.0&nbsp;million in dividends. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:18pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B><I>Impact of U.S. tax reform on income tax expense </I></B></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">On December&nbsp;22, 2017 &#147;H.R.1&#148;, originally known as the Tax Cuts and Jobs Act, was enacted into law (&#147;U.S. tax reform&#148;). ASC 740 Income
Taxes requires companies to recognize the effect of any tax laws during the period in which they are enacted. Accordingly, during the quarter ended December&nbsp;31, 2017, we performed preliminary calculations which have been refined during the
remainder of the fiscal year. Based on these refinements, and additional guidance from the U.S. Internal Revenue Service, we recognized additional income tax expense of $5.8&nbsp;million during the three months ended June&nbsp;30, 2018 for a total
income tax expense of $138.0&nbsp;million during the year ended June&nbsp;30, 2018. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">The U.S. tax reform significantly revises the U.S. corporate income
tax by, among other things, imposing a <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">one-time</FONT> transition tax on unremitted foreign earnings, lowering the corporate income tax rate from 35&nbsp;percent to 21&nbsp;percent and implementing a territorial tax
system in relation to foreign earnings. </P>

<Center><DIV STYLE="width:8.5in" align="left">
 <P STYLE="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B><I>Analysis of fiscal year 2018 results </I></B></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Revenue for the year increased 13&nbsp;percent over the prior year to $2.3&nbsp;billion, or a 10&nbsp;percent increase on a constant currency basis. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Income from operations for the year was $541.8&nbsp;million, a 27&nbsp;percent increase over the prior year. <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">Non-GAAP</FONT>
income from operations for the year was $606.6&nbsp;million, a 19&nbsp;percent increase over the prior year. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">Non-GAAP</FONT> measures adjust for amortization of acquired intangibles, impact of U.S. tax reform on income tax expense,
restructuring expenses, impact of foreign tax credit adjustments on income tax expense, litigation settlement expenses, acquisition related expenses and the Astral battery field safety notification expenses. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Net income for the year was $315.6&nbsp;million, an 8&nbsp;percent decrease over the prior year. <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">Non-GAAP</FONT> net income
was $507.8&nbsp;million, a 27&nbsp;percent increase compared to the prior year. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">GAAP diluted earnings per share decreased 9&nbsp;percent to $2.19. <FONT
STYLE="white-space:nowrap">Non-GAAP</FONT> diluted earnings per share for the year was $3.53, a 25&nbsp;percent increase compared with the prior year. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Cash flow from operations for the year was $505.0&nbsp;million. During the year we paid $199.5&nbsp;million in dividends and repaid a net amount of
$796.2&nbsp;million of our outstanding debt. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:18pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B><I>Debt refinancing </I></B></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">As reported during the previous quarter, on April&nbsp;17, 2018, we entered into a new unsecured syndicated facility (&#147;Facility&#148;) that provides for
an $800&nbsp;million five-year revolving Credit Facility and a $200&nbsp;million five-year Term Loan. The proceeds from the initial funding of the Term Loan were used to repay a portion of the outstanding balance of the Credit Facility. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:18pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B><I>Share repurchase program </I></B></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">During the quarter, we
repurchased 250,000 shares at a cost of $25.9&nbsp;million, as part of our ongoing capital management program. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:18pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B><I>Dividend program </I></B></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">The ResMed board of directors today declared quarterly cash dividend of $0.37 per share. The dividend will have a record date of August&nbsp;16, 2018, payable
on September&nbsp;20, 2018. The dividend will be paid in U.S. currency to holders of ResMed&#146;s common stock trading on the New York Stock Exchange. Holders of Chess Depositary Instruments trading on the Australian Securities Exchange will
receive an equivalent amount in Australian currency, based on the exchange rate on the record date, and reflecting the 10:1 ratio between CDIs and NYSE shares. The <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">ex-dividend</FONT> date will be August&nbsp;15, 2018
for common stock holders and for CDI holders. ResMed has received a waiver from the ASX&#146;s settlement operating rules, which will allow ResMed to defer processing conversions between its common stock and CDI registers from August&nbsp;15, 2018
through August&nbsp;16, 2018, inclusive.</P>
