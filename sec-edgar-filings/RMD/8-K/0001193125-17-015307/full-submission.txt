</TABLE> <P STYLE="margin-top:24pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman" ALIGN="center"><B>ResMed Inc. Announces Results for the Second Quarter of Fiscal Year 2017 </B></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman" ALIGN="center"><B><I>Revenue increased 17% to $530.4&nbsp;million; up 18% on a constant currency basis </I></B></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman" ALIGN="center"><B><I>GAAP diluted earnings per share of $0.54; non-GAAP diluted earnings per share of $0.73 </I></B></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman" ALIGN="center"><B><I>Operating cash flow of $119.9&nbsp;million in the second quarter </I></B></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>SAN DIEGO, January&nbsp;23, 2017</B>&nbsp;&#150; ResMed Inc. (NYSE: RMD) today announced results for its quarter ended December&nbsp;31, 2016. Revenue for
the quarter was $530.4&nbsp;million, a 17&nbsp;percent increase compared to the same period of the prior year. Excluding the contribution from the Brightree business acquired in April 2016, revenue for the quarter was $496.6&nbsp;million, a
9&nbsp;percent increase. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">&#147;We had a strong quarter with 18&nbsp;percent constant currency revenue growth led by solid growth in our Brightree
software offerings and global device sales,&#148; said Mick Farrell, ResMed&#146;s chief executive officer. &#147;We continue to pioneer game-changing products and create value with our solutions. This quarter we announced: FDA clearance for the
world&#146;s smallest travel CPAP; the creation of SleepScore Labs to focus on consumer sleep wellness; reaching the milestone of one billion nights of sleep data; and new research demonstrating that the use of myAir&#153; significantly improves
patient adherence to sleep therapy in Europe.&#148; </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Farrell concluded, &#147;We are focused on our goal of changing the lives of 20&nbsp;million patients
by 2020 and we continue to lead the industry through an exciting pipeline of new products and connected care solutions that improve patient outcomes, create efficiencies for our homecare customers, and help physicians and providers better manage
chronic disease and lower healthcare costs.&#148; </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:18pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B><I>Analysis of second quarter results </I></B></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Second quarter revenue in the Americas was $326.8&nbsp;million, a 21&nbsp;percent increase over the same period of the prior year. This included Brightree
revenue of $33.8&nbsp;million. Excluding Brightree, revenue in the Americas was $293.0&nbsp;million, a 9&nbsp;percent increase over the prior year. Revenue in combined EMEA and APAC was $203.6&nbsp;million, an increase of 13&nbsp;percent on a

 <P STYLE="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Gross margin in the second quarter was 58.3&nbsp;percent, higher than the prior year&#146;s quarter gross margin

<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Income from operations for the quarter was $96.9&nbsp;million, a 10&nbsp;percent decline compared with the quarter ended December&nbsp;31, 2015. Non-GAAP

$38.2&nbsp;million, or 7.2&nbsp;percent of revenue. R&amp;D expenses increased by 32&nbsp;percent compared with the same period last year, or a 28&nbsp;percent increase on a constant currency basis. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Amortization of acquired intangible assets was $11.7&nbsp;million during the quarter, an increase of $7.3&nbsp;million compared with the same period last
year. The increase in amortization of acquired intangible assets was primarily due to the amortization expense associated with the Brightree and Inova acquisitions. Stock-based compensation costs incurred during the quarter of $10.8&nbsp;million

20&nbsp;percent decrease compared to the same period of the prior year. Non-GAAP net income was $103.3&nbsp;million, a 1&nbsp;percent increase compared to the prior year. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Non-GAAP measures adjust for amortization of acquired intangibles, litigation settlement expenses, acquisition related expenses associated with additional
