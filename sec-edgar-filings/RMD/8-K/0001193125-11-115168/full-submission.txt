 <P STYLE="margin-top:12px;margin-bottom:0px" ALIGN="center"><FONT STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2"><B>RESMED INC. ANNOUNCES RECORD FINANCIAL RESULTS FOR THE </B></FONT></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:0px;margin-bottom:0px" ALIGN="center"><FONT STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2"><B>QUARTER ENDED AND NINE MONTHS ENDED MARCH 31, 2011 </B></FONT></P> <P STYLE="font-size:12px;margin-top:0px;margin-bottom:0px">&nbsp;</P>
<P STYLE="line-height:0px;margin-top:0px;margin-bottom:2px;border-bottom:0.5pt solid #000000">&nbsp;</P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2">SAN DIEGO, California, April&nbsp;28, 2011
&#150; ResMed Inc. (NYSE: RMD) today announced record revenue and income for the quarter ended March&nbsp;31, 2011. Revenue for the quarter ended March&nbsp;31, 2011 was $313.3 million, a 12% increase (also a 12% increase on a constant currency
basis) over the quarter ended March&nbsp;31, 2010. For the quarter ended March&nbsp;31, 2011, income from operations was $64.0 million and net income was $53.4 million, an increase of 5% and 9%, respectively, compared to the quarter ended
March&nbsp;31, 2010. Diluted earnings per share for the quarter ended March&nbsp;31, 2011 were $0.34, an increase of 10% compared to the quarter ended March&nbsp;31, 2010. </FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT
STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2">SG&amp;A expenses were $92.5 million for the quarter ended March&nbsp;31, 2011, an increase of $8.4 million, or 10% (an 8% increase on a constant currency basis) compared to the quarter ended
March&nbsp;31, 2010. SG&amp;A costs were 29.5% of revenue in the quarter ended March&nbsp;31, 2011, compared to 30.2% for the quarter ended March&nbsp;31, 2010. </FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT
STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2">R&amp;D expenses were $23.3 million for the quarter ended March&nbsp;31, 2011, or 7.4% of revenue. R&amp;D expenses increased by 28% (an 18% increase on a constant currency basis) compared to the quarter
ended March&nbsp;31, 2010. R&amp;D expenses were negatively impacted by the appreciation of the Australian dollar against the U.S. dollar. </FONT></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2">The company amortized acquired intangibles of $2.7 million ($1.8 million, net of tax) during the quarter ended March&nbsp;31, 2011. Stock-based
compensation costs incurred during the quarter ended March&nbsp;31, 2011 of $6.8 million ($4.8 million, net of tax) consisted of expenses associated with stock options, restricted stock units, and our employee stock purchase plan. </FONT></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2">For the nine months ended March&nbsp;31, 2011, revenue was $901.3 million, an increase of 13% over the nine months ended March&nbsp;31, 2010 (a 14%
increase on a constant currency basis). For the nine months ended March&nbsp;31, 2011, income from operations and net income were $200.2 million and $168.5 million, an increase of 17% and 23%, respectively, compared to the nine months ended
March&nbsp;31, 2010. Diluted earnings per share for the nine months ended March&nbsp;31, 2011 were $1.07 per diluted share, an increase of 20% compared to the nine months ended March&nbsp;31, 2010. </FONT></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2">Inventory, at $205.0 million, increased by $19.4 million compared to June&nbsp;30, 2010. Accounts receivable days sales outstanding, at 68 days,
decreased by 3 days compared to June&nbsp;30, 2010. </FONT></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12px;margin-bottom:0px"><FONT STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2">Peter C. Farrell PhD, Chairman and Chief Executive Officer, commented, &#147;In the third
quarter of fiscal 2011 we continued to show strong year-over-year growth especially in Europe and the Asia-Pacific region. Revenue outside the Americas increased by 16% to $152.7 million over the prior year&#146;s quarter, or a 15% increase on a
constant currency basis. Revenue in the Americas increased by over 9% to $160.5 million over the prior year&#146;s quarter. Americas&#146; revenues continued to be adversely impacted by reduced bilevel sales, but we expect that the recent launch of
the S9&#153; bilevel series will help to reverse this trend. Worldwide, our growth in flow generators was mainly driven by sales of the S9 AutoSet&#153;. Patient interface sales did extremely well in all regions. With the recent launches of the
