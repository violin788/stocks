<P STYLE="font: 10pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0">Item 5.02. Departure of Directors or Certain Officers; Election of Directors; Appointment of Certain Officers; Compensatory
Arrangements of Certain Officers.</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0; text-indent: 0.5in">On December 4, 2018, Muhtar Kent provided notice to The Coca-Cola Company (the &ldquo;Company&rdquo;) of his
intention not to stand for re-election at the Company&rsquo;s 2019 Annual Meeting of Shareowners (the &ldquo;2019 Annual Meeting&rdquo;)
and to resign from Chairman of the Board of Directors (the &ldquo;Board&rdquo;) on that same date. In addition, on December 4,
2018, Sam Nunn provided notice to the Company of his intention not to stand for re-election at the 2019 Annual Meeting. Messrs.
Kent and Nunn will remain Chairman of the Board and Lead Independent Director, respectively, and will maintain their committee
memberships, through the 2019 Annual Meeting.</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0; text-indent: 0.5in">On December 6, 2018, the Board elected James Quincey, who currently serves as the
Company&rsquo;s President and Chief Executive Officer, to serve as Chairman of the Board following Mr. Kent&rsquo;s retirement, contingent
on Mr. Quincey&rsquo;s re-election as a Director at the 2019 Annual Meeting. On December 6, 2018, the Board also elected Maria Elena
Lagomasino to replace Mr. Nunn as Lead Independent Director, contingent on her re-election as a Director at the 2019 Annual
Meeting.</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 12pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0; text-align: center; text-indent: 19.45pt"><B>James Quincey to Succeed
Muhtar Kent as Chairman of The Coca-Cola Company</B></P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0; text-align: center; text-indent: 19.45pt"><B>&nbsp;</B></P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0; text-align: center; text-indent: 19.45pt">Kent to Retire After 41
Years in the Coca-Cola System;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0; text-align: center; text-indent: 19.45pt">Board of Directors Elects
Quincey as Company Chairman</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0; text-align: center; text-indent: 19.45pt">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0; text-align: center; text-indent: 19.45pt">Sam Nunn to Step Down After
22 Years as a Director;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0; text-align: center; text-indent: 19.45pt">Board Elects Maria Elena
Lagomasino as Lead Independent Director to Succeed Nunn</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0; text-align: center; text-indent: 19.45pt">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0"><B>ATLANTA, Dec. 6, 2018</B> &ndash; James Quincey was elected today
as the 14th chairman of The Coca-Cola Company, succeeding Muhtar Kent, who plans to retire in April after a Coca-Cola system career
that started in 1978.</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0">Quincey, who currently serves as president and CEO, will become chairman
following the company&rsquo;s annual meeting in April 2019. He will then serve as chairman and CEO. As previously announced, Brian
Smith will become president and chief operating officer of the company on Jan. 1, 2019.</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0">Kent will remain as the company&rsquo;s chairman through the 2019
annual meeting, where he will not stand for reelection as a director. Kent served as both chairman and CEO from 2009 until 2017
and continued as chairman after Quincey became CEO.</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0"><B>Nunn to retire</B></P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0">The company also announced that Sam Nunn will not stand for reelection
at the 2019 annual meeting, closing a 22-year tenure as a Coca-Cola director, including the last five years as lead independent
director. Nunn, a former U.S. senator who represented the state of Georgia from 1972 to 1996, has served as a Coca-Cola board member
through a period of remarkable changes, including the successful transition of the CEO position from Kent to Quincey in May 2017
and the rapid diversification of the company&rsquo;s beverage portfolio.</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0.15pt 0 0">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0.15pt 0 0">The board has elected Maria Elena &ldquo;Mel&rdquo; Lagomasino
as Nunn&rsquo;s successor as lead independent director. Lagomasino joined the Coca-Cola board in 2008.</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0">The new roles for Quincey and Lagomasino are contingent on their
reelections as directors at the 2019 annual meeting.</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0"><B>From delivery truck to chairman</B></P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0">Kent, whose early days with Coca-Cola included time working on a
delivery truck, has held a wide range of roles over the years. Kent&rsquo;s full bio is available at https://www.coca-colacompany.com/our-company/board-of-directors-muhtar-kent.</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0">&ldquo;One of the most important jobs of a chairman is to ensure
that a strong leadership succession plan is in place,&rdquo; Kent said. &ldquo;I&rsquo;m delighted that the board has elected James
as chairman. He is the right leader to take the Coca-Cola system to the next level and through the next decade.&rdquo;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0">Quincey becomes the 14th chairman in the 132-year history of the
company. He joined Coca-Cola in 1996 and has held a variety of positions around the world. Quincey&rsquo;s full bio is available
at https://www.coca-colacompany.com/our-company/operations-leadership-james-quincey.</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0">&ldquo;I&rsquo;m honored that the board of directors has asked me
to serve as chairman of the company,&rdquo; Quincey said. &ldquo;I appreciate the years of mentorship, encouragement and insights
I&rsquo;ve gained from Muhtar. I also join my fellow directors in thanking Sam for his many years of service, and I look forward
to working with Mel as she steps into an expanded role as lead independent director.&rdquo;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0"><B>New lead independent director</B></P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0"><B>&nbsp;</B></P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0.15pt 0 0">Nunn&rsquo;s wide-ranging career includes serving as co-chairman
and former CEO of the Nuclear Threat Initiative. He was also a director of Chevron Corp., Dell Inc., General Electric Co. and Scientific
Atlanta. Nunn&rsquo;s full bio is available at https://www.coca-colacompany.com/our-company/board-of-directors-sam-nunn.</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0.15pt 0 0">Lagomasino is CEO and managing partner of WE Family Offices,
a global family office serving high-net-worth families. She has held a variety of business leadership roles. She serves on the
board of The Walt Disney Co. and previously was a director of Avon Products Inc. Lagomasino&rsquo;s full bio is available at https://www.coca-colacompany.com/our-company/board-of-directors-maria-elena-lagomasino.</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0.15pt 0 0">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0.15pt 0 0">As lead independent director, Lagomasino&rsquo;s duties
will include executing all matters of the board as outlined in the company&rsquo;s corporate governance guidelines. These include
leading the board&rsquo;s annual evaluation of the chair and CEO; having the authority to call meetings of independent directors;
approving board agendas; and presiding over meetings of independent directors and non-employee directors. For more information
on the role of lead independent director, visit https://www.coca-colacompany.com/investors/corporate-governance-guidelines.</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0">&ldquo;Sam has had a remarkable career and has served as a valued
voice on our board of directors,&rdquo; Kent said. &ldquo;As he steps down, I can&rsquo;t think of a better person to succeed him
than Mel.&rdquo;</P>
