<P STYLE="font: 11pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0"><B>Item 5.02.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Departure of Directors or Certain Officers; Election
of Directors; Appointment of Certain Officers; Compensatory Arrangements of Certain Officers.</B></P>

<P STYLE="font: 11pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 11pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0 0.1in 0 0; text-indent: 0.5in">On December 8, 2017, The Coca-Cola
Company (the &ldquo;Company&rdquo;) announced that Irial Finan, Executive Vice President and President, Bottling Investments Group,
will be retiring from the Company. On December 31, 2017, Mr. Finan will step down from his position as Executive Vice President
and President, Bottling Investments Group, and he will continue to serve as an executive advisor until his retirement on March
31, 2018.</P>

<P STYLE="font: 11pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0 0.1in 0 0; text-indent: 0.5in">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 11pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0 0.1in 0 0; text-indent: 0.5in">On December 7, 2017, the Company and
Mr. Finan entered into a Separation Agreement detailing the terms of his retirement. The Separation Agreement provides that Mr.&nbsp;Finan
will receive severance benefits under the terms of The Coca-Cola Severance Pay Plan. With respect to annual incentives, if Mr.
Finan remains employed through December 31, 2017, he will receive an award for 2017, and if Mr. Finan remains employed through
March 31, 2018, he will receive an award for 2018, prorated for three months, in each case, under the standard terms of the Performance
Incentive Plan. With respect to long-term incentives, Mr. Finan will not receive any additional equity grants, all Mr. Finan&rsquo;s
outstanding performance share unit awards will be treated according to the existing terms of the equity plans and related agreements
and all Mr. Finan&rsquo;s outstanding stock options awards will be exercisable according to the existing terms of the equity plans
and related agreements. Mr.&nbsp;Finan&rsquo;s retirement benefits will consist of those benefits accrued and vested under the
standard terms and conditions of the plans in which he participates, including health and welfare coverage. Mr.&nbsp;Finan will
receive outplacement services. In connection with the Separation Agreement, on December 7, 2017, Mr.&nbsp;Finan entered into a
Full and Complete Release and Agreement on  Trade Secrets and Confidentiality. The foregoing description is qualified
in its entirety by the agreements with Mr. Finan, a copy of which is attached hereto as Exhibit 10.1 and incorporated herein by
reference.</P>

<P STYLE="font: 11pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0 0.1in 0 0; text-indent: 0.5in">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0 2.15pt 0 0; text-align: center"><B><U>IRIAL FINAN TO RETIRE FROM
COCA-COLA; CALIN DRAGAN TO TAKE LEADERSHIP OF BOTTLING INVESTMENTS GROUP</U></B></P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0 2.35pt 0 0"><B>&nbsp;</B></P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0"><B>ATLANTA, Dec. 8, 2017 </B>&ndash; The&nbsp;Coca-Cola&nbsp;Company today
announced that Irial Finan, a 36-year veteran of the&nbsp;Coca-Cola&nbsp;system, plans to retire from his post as Executive Vice President
and President of Bottling Investments Group, or BIG. Calin Dragan, who currently serves as Regional Director for BIG&rsquo;s ASEAN
(Southeast Asia) and Middle East bottlers, will be promoted to lead BIG.</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0">Finan, who reports to President and CEO James Quincey, will retire
March 31, 2018. He will step down from his current role on Dec. 31 and will remain with the company as executive advisor until
his retirement. &ldquo;I want to thank Irial for his leadership and dedicated service to our global&nbsp;Coca-Cola&nbsp;system over the
last 36 years,&rdquo; Quincey said. &ldquo;All of us at&nbsp;Coca-Cola&nbsp;join in wishing Irial, his wife, Deirdre, and his daughters, Ciara
and R&ouml;is&iuml;n, great success in their future plans.&rdquo;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0">Dragan, who begins his new role as President of BIG on Jan.
1, 2018, is based in Singapore and will report to John Murphy, President of&nbsp;Coca-Cola&rsquo;s&nbsp;Asia Pacific Group. Dragan will oversee
BIG operations globally. BIG, which will continue to play a significant role in contributing to&nbsp;Coca-Cola&rsquo;s&nbsp;overall growth,
is now largely concentrated in the Asia Pacific region. The company continues to work toward refranchising&nbsp;Coca-Cola&nbsp;Beverages
Africa, which is part of BIG.</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0.15pt 0 0">&ldquo;Calin brings a great breadth of system experience
and proven leadership abilities,&rdquo; Murphy said. &ldquo;He is the right person to lead BIG, including a strong focus on execution
and people development, as the business continues to be reshaped for the future.&rdquo;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0.15pt 0 0">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0.15pt 0 0"><B><U>About Irial Finan</U></B></P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0.15pt 0 0">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0.15pt 0 0">Finan, a native of Ireland, started
his&nbsp;Coca-Cola&nbsp;system career with multiple finance and operational roles across Europe. In 1995, he was promoted to
managing director of Molino Beverages, with responsibility for expanding markets, including Ireland, Romania, Russia and
Nigeria.</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0.15pt 0 0">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0.15pt 0 0">From 2001 to 2003, he served as CEO of bottler Coca-Cola
HBC, where he managed the merger integration of&nbsp;Coca-Cola&nbsp;Beverages PLC and Hellenic Bottling SA. He led the combined company&rsquo;s
operations in 26 countries.</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0.15pt 0 0">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0.15pt 0 0"></P>

bottling operations. At its height, BIG had operations across five continents and more than 80,000 employees.</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0.15pt 0 0">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0.15pt 0 0"><B><U>About Calin Dragan</U></B></P>

and acting Chairman, President and CEO for&nbsp;Coca-Cola&nbsp;East Japan.</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0">Dragan, 51, also served as Executive Vice President and Representative
Director of&nbsp;Coca-Cola&nbsp;West Bottling Co. in Japan from 2011 to 2012 and was CEO and General Administrator of&nbsp;Coca-Cola&nbsp;HBC&rsquo;s business in Romania and the Republic of Moldova from 2005 to 2011. Dragan started his&nbsp;Coca-Cola&nbsp;system career
in 1993 in Timisoara, Romania.</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Arial, Helvetica, Sans-Serif; margin: 0">Dragan, a native of Romania, graduated from the Polytechnic
