<TD ALIGN="left" VALIGN="top"><B>Departure of Directors or Certain Officers; Election of Directors; Appointment of Certain Officers; Compensatory Arrangement of Certain Officers. </B></TD></TR></TABLE>
<P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">On May&nbsp;26, 2016, upon the recommendation of its Nominating and Corporate Governance Committee, the Board of Directors (the
&#147;Board&#148;) of Rubicon Technology, Inc. (&#147;Rubicon&#148;) approved the expansion of the size of the Board from five to six members and the appointment of Timothy E. Brog as a new Class II director. Mr.&nbsp;Brog&#146;s initial term
started immediately upon Board approval and will expire at the 2018 annual meeting of Rubicon&#146;s stockholders. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">There is no
arrangement or understanding between Mr.&nbsp;Brog and any other person pursuant to which Mr.&nbsp;Brog was selected as a director and there are no transactions that would be reportable under Item&nbsp;404(a) of Regulation S-K. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">The Board now consists of six directors, five of whom are independent, including Mr.&nbsp;Brog. The Board has not determined at this time the
Board committees, if any, to which Mr.&nbsp;Brog will be appointed. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">In connection with Mr.&nbsp;Brog&#146;s appointment as a director,
Mr.&nbsp;Brog received a one-time equity compensation grant on May&nbsp;26, 2016 (the &#147;Date of Grant&#148;) of 73,529 shares of restricted common stock of Rubicon with an estimated value of $50,000 on the Date of Grant (the &#147;Appointment
Stock Grant&#148;). The Appointment Stock Grant vests in equal installments on each of the first three anniversaries of the Date of Grant, so long as Mr.&nbsp;Brog continuously serves as a director on the Board from the Date of Grant through each
such anniversary date, subject to the Rubicon Technology, Inc. 2007 Stock Incentive Plan, as amended and restated effective March&nbsp;23, 2011 (the &#147;2007 Plan&#148;) and the standard form restricted stock agreement utilized by Rubicon.
Although the estimated value of the Appointment Stock Grant was $50,000 on the Date of Grant, the actual value of the award to Mr.&nbsp;Brog will depend upon the performance of Rubicon&#146;s common stock between the Date of Grant and the vesting
date. To provide liquidity for Mr.&nbsp;Brog to pay income taxes with respect to the Appointment Stock Grant, Mr.&nbsp;Brog will receive a one-time cash payment of $50,000, payable in three equal annual installments on each of the first three
anniversaries of the Date of Grant and subject to the same restrictions as the vesting of the Appointment Stock Grant. In accordance with Rubicon&#146;s compensation policy applicable to all non-employee directors, Mr.&nbsp;Brog will receive an
annualized base fee of $70,000 for his service on the Board, payable in installments at the end of each remaining full calendar quarter in 2016 in an equal combination of cash and restricted common stock of Rubicon. The restricted common stock
issued to Mr.&nbsp;Brog as part of his base fee will be subject to the terms and conditions set forth in the 2007 Plan and the standard form restricted stock agreement utilized by Rubicon. In the event Mr.&nbsp;Brog is appointed to any committees of
the Board, he will receive additional committee service compensation in accordance with Rubicon&#146;s non-employee director compensation policy. The current Board committee service compensation amounts are disclosed in Rubicon&#146;s 2016
definitive proxy statement on Schedule 14A filed on May&nbsp;18, 2016. </P> <P STYLE="font-size:18pt;margin-top:0pt;margin-bottom:0pt">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman" ALIGN="center"><B>Rubicon Technology Appoints Timothy E. Brog as New Independent Director </B></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>BENSENVILLE, Illinois &#151; May&nbsp;31, 2016 &#151; </B>Rubicon Technology, Inc. (NASDAQ: RBCN) (&#147;Rubicon&#148; or the &#147;Company&#148;), a
leading provider of sapphire substrates and products to the LED, optical and semiconductor industries, today announced that it has appointed a new independent director, Timothy E. Brog, President of Locksmith Capital, to its Board of Directors,
effective immediately. With the addition of Mr.&nbsp;Brog, the Board expands to six members, five of whom are independent. <B> </B></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">&#147;We&#146;re
pleased to add Timothy to Rubicon&#146;s Board of Directors,&#148; said Don N. Aquilano, Chairman of the Board. &#147;He is an accomplished executive, with extensive investment, legal, management and financial experience &#150; as well as a strong
track record of driving change as a member of public company boards, serving on director slates of both management and activist shareholders. We welcome his insights as we pursue the appropriate path forward to drive long-term stockholder value.
Timothy brings valuable new perspectives to our Board, and his appointment is consistent with our commitment to maintaining a highly qualified Board with the necessary skills and expertise to navigate the current environment.&#148; </P>
