 <P STYLE="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman" ALIGN="right"><B>Exhibit 99.1 </B></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman" ALIGN="center"><B>RUBICON TECHNOLOGY SENDS LETTER TO STOCKHOLDERS </B></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman" ALIGN="center"><I>Recommends Stockholders Vote the <U>WHITE</U> Proxy Card in Support of Rubicon&#146;s Highly Qualified Nominees </I></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>BENSENVILLE, Illinois &#151; May&nbsp;26, 2016 &#151;</B>Rubicon Technology, Inc. (NASDAQ:RBCN), a leading provider of sapphire substrates and products to
the LED, optical and semiconductor industries, today sent a letter urging stockholders to support the Company&#146;s highly qualified nominees on the <B><U>WHITE</U></B> proxy card. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Included below is the text of the letter to Rubicon stockholders: </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:24pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman" ALIGN="center"><B>YOUR VOTE IS IMPORTANT </B></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman" ALIGN="center"><B>PLEASE VOTE THE WHITE PROXY CARD TODAY! </B></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">May&nbsp;26, 2016 </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Dear Fellow Stockholders: </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Rubicon Technology&#146;s Annual Meeting of Stockholders &#150; scheduled for Friday, June&nbsp;24, 2016 &#150; is fast approaching. Your support is critical
to prevent Paragon Technologies, Inc., a stockholder that holds less than 0.3% of our stock, from replacing two of Rubicon&#146;s highly qualified nominees with its own nominees. <B>We urge you to reject Paragon&#146;s nominees and vote
&#147;FOR&#148; Rubicon&#146;s incumbent director nominees, Don N. Aquilano and Donald R. Caldwell, on the WHITE proxy card.</B> </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Your Board and
management team are executing a plan to strengthen the business in the near term and to drive long-term stockholder value. We are already implementing a number of initiatives to improve sales, reduce costs and expand our product reach. We also
remain committed to regularly reviewing all options to maximize returns for all stockholders. We are confident these actions are the right ones to best position Rubicon for the future. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Nonetheless, Paragon intends to continue to disrupt our progress with its attempt to elect its two nominees to the Rubicon Board. <B>We strongly recommend
that stockholders reject Paragon&#146;s nominees &#150; please do not sign or return or vote any proxy card sent to you by Paragon!</B> </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:24pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman" ALIGN="center"><B>OUR HIGHLY QUALIFIED BOARD IS FOCUSED ON THE INTERESTS OF </B><B><I>ALL</I></B><B> STOCKHOLDERS </B></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">We believe Rubicon&#146;s Board is comprised of strong and experienced directors who are committed to doing what is best for <I>all</I> of the Company&#146;s
stockholders. At this year&#146;s Annual Meeting, we are asking for your support for two directors, Don Aquilano and Donald Caldwell. Not only are Messrs. Aquilano and Caldwell, in our view, highly qualified, but together, they own 10% of Rubicon
stock &#150; without a doubt, their interests are strongly aligned with all Rubicon stockholders. </P>

<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">During Mr.&nbsp;Jacobs&#146; short tenure as a director of SED, he approved fees to be paid to him and other non-executive directors, including a fee of

board with Mr.&nbsp;Gad. Considering Mr.&nbsp;Jacobs&#146; close ties to Mr.&nbsp;Gad, we would ask you to consider &#150; whose interests do you think he will protect, Mr.&nbsp;Gad&#146;s and Paragon&#146;s or those of <I>all </I>Rubicon
