<TD WIDTH="9%" VALIGN="top" ALIGN="left"><B>Item&nbsp;5.02</B></TD>
<TD ALIGN="left" VALIGN="top"><B>Departure of Directors or Certain Officers; Election of Directors; Appointment of Certain Officers; Compensatory Arrangement of Certain Officers. </B></TD></TR></TABLE>
<P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">On November&nbsp;30, 2016, Rubicon Sapphire Technology (Malaysia) Sdn. Bhd (&#147;Rubicon Malaysia&#148;), a wholly owned subsidiary of Rubicon
Technology, Inc. (the &#147;Company&#148;), provided written notice to Hany Tamim, the Company&#146;s Chief Operating Officer, to terminate Mr.&nbsp;Tamim&#146;s employment effective December&nbsp;31, 2016. This notice was delivered in accordance
with the termination without cause provision of Mr.&nbsp;Tamim&#146;s Letter of Appointment, which was previously filed as Exhibit 10.9 to the Company&#146;s Annual Report on Form 10-K, as amended, for the year ended December&nbsp;31, 2015.
Mr.&nbsp;Tamim&#146;s termination coincides with the anticipated closing of the Company&#146;s facility in Malaysia. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Pursuant to the
Letter of Appointment, subject to Mr.&nbsp;Tamim&#146;s execution and delivery of a complete release, Mr.&nbsp;Tamim is entitled to the following upon his termination: (i)&nbsp;payment of his base salary through and including the termination date,
and for six months thereafter, (ii)&nbsp;any unused vacation pay, (iii)&nbsp;any bonus earned prior to the termination date that remains unpaid, (iv)&nbsp;immediate vesting of his options and restricted stock units, (v)&nbsp;reimbursement of medical
claims in accordance with the Letter of Employment for six months following the termination date, and (vi)&nbsp;health insurance coverage for six months following the termination date. Mr.&nbsp;Tamim is subject to non-competition and
non-solicitation covenants contained in his Letter of Appointment for a period of one year after his termination. </P>
