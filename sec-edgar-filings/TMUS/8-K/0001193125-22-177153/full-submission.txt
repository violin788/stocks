 <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman;font-weight:bold;text-align:center">INTRODUCTORY NOTE </p> <p style="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">On April&#160;1, 2020, <span style="white-space:nowrap">T-Mobile</span> US, Inc., a Delaware corporation <span style="white-space:nowrap">(&#8220;T-Mobile&#8221;),</span> and Sprint Corporation, a Delaware corporation (&#8220;Sprint,&#8221; collectively with <span style="white-space:nowrap">T-Mobile,</span> the &#8220;Sellers&#8221;), completed the previously announced business combination (the &#8220;Merger&#8221;) between <span style="white-space:nowrap">T-Mobile</span> and Sprint pursuant to the Business Combination Agreement, dated as of April&#160;29, 2018, as amended, by and among <span style="white-space:nowrap">T-Mobile,</span> Sprint, and the other parties thereto. As a result of the Merger, Sprint became an indirect wholly-owned subsidiary of <span style="white-space:nowrap">T-Mobile.</span> </p> <p style="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">On July&#160;1, 2020, <span style="white-space:nowrap">T-Mobile</span> and Sprint completed the previously announced divestiture of Sprint&#8217;s prepaid wireless business to DISH Network Corporation, a Nevada corporation (&#8220;DISH&#8221;), pursuant to the terms and conditions of the Asset Purchase Agreement, dated as of July&#160;26, 2019 (as amended, the &#8220;Asset Purchase Agreement&#8221;). Pursuant to the terms of the Asset Purchase Agreement, DISH acquired Sprint&#8217;s prepaid wireless business, which was historically operated by Sprint under the Boost Mobile, Virgin Mobile and Sprint prepaid brands, including customer accounts, inventory, contracts, intellectual property and certain other specified assets (but excluding the Assurance brand Lifeline customers and the prepaid wireless customers of Shenandoah Telecommunications Company and Swiftel Communications, Inc.), and assumed certain related liabilities (collectively, the &#8220;Prepaid Business Sale&#8221;). </p> <p style="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">On July&#160;1, 2020, in connection with the closing of the Prepaid Business Sale, the Sellers and DISH entered into a Master Network Services Agreement (the &#8220;MNSA&#8221;). Pursuant to the terms of the MNSA, DISH would receive network services from <span style="white-space:nowrap">T-Mobile</span> for a period of seven years, including (i)&#160;legacy network services for certain Boost Mobile prepaid end users on the Sprint network, <span style="white-space:nowrap">(ii)&#160;T-Mobile</span> network services for certain end users that have been migrated to the <span style="white-space:nowrap">T-Mobile</span> network or provisioned on the <span style="white-space:nowrap">T-Mobile</span> network by or on behalf of DISH, and (iii)&#160;infrastructure mobile network operator services to assist in the access and integration of the DISH network. </p> <p style="margin-top:18pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman;font-weight:bold">Item&#160;1.01 - Entry into a Material Definitive Agreement </p> <p style="font-size:6pt;margin-top:0pt;margin-bottom:0pt">&#160;</p>
