<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman" ALIGN="center"><B><FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">T-Mobile</FONT> US Appoints Mark W. Nelson as New Executive Vice President and General Counsel </B></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman" ALIGN="center"><I>Announces retirement of General Counsel David Miller in 2022 after 26 years with <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">T-Mobile</FONT> </I></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>Bellevue, Washington and Overland Park, Kansas &#151; September</B><B></B><B>&nbsp;16, 2021 </B>&#151; <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">T-Mobile</FONT> US,
Inc. (NASDAQ: TMUS) announced today that Mark W. Nelson will join the company as executive vice president and general counsel in October. Nelson was named to the position as current General Counsel Dave Miller announced his retirement after 26 years
with <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">T-Mobile.</FONT> Miller will be retiring from the company on April&nbsp;1, 2022. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">With deep expertise in regulatory
and global antitrust matters as well as in U.S. civil litigation, Nelson is one of the most highly regarded lawyers in Washington D.C. He will report directly to Chief Executive Officer Mike Sievert and have offices in Bellevue, Wash., and
Washington D.C. The company also shared that its Board of Directors has elected Broady Hodder, senior vice president of corporate governance and strategic transactions, as its corporate secretary, reporting to Nelson. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">&#147;When Dave approached me about his retirement, I knew we had incredibly big shoes to fill and with Mark we have definitely found the right legal mind for

<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Having practiced law for over 25 years at Cleary Gottlieb, Nelson joins <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">T-Mobile</FONT> with a broad range of experience in
merger work and antitrust counseling, civil and criminal litigation, and regulatory proceedings before federal and state government agencies. Nelson is distinguished as one of the nation&#146;s leading antitrust lawyers and litigators by
<I>Chambers, Benchmark Litigation, </I>and<I> Legal 500</I>, among other publications. Notably, he has been counsel to <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">T-Mobile</FONT> US for the past two decades and has advised the company on a variety of matters,
including <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">T-Mobile</FONT> US&#146;s acquisition of MetroPCS and its merger with Sprint, for which Nelson was recognized by <I>The American Lawyer </I>as both &#147;Dealmaker of the Year&#148; and &#147;Litigator of
the Week&#148;. Nelson received a Juris Doctor from Harvard Law School and a Bachelor of Science degree from Cornell University. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Miller has played a
pivotal role in building <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">T-Mobile</FONT> US, including leading the company&#146;s legal and regulatory efforts in the <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">T-Mobile/Sprint</FONT> merger. Miller started his career in
wireless at Western Wireless in 1995, where he played an integral role in its IPO, the <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">spin-off</FONT> of its VoiceStream subsidiary, the roll up of the regional GSM carriers to a single national carrier, and the
acquisition of VoiceStream by Deutsche Telekom in 2001. That same year, Miller was named senior vice president and general counsel, and helped introduce the <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">T-Mobile</FONT> brand name in the U.S. Miller was named
executive vice president in 2011 and in subsequent years he and his team helped lead the company through its acquisition of MetroPCS in 2013, the roll out of its highly successful <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">Un-Carrier</FONT> strategy, and <FONT
STYLE="white-space:nowrap">T-Mobile&#146;s</FONT> game-changing merger with Sprint. </P>
