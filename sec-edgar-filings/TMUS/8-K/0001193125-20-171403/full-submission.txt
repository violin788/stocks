<td style="width:10%;vertical-align:top;white-space:nowrap;">Item&#160;1.01 </td>
<td style="font-family:Times New Roman;font-weight:bold;text-align:left;font-size:10pt;margin-bottom:0pt;margin-top:0pt;">Entry into a Material Definitive Agreement.</td> </tr> </table> <p style="font-family:Times New Roman;text-align:left;text-indent:4%;font-size:10pt;margin-bottom:0px;margin-top:6pt">As previously reported, on July&#160;26, 2019, <span style="white-space:nowrap">T-Mobile</span> US, Inc. <span style="white-space:nowrap">(&#8220;T-Mobile&#8221;)</span> entered into an Asset Purchase Agreement (the &#8220;Asset Purchase Agreement&#8221;) with Sprint Corporation (&#8220;Sprint&#8221;) and DISH Network Corporation (&#8220;DISH&#8221;). Pursuant to the terms and conditions of the Asset Purchase Agreement, DISH will acquire Sprint&#8217;s prepaid wireless business, which was&#32;historically operated by Sprint under the Boost Mobile, Virgin Mobile and Sprint prepaid brands, including customer accounts, inventory, contracts, intellectual property and certain other specified assets (but excluding the Assurance brand Lifeline customers and the prepaid wireless customers of Shenandoah Telecommunications Company and Swiftel Communications, Inc.), and assume certain related liabilities (collectively, the &#8220;Prepaid Business Sale&#8221;). </p> <p style="font-family:Times New Roman;text-align:left;text-indent:4%;font-size:10pt;margin-bottom:0px;margin-top:12pt">On June 17, 2020, the Department of Justice (the &#8220;DOJ&#8221;) determined that T-Mobile has complied with the requirement in the final judgment entered by a federal district court in Washington, D.C. on April 1, 2020 (the &#8220;Final Judgment&#8221;) to provide DISH the ability to cross-provision any new or existing customer of the prepaid business with a compatible handset onto the T-Mobile network. As a result, we believe all conditions to closing under the Asset Purchase Agreement (other than those conditions that can only be satisfied at closing) have been satisfied and, subject to the satisfaction of the conditions that must be satisfied at closing, the closing of the Prepaid Business Sale will occur on July 1, 2020. </p> <p style="font-family:Times New Roman;text-align:left;text-indent:4%;font-size:10pt;margin-bottom:0px;margin-top:12pt">In connection with the DOJ determination, on June 17, 2020, <span style="white-space:nowrap">T-Mobile,</span> Sprint and DISH entered into the First Amendment to the Asset Purchase Agreement (the &#8220;First Amendment&#8221;). Pursuant to the First Amendment, <span style="white-space:nowrap">T-Mobile,</span> Sprint and DISH agreed to proceed with the closing of the Prepaid Business Sale (the &#8220;Closing&#8221;) in accordance with the Asset Purchase Agreement on July&#160;1, 2020, subject to the terms and conditions of the Asset Purchase Agreement and the terms and conditions of the Final Judgment.
