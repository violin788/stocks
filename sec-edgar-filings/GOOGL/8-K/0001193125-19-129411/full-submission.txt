<TD ALIGN="left" VALIGN="top"> <P STYLE=" margin-top:0pt ; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman; " ALIGN="left"><B>Other Events. </B></P></TD></TR></TABLE>
<P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">On April&nbsp;24, 2019, the Board of Directors (the &#147;<U>Board</U>&#148;) of Alphabet Inc. (the &#147;<U>Company</U>&#148;) appointed Robin
L. Washington to serve as a member of the Board and the Leadership Development and Compensation Committee of the Board, effective April&nbsp;25, 2019. There are no arrangements or understandings between Ms.&nbsp;Washington and any other persons
pursuant to which Ms.&nbsp;Washington was selected as a director of the Company. There are no relationships or related transactions between Ms.&nbsp;Washington and the Company that would be required to be reported. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Ms.&nbsp;Washington has been the Executive Vice President and Chief Financial Officer of Gilead Sciences, Inc., a biopharmaceutical company,
since February 2014. She joined Gilead as Senior Vice President and Chief Financial Officer in May 2008. From January 2006 to June 2007, Ms.&nbsp;Washington served as Chief Financial Officer of Hyperion Solutions Corporation, an enterprise software
company. Prior to Hyperion, Ms.&nbsp;Washington served in a number of executive positions with PeopleSoft, Inc., a provider of enterprise application software. Ms.&nbsp;Washington has also been a member of the board of directors of Honeywell
International, Inc., a diversified technology and manufacturing company, since April 2013; and salesforce.com, inc., a provider of cloud-based customer relationship management platform, since September 2013. Ms.&nbsp;Washington also serves as a
member of the Board of Visitors, Graziadio School of Business and Management, Pepperdine University; the Presidents Council&nbsp;&amp; Ross Business School Advisory Board, University of Michigan; and the UCSF Benioff Children&#146;s Hospital Oakland
Board of Directors. Ms.&nbsp;Washington holds a Bachelor of Arts degree in business administration from the University of Michigan, a Master of Business Administration degree from Pepperdine University, and is a certified public accountant. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">In connection with her appointment to the Board, Ms.&nbsp;Washington will be granted an initial equity award of $1,000,000 in the form of the
Company&#146;s Restricted Stock Units (&#147;<U>GSUs</U>&#148;) on the first Wednesday of the month following her appointment to the Board, with each GSU entitling Ms.&nbsp;Washington to receive one share of the Company&#146;s Class&nbsp;C capital
stock as the GSU vests. The exact number of GSUs comprising the grant will be calculated by dividing $1,000,000 by the closing price of the Company&#146;s Class&nbsp;C capital stock on the trading day prior to grant. These GSUs will vest at the rate
of 25% on the 25th day of the month in which the grant&#146;s first anniversary occurs, and an additional 1/48th will vest on the 25th day of each month thereafter, subject to continued service on the Board on the applicable vesting dates. Following
each of the Company&#146;s annual stockholder meetings, Ms.&nbsp;Washington will also be eligible to receive the Company&#146;s standard compensation arrangement for <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">non-employee</FONT> directors, which consists of
an annual $350,000 GSU grant, vesting monthly over a period of four years, and an annual $75,000 cash retainer. The grant and payment following the 2019 annual stockholder meeting will be prorated based upon the time between the effective date of
Ms.&nbsp;Washington&#146;s appointment to the Board and the date of the 2019 annual stockholder meeting. The GSUs are subject to the terms and conditions of the Company&#146;s 2012 Stock Plan and its related grant agreements. The Company will also
reimburse Ms.&nbsp;Washington all reasonable expenses in connection with her services to the Company. A copy of the letter agreement between Ms.&nbsp;Washington and the Company is filed as Exhibit 10.1 to this Current Report on Form <FONT
STYLE="white-space:nowrap">8-K.</FONT> </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Ms.&nbsp;Washington will execute the Company&#146;s form of indemnification agreement, a copy of
which has been filed as Exhibit 10.4 to the Company&#146;s Current Report on Form <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">8-K</FONT> filed with the U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission on October&nbsp;2, 2015. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">In addition, on April&nbsp;24, 2019, each of Eric E. Schmidt and Diane B. Greene agreed with the Board and its Nominating and Corporate
Governance Committee that each of them will not stand for <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">re-election</FONT> to the Board at the expiration of their respective current terms, which will be at the 2019 annual meeting of stockholders. Such agreements
did not result from any disagreement between the Company and each of Mr.&nbsp;Schmidt and Ms.&nbsp;Greene. Mr.&nbsp;Schmidt will continue to serve as a technical advisor to the Company with a salary of $1. </P>
