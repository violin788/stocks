</B></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>SEATTLE &#150; August&nbsp;12, 2021 &#150; Silverback Therapeutics, Inc. (Nasdaq: SBTX) (&#147;Silverback&#148;), a clinical-stage
biopharmaceutical company leveraging its proprietary ImmunoTAC technology platform to develop systemically delivered, tissue targeted therapeutics for the treatment of cancer, chronic viral infections, and other serious diseases, today reported
financial results for the second quarter ended June&nbsp;30, 2021 and provided a business update. </B></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">&#147;The second quarter was notable for the
significant progress we made across our entire pipeline of tissue-targeted therapies, with SBT6050, our HER2-TLR8 ImmunoTAC leading the way with continued robust enrollment in our Phase 1/1b study,&#148; said Laura Shawver, Ph.D., chief executive
officer of Silverback. &#147;We are deeply appreciative of the patients, their families, and our clinical investigators who continue to contribute to the <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">SBT6050-101</FONT> clinical trial, and we look forward to
providing the first update of the clinical data at the ESMO conference in September.&#148; </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:18pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>Recent Highlights </B></P>

and six months ended June&nbsp;30, 2021 were $4.7&nbsp;million and $9.0&nbsp;million of <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">non-cash</FONT> stock-based compensation compared to $128,000 and $175,000 for the same periods in 2020. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Research and development expenses for the second quarter ended June&nbsp;30, 2021 were $17.7&nbsp;million, compared to $5.1&nbsp;million for the same period

increases in general and administrative expenses were primarily attributable to an increase in personnel-related expenses due to increased headcount in 2021, including new executives, as well as increases in salaries, bonuses, and stock-based
