 </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:24pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman" ALIGN="center"><B>Arcellx Provides Business Updates and Reports First Quarter 2022 Financial Results </B></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman" ALIGN="center"><B>&#151; </B>First patient dosed in the Phase 1 clinical trial evaluating <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">ACLX-001</FONT> utilizing
groundbreaking <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">ARC-SparX</FONT> technology in relapsed or refractory multiple myeloma (r/r MM) &#151; </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman" ALIGN="center">&#151; Management to host live webcast event during the 2022 ASCO Annual Meeting on Sunday, June&nbsp;5, 2022, at 7:00 p.m. CDT to discuss new
CART-ddBCMA data with a panel of clinician experts &#151; </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">FOSTER CITY, Calif., May&nbsp;12, 2022 /PRNewswire/ &#151; Arcellx, Inc. (NASDAQ: ACLX), a
biotechnology company reimagining cell therapy through the development of innovative immunotherapies for patients with cancer and other incurable diseases, today reported business highlights and financial results for the first quarter ended
March&nbsp;31, 2022. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">&#147;This is an exciting time for our company as we successfully completed our initial public offering in February, raising
$142&nbsp;million in gross proceeds and strengthened our balance sheet as we advance our pioneering platform to treat cancer and other incurable diseases,&#148; said Rami Elghandour, Arcellx&#146;s chairman and chief executive officer. &#147;In our
first quarter as a public company, we continued to lay the foundation for our near-term and long-term success and our dedicated team made meaningful strides towards our 2022 milestones. We are looking forward to presenting additional patient and
longer-term <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">follow-up</FONT> data on approximately 25 patients for our lead CART-ddBCMA program for patients with relapsed or refractory multiple myeloma (r/r MM) during an oral presentation at ASCO in June.
Recently, we also advanced our development pipeline programs with the dosing of the first patient in our Phase 1 study evaluating <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">ACLX-001</FONT> for patients with r/r MM utilizing our
<FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">ARC-SparX</FONT> technology. <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">ARC-SparX</FONT> has the potential to overcome some of the core challenges in cell therapy by reducing toxicities and addressing antigen heterogeneity,
opening up significant market opportunities in harder to treat indications. In the second half of this year, we look forward to initiating our Phase 1 <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">ARC-SparX</FONT> clinical trial in patients with acute myeloid
leukemia and high-risk myelodysplastic syndrome, our Phase 2 CART-ddBCMA pivotal trial in patients with r/r MM, and presenting longer-term patient data from our Phase 1 CART-ddBCMA expansion trial in r/r MM. The capstone of our progress is driven by
our <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">D-Domain</FONT> technology which allows us to choose the right approach for the right indication, and we believe this differentiates our programs from traditional <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">CAR-T</FONT>
therapies.&#148; </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:18pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>Recent Business Highlights </B></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>Clinical results from CART-ddBCMA Phase 1 study published in </B><B><I>Blood Advances</I></B><B>. </B>On May&nbsp;9, 2022, Arcellx announced the publication
of clinical results from the dose escalation cohorts of its CART-ddBCMA Phase 1 study in patients with r/r MM in <I>Blood Advances,</I> the open-access journal of the American Society of Hematology. The data demonstrate 100% ORR and 75% CR/sCR; and
evidence of durable clinical benefit in a population with poor prognostic features were observed in the dose escalation cohorts with CART-ddBCMA. The full online publication can be accessed here. </P>

<Center><DIV STYLE="width:8.5in" align="left">
 <P STYLE="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>Dosed first patient in Phase 1 clinical trial evaluating <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">ACLX-001</FONT>
utilizing the <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">ARC-SparX</FONT> platform for the treatment of patients with r/r MM. </B>On May&nbsp;10, 2022, Arcellx announced that the first patient was dosed in its open-label, multicenter Phase 1 clinical trial
(NCT04155749) to evaluate the company&#146;s novel dosable and controllable <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">ARC-SparX</FONT> program in patients with r/r MM. <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">ARC-SparX</FONT> is comprised of SparX (soluble protein
antigen receptor <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">X-linkers)</FONT> proteins engineered to target BCMA on myeloma cells together with <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">ARC-T</FONT> (Antigen Receptor <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">Complex-T)</FONT>
cells that are dosed separately and engineered to activate only when engaged with a SparX protein bound to a myeloma cell. Both the ARC cells and the SparX proteins utilize the company&#146;s proprietary novel synthetic binding scaffold called the <FONT
STYLE="white-space:nowrap">D-Domain.</FONT> </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>CART-ddBCMA accepted as an oral abstract presentation at the 2022 American Society of Clinical Oncology
(ASCO) Annual Meeting. </B>On April&nbsp;27, 2022, Arcellx announced the presentation of new clinical data from its CART-ddBCMA Phase 1 trial in patients with relapsed or refractory multiple myeloma in an oral abstract session at the 2022 American
Society of Clinical Oncology (ASCO) Annual Meeting taking place June <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">3-7,</FONT> 2022, in Chicago, Illinois. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:18pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><U>Oral
Presentation Details: </U></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>Title:</B>&nbsp;Phase 1 Study of CART-ddBCMA in Relapsed or Refractory Multiple Myeloma <B> </B></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>Speaker:</B>&nbsp;Matthew J. Frigault, M.D., Assistant Director of the Cellular Therapy Service at Massachusetts General Cancer Center, and Instructor at
Harvard Medical School <B> </B></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>Session Type/Title:</B>&nbsp;Oral Abstract Session/Hematologic Malignancies&#151;Plasma Cell Dyscrasia <B> </B></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>Session Date:</B>&nbsp;Sunday, June&nbsp;5, 2022 <B> </B></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>Session Time:</B> 8:00 a.m. &#150; 11:00 a.m. CDT <B> </B></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>Location:</B>&nbsp;McCormick Place Convention Center, Chicago, Illinois&nbsp;<B> </B></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>Abstract Number:</B>&nbsp;8003 </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>Live webcast event with
management and panel of clinician experts. </B>On Sunday, June&nbsp;5, 2022, at 7:00 p.m. CDT, Arcellx will host a live webcast event with an expert panel of clinicians to discuss the clinical results from its CART-ddBCMA study being presented
during ASCO. The event will be accessible from Arcellx&#146;s website at&nbsp;www.arcellx.com&nbsp;in the Investors section. A replay of the webcast will be archived and available for 30 days following the event. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:18pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>First Quarter 2022 Financial Highlights </B></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>Cash, cash
equivalents, and marketable securities: </B> </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">As of March&nbsp;31, 2022, Arcellx had cash, cash equivalents, and marketable securities of
$210.9&nbsp;million, which is anticipated to fund its operations into the second half of 2023. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>R&amp;D expenses: </B> </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Research and development expenses were $24.4&nbsp;million and $8.5&nbsp;million for the quarters ended March&nbsp;31, 2022 and 2021, respectively, an increase
of $15.9&nbsp;million. This increase was driven by higher external costs associated with the advancement of our CART-ddBCMA clinical program, preclinical development of our other pipeline candidates, and increased headcount. </P>

<p style="margin-top:1em; margin-bottom:0em; page-break-before:always">

<Center><DIV STYLE="width:8.5in" align="left">
 <P STYLE="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>G&amp;A expenses: </B></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">General and administrative expenses were $8.0&nbsp;million and $2.8&nbsp;million for the quarters ended March&nbsp;31, 2022 and 2021, respectively, an increase
of $5.2&nbsp;million. This increase was driven by increased headcount, professional fees related to consulting and accounting, audit services, and other expenses. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:18pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>Net loss: </B></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Net loss was $32.4&nbsp;million and
$11.3&nbsp;million for the quarters ended March&nbsp;31, 2022 and 2021, respectively. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:18pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>About the <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">ARC-SparX</FONT>
Platform Technology </B></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">The <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">ARC-SparX</FONT> platform is designed to allow for controllability and adaptability to
potentially reduce toxicities that are often associated with serious dose-limiting adverse events and to overcome tumor heterogeneity. It is a modular therapy which utilizes a universal <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">ARC-T</FONT> cell combined
with an <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap"><FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">off-the-shelf</FONT></FONT> SparX protein to separate the tumor-recognition and tumor-killing functions. SparX (soluble protein antigen-receptor
<FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">X-linkers)</FONT> proteins utilize our <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">D-Domain</FONT> technology engineered to recognize antigens on the surface of diseased cells and flags those cells for detection by the <FONT
STYLE="white-space:nowrap">ARC-T</FONT> cells. <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">ARC-T</FONT> cells express a <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">D-Domain-based</FONT> CAR engineered to specifically recognize a unique TAG in the SparX protein. <FONT
STYLE="white-space:nowrap">ARC-T</FONT> cells are dosed separately and only activated to kill the target cell when they encounter a SparX protein bound to the target antigen and thus are controlled through SparX dose modulation. Arcellx has
developed a collection of SparX proteins that bind different antigens on the surface of diseased cells. Multiple SparX proteins with different antigen specificity can be administered to potentially address antigen heterogeneity or antigen escape
that contribute to relapsed and refractory disease. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:18pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>About Arcellx, Inc. </B></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Arcellx, Inc. is a clinical-stage biotechnology company reimagining cell therapy by engineering innovative immunotherapies for patients with cancer and other
incurable diseases. Arcellx believes that cell therapies are one of the forward pillars of medicine and Arcellx&#146;s mission is to advance humanity by developing cell therapies that are safer, more effective, and more broadly accessible.
Arcellx&#146;s lead product candidate, CART-ddBCMA, is being developed for the treatment of relapsed or refractory multiple myeloma (r/r MM) in an ongoing Phase 1 study. CART-ddBCMA has been granted Fast Track, Orphan Drug, and Regenerative Medicine
Advanced Therapy designations by the U.S. Food and Drug Administration. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Arcellx is also advancing its dosable and controllable <FONT
STYLE="white-space:nowrap">CAR-T</FONT> therapy, <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">ARC-SparX,</FONT> through two programs: a Phase 1 study of <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">ACLX-001</FONT> for r/r MM, initiated in the second quarter of 2022; and <FONT
STYLE="white-space:nowrap">ACLX-002</FONT> in relapsed or refractory acute myeloid leukemia and high-risk myelodysplastic syndrome, expected to enter the clinic in the second half of 2022. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Visit&nbsp;www.arcellx.com&nbsp;for more information. </P>
