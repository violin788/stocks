 </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman" ALIGN="center"><B>Arcellx Provides Fourth Quarter and <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">Year-End</FONT> 2024 Financial

pivotal <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">iMMagine-1</FONT> study of <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">anito-cel</FONT> in patients with RRMM which demonstrated 97% ORR and 62% CR/sCR at a median <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">follow-up</FONT> of

<P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman" ALIGN="center">&#151; <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">iMMagine-1</FONT> update planned for presentation <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">mid-year</FONT> 2025
&#151; </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman" ALIGN="center">&#151; Commercial launch of <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">anito-cel</FONT> in RRMM planned for 2026 &#151; </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman" ALIGN="center">&#151; Ended the quarter with $626M, reiterating cash runway into 2027 &#151; </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>REDWOOD CITY, Calif., February</B><B></B><B>&nbsp;27, 2025 </B>(BUSINESS WIRE) &#150; Arcellx, Inc. (NASDAQ: ACLX), a biotechnology company reimagining
cell therapy through the development of innovative immunotherapies for patients with cancer and other incurable diseases, today reported business highlights and financial results for the fourth quarter and year ended December&nbsp;31, 2024. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">&#147;2024 was a transformational year for Arcellx as our ASH data presentations for <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">anito-cel,</FONT> in partnership with
Kite, continued to demonstrate <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">anito-cel&#146;s</FONT> differentiated clinical profile for the potential treatment of patients with relapsed or refractory multiple myeloma,&#148; said Rami Elghandour, Arcellx&#146;s
Chairman and Chief Executive Officer. &#147;In our Phase 1 and <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">iMMagine-1</FONT> studies, data demonstrate that <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">anito-cel</FONT> has the potential to provide a meaningful benefit to a
broad population of patients. Our Phase 1 study demonstrated that the deep responses observed with <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">anito-cel</FONT> also translated to durable benefit for patients with a 30.2 month median progression-free survival
in a challenging patient cohort and the preliminary data from our pivotal <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">iMMagine-1</FONT> study demonstrated <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">anito-cel</FONT> also delivered deep responses for late line myeloma
patients with a 97% overall response rate and 62% complete response rate at a median <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">follow-up</FONT> of 9.5 months. Most notably, as presented during ASH, the safety profile for
<FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">anito-cel</FONT> continues to be manageable with no delayed neurotoxicities, including no Parkinsonism, no cranial nerve palsies, and no Guillain-Barr&eacute; syndrome in the155 patients dosed across the Phase 1 and <FONT
STYLE="white-space:nowrap">iMMagine-1</FONT> studies. Physician engagement and enthusiasm for <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">anito-cel</FONT> continues to build following these data presentations and as more sites gain experience with <FONT
STYLE="white-space:nowrap">anito-cel</FONT> through <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">iMMagine-3,</FONT> our earlier-line study. Kite is manufacturing for <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">iMMagine-3,</FONT> and turnaround times are consistent with
Kite commercial products. This year, we look forward to presenting updated data from our <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">iMMagine-1</FONT> study <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">mid-year,</FONT> preparing for the commercial launch of <FONT
STYLE="white-space:nowrap">anito-cel</FONT> in multiple myeloma in 2026, enrolling our program in generalized myasthenia gravis, and expanding our ARC-SparX program in acute myeloid leukemia to include an additional SparX antigen target. Thank you
to our Arcellx team who are willing to embrace challenges and think creatively to make an impact for the patients we serve.&#148; </P>

 <P STYLE="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>Recent Business Progress </B></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>Presented positive preliminary data for the Phase 2 pivotal <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">iMMagine-1</FONT> study of
<FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">anito-cel</FONT> in patients with relapsed or refractory multiple myeloma (RRMM) at the 66th ASH Annual Meeting and Exposition.</B> The data presented on December&nbsp;9, 2024 demonstrated deep and durable responses
with a predictable and manageable safety profile in a high-risk fourth-line or higher (4L+) RRMM population, including triple- and penta-class refractory disease. The data were from an October&nbsp;31, 2024 data cutoff date, with a median <FONT
STYLE="white-space:nowrap">follow-up</FONT> of 9.5 months for the 86 patients who were evaluable for efficacy based on a <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">follow-up</FONT> of at least two months after treatment with
<FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">anito-cel,</FONT> and 98 patients were evaluable for safety based on a <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">follow-up</FONT> of at least one month after treatment with <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">anito-cel.</FONT>
</P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Overall response rate was 97% (83/86) with a complete response/stringent complete response rate of 62% (53/86) and a very good partial response or higher
rate of 81% (70/86), per International Myeloma Working Group (IMWG) criteria as investigator-assessed. Of those evaluable for minimal residual disease (MRD) testing, 93.1% (54/58) achieved MRD negativity at a minimum of 10<SUP
STYLE="font-size:75%; vertical-align:top">&nbsp;-5</SUP>&nbsp;sensitivity. Median progression-free survival (mPFS) and overall survival (OS) were not reached; <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">6-month</FONT> PFS and OS rates were 93.3% and 96.5%,
respectively, and <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">12-month</FONT> PFS and OS rates were 78.5% and 96.5%, respectively. No delayed or <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">non-ICANS</FONT> neurotoxicities, including no Parkinsonism, no cranial nerve
palsies, and no Guillain-Barr&eacute; syndrome, were observed in the 155 patients dosed with <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">anito-cel.</FONT> </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:18pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>Fourth

equivalents, and marketable securities will fund its operations into 2027. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:18pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>Collaboration revenue: </B></P>

<P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Research and development expenses were $44.6&nbsp;million and $28.8&nbsp;million for the quarters ended December&nbsp;31, 2024 and 2023, respectively, an

 <P STYLE="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>About Multiple Myeloma </B></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Multiple Myeloma (MM)&nbsp;is a type of hematological cancer in which diseased plasma cells proliferate and accumulate in the bone marrow, crowding out healthy
blood cells and causing bone lesions, loss of bone density, and bone fractures. These abnormal plasma cells also produce excessive quantities of an abnormal immunoglobulin fragment, called a myeloma protein (M protein), causing kidney damage and
impairing the patient&#146;s immune function. Multiple myeloma is the third most common hematological malignancy in the United States and Europe, representing approximately 10% of all hematological cancer cases and 20% of deaths due to hematological
malignancies. The median age of patients at diagnosis is 69 years with <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">one-third</FONT> of patients diagnosed at an age of at least 75 years. Because MM tends to afflict patients at an advanced stage of life,
patients often have multiple <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">co-morbidities</FONT> and toxicities that can quickly escalate and become life-endangering. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:18pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>About anitocabtagene autoleucel <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">(anito-cel)</FONT> </B></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Anitocabtagene autoleucel <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">(anito-cel,</FONT> previously CART-ddBCMA) is the first BCMA-directed CAR <FONT
STYLE="white-space:nowrap">T-cell</FONT> therapy to be investigated in multiple myeloma that utilizes Arcellx&#146;s novel and compact binder known as the <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">D-Domain.</FONT> The small, stable <FONT
STYLE="white-space:nowrap">D-Domain</FONT> binder enables high CAR expression without tonic signaling and is designed to quickly release from the BCMA target. This combination may allow for the effective elimination of multiple myeloma cells without
severe immunotoxicity. <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">Anito-cel</FONT> has been granted Fast Track, Orphan Drug, and Regenerative Medicine Advanced Therapy Designations by the U.S. Food and Drug Administration. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:18pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>About Arcellx and Kite Collaboration </B></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Arcellx and Kite, a
Gilead Company, formed a global strategic collaboration and license agreement to <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">co-develop</FONT> and <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">co-commercialize</FONT> <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">anito-cel</FONT> for
patients with relapsed or refractory multiple myeloma (RRMM). <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">Anito-cel</FONT> is currently being developed in a Phase 2 registrational pivotal study and a Phase 3 randomized controlled study for RRMM. Kite and
Arcellx will jointly commercialize the <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">anito-cel</FONT> asset in the United States, and Kite will commercialize the product outside the United States. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:18pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>About Arcellx, Inc. </B></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Arcellx, Inc. is a clinical-stage
biotechnology company reimagining cell therapy by engineering innovative immunotherapies for patients with cancer and other incurable diseases. Arcellx believes that cell therapies are one of the forward pillars of medicine and Arcellx&#146;s
mission is to advance humanity by developing cell therapies that are safer, more effective, and more broadly accessible. For more information on Arcellx, please visit <U>www.arcellx.com</U>. Follow Arcellx on X @arcellx and LinkedIn. </P>
