Baker, who served as the Company&#8217;s Senior Vice President, Chief Legal and Compliance Officer until January 5, 2024, notified the Company that he will retire in early 2025. <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman" ALIGN="center"><I>All business operations organized by markets in The Americas and Europe supporting strategic goals </I></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman" ALIGN="center"><I>Adrian Downes to step down as Chief Financial Officer; National search commences for next CFO </I></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman" ALIGN="center"><I>Succession plan completed for Chief Legal Officer </I></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>Lake Oswego, Oregon, January</B><B></B><B>&nbsp;8, 2024</B> &#150;&nbsp;The Greenbrier Companies, Inc. (NYSE:GBX) today announced a new
business operations organizational structure supporting its &#147;Better Together&#148; business strategy, unveiled at its April 2023 Investor Day. The streamlined organization is designed to drive organizational effectiveness, improve efficiency,
leverage best practices and improve customer experience to drive greater shareholder value. The company also announced planned changes in the Chief Financial Officer (CFO) role and the completion of a succession plan for the leadership of its Legal
function. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Lorie Tekorius, CEO and President said, &#147;Since Greenbrier&#146;s formation in the 1980s, we have grown to lead in all
markets where we operate. Now is the time to leverage the benefits of scale and standardization. The changes being announced today will bring greater focus and accountability to our operating units while driving efficiency in our enabling functions.
To advance to Greenbrier&#146;s next growth phase, we must continue collaborating and working Better Together as a unified organization.&#148; </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Greenbrier&#146;s structure now frames its operating activities in two distinct geographies: The Americas and Europe. Brian Comstock, a <FONT
STYLE="white-space:nowrap">26-year</FONT> Greenbrier veteran with more than four decades of railway industry experience, assumes responsibility for all operations in The Americas, which includes the United States, Mexico, Canada and Brazil. William
Glenn, who has spent more than 20 years at Greenbrier in various roles, assumes responsibility for all operations in Europe. Greenbrier&#146;s Board of Directors has appointed Comstock as Executive Vice President&nbsp;&amp; President, The Americas
and Glenn as Senior Vice President&nbsp;&amp; President, Europe. Both Comstock and Glenn continue to report to Tekorius. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">&#147;I am
confident changes in our business operations will support progress on our strategic priorities and Greenbrier will continue to excel as we simplify our structure. These changes are intended to support and empower Greenbrier employees across the
organization. The new organizational design of our business operations provides the right platform for the next phase of our growth and to deliver value for all Greenbrier stakeholders, especially our employees, customers and shareholders,&#148;
Tekorius said. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman" ALIGN="center"><I>-More-</I> </P>

<P STYLE="margin-top:18pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>About Greenbrier </B></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Greenbrier, headquartered in Lake Oswego, Oregon, is a leading international supplier of equipment and services to global freight
transportation markets. Through its wholly-owned subsidiaries and joint ventures, Greenbrier designs, builds and markets freight railcars in North America, Europe and Brazil. We are a leading provider of freight railcar wheel services, parts,
maintenance and retrofitting services in North America through our maintenance services business unit. Greenbrier owns a lease fleet of approximately 14,100 railcars that originate primarily from Greenbrier&#146;s manufacturing operations.
Greenbrier offers railcar management, regulatory compliance services and leasing services to railroads and other railcar owners in North America. Learn more about Greenbrier at <U>www.gbrx.com</U>. </P>
