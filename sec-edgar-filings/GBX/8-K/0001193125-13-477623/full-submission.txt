<TD ALIGN="left" VALIGN="top"><B>Compensatory Arrangements of Certain Officers. </B></TD></TR></TABLE> <P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>(e) Compensatory Arrangements of Certain Officers.
</B></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">On December&nbsp;13, 2013 we entered into an amendment to the employment agreement with William A. Furman, our President and Chief
Executive Officer. The amendment modifies the calculation and timing of payment of the pro-rated bonus that Mr.&nbsp;Furman would be entitled to receive in the event of his termination without cause, in order to enable the pro-rated bonus to qualify
as &#147;performance-based compensation&#148; for purposes of Internal Revenue Code 162(m), and be fully deductible by the Company. As amended, the pro-rated bonus payable in respect of the year in which Mr.&nbsp;Furman&#146;s employment so
terminates shall be calculated solely based on achievement of performance measures and shall be paid at the time bonuses are otherwise payable under the Company&#146;s bonus program. In addition, the amendment deletes acceleration of vesting of
performance-based restricted stock units upon termination of Mr.&nbsp;Furman&#146;s employment without cause. As amended, any performance-based restricted stock units held by Mr.&nbsp;Furman at the time of such termination of employment will
continue to vest, if at all, based upon the achievement of the applicable performance measures during the applicable performance measurement period. </P>
