<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt"><U><B>Discussion of Quarterly Results and Outlook</B></U>
</DIV>


<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;William A. Furman, president and chief executive officer, said, &#147;As anticipated, we returned
to profitability during the quarter, excluding the one-time charge associated with retiring our
$235&nbsp;million senior unsecured notes. However, these results did not fully meet our expectations,
principally due to a temporary shortage of castings in North America and a temporary delay in
certification of railcars in Europe, which dampened new railcar deliveries by about 300 units. In
addition, about $2&nbsp;million of certain other non-recurring general &#038; administrative costs were
incurred during the third quarter.&#148;
</DIV>

<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Furman added, &#147;Business momentum continues in what we believe is the early stage of an upturn
in the markets we serve. Revenue in our Manufacturing and Wheel Services, Refurbishment &#038; Parts
segments and lease rates on our lease fleet have grown for the third consecutive quarter, driven by
stronger demand for our products and services. Business visibility continues to improve,
particularly in new railcar manufacturing, where we are experiencing a cyclical recovery and
benefitting from the strength of our diversified and expanded product portfolio and the ramping up
of additional capacity. Since the beginning of our fiscal year, we have received new railcar orders
for over 14,000 units, 6,400 of which were received during the third quarter. We are also on track
with our plans to open an additional production line in July&nbsp;2011 that will provide more capacity
and a lower cost manufacturing footprint. We currently expect to deliver around 9,300 new railcars
in fiscal 2011.&#148;
</DIV>

<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Furman concluded, &#147;We remain on track to achieve our fiscal 2011 objectives. In the fourth
quarter, we will continue to focus on expanding gross margins, improving working capital usage and
operational efficiencies, meeting increased demand and enhancing our leasing platform. We expect
to produce increased earnings leverage as we continue to benefit from operating at higher plant
utilization levels, and achieve economies of scale. Our recent debt refinancing strengthened our
balance sheet and will save us over $10&nbsp;million in annual pre-tax interest expense.&#148;
</DIV>
<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt"><U><B>Segment Details</B></U>
</DIV>


<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Manufacturing segment consists of marine and new railcar production in Europe and North
America. Manufacturing segment revenue for the third quarter was $173.5&nbsp;million, compared to $77.9
million in the third quarter of 2010. The revenue increase was primarily due to higher railcar

<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt"><U><B>Liquidity Update</B></U>
</DIV>


<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;During the quarter, the Company enhanced and optimized its balance sheet, cash flow and
liquidity through the sale of $230&nbsp;million of 3.5% senior convertible notes due 2018, which were
convertible into shares of Greenbrier common stock at an initial conversion price of $38.05 per
share. The proceeds were used to fully retire $235&nbsp;million of outstanding 8 3/8% Senior Notes due
2015. The difference in cash interest expense between the newly issued notes and the retired notes
will be over $10&nbsp;million annually. A charge of $10.0&nbsp;million was recorded to Other costs during
the third quarter for the write-off of unamortized debt acquisition costs, prepayment premiums and
other costs associated with the full retirement of the $235&nbsp;million senior unsecured notes.
</DIV>


<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Subsequent to quarter end, Greenbrier completed the refinancing of its North American credit
facility and fully repaid $72&nbsp;million outstanding under a term loan due June&nbsp;2012. The new
facility is a five-year, $245&nbsp;million revolving line of credit maturing June&nbsp;30, 2016. Borrowings
under the facility currently bear interest at Libor &#043; 2.50%. During the fourth quarter, the
Company expects to take a one-time charge of about $6&nbsp;million, pre-tax, related to the early
retirement of the term loan and revolving line of credit.

<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As a result of these refinancings, the Company extended its average loan maturity date from
three to five years and added nearly $75&nbsp;million of liquidity and borrowing capacity. As
previously noted, the Company will also realize $10&nbsp;million per annum in cash interest savings.
