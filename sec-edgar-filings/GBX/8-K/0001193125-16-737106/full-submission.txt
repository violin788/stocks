<TD ALIGN="left" VALIGN="top"><B>Other Events. </B></TD></TR></TABLE> <P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">On October 11, 2016, The Greenbrier Companies, Inc. (the &#147;<U>Registrant</U>&#148; or
&#147;<U>Greenbrier</U>&#148;) and Astra Rail Management GmbH (&#147;<U>Astra</U>,&#148; Greenbrier and Astra are referred to herein collectively as the &#147;<U>Parties</U>&#148;), through wholly owned subsidiaries, entered into definitive
agreements for the formation of a joint venture (the &#147;<U>Joint Venture</U>&#148;) of the Parties&#146; Europe-based freight railcar businesses.&nbsp;The Joint Venture will include all European freight railcar operations of Greenbrier and Astra,
and will be controlled by Greenbrier as owner of approximately seventy-five percent of equity interests, with Astra owning approximately twenty-five percent.&nbsp;Upon closing, the combined enterprise will offer an expanded manufacturing platform in
Europe with greater scale and efficiency and the capability to serve growth opportunities in new and growing railcar markets.&nbsp; </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">The Parties entered
into a Joint Venture Investment Agreement (the &#147;<U>Investment Agreement</U>&#148;) pursuant to which each Party will contribute assets in exchange for their ownership interests in the Joint Venture.&nbsp;Pursuant to the Investment Agreement,
Greenbrier, through its wholly owned subsidiary, Greenbrier Europe B.V., will contribute to a newly formed Dutch (BV) holding company 100% of its interests in Greenbrier Germany GmbH and Wagony Swidnica S.A. (&#147;Wagony Swidnica&#148;), including
Wagony Swidnica&#146;s wholly owned subsidiaries, Zaklad Transportu Kolejowego Siarkopol sp. z&nbsp;o.o. and Zaklad Naprawczy Taboru Kolejowego Olawa sp. z&nbsp;o.o., together with all their associated assets and certain liabilities, intellectual
property and employees.&nbsp;Greenbrier will pay Astra &#128;60&nbsp;million as additional consideration in respect of Greenbrier&#146;s controlling interest in the Joint Venture, with &#128;30&nbsp;million to be paid at closing and the remaining
&#128;30&nbsp;million (plus interest at five percent per annum) to be paid twelve months after closing. Astra will contribute its railcar manufacturing facilities in Arad, Severin and Caracal, Romania and its Slovakian affiliate company, together
with all associated assets and certain liabilities, intellectual property and employees.</P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">The Investment Agreement contains customary terms, conditions,
representations and warranties, including representations and warranties regarding each Party&#146;s respective freight railcar business and the assets contributed to the Joint Venture or otherwise to be operated by the Joint Venture after the
closing. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">The Investment Agreement also contemplates that at closing, Greenbrier and Astra will enter into a Shareholders Agreement relating to the
relationship between Greenbrier and Astra as shareholders of the Joint Venture, and that Greenbrier will provide a Guarantee in connection with the &#128;30 million to be paid to Astra twelve months after the closing. </P>

<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>Lake Oswego, Oregon, October 13, 2016 </B>&#150;&nbsp;The Greenbrier Companies, Inc. (NYSE: GBX) and Astra Rail Management GmbH today
announced plans to form a new company, Greenbrier-Astra Rail, that will create an end-to-end, Europe-based freight railcar manufacturing, engineering and repair business. The combined enterprise will be formed between Greenbrier&#146;s European
operations headquartered in Swidnica, Poland and Astra Rail based in Germany and Arad, Romania.&nbsp;It will be led by an experienced Europe-based management team from both companies.&nbsp;Greenbrier-Astra Rail will offer manufacturing and service
capability in Europe with greater scale and efficiency for current customers.&nbsp;It also provides the opportunity to pursue growth in railcar markets in the Gulf Cooperation Council (GCC) nations and Eurasia.&nbsp;As partial consideration for its
majority interest, Greenbrier will pay Astra Rail &#128;30 million at closing and &#128;30 million 12 months after closing.&nbsp;Greenbrier expects the transaction to be accretive to earnings per share by the end of fiscal year 2017. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Greenbrier-Astra Rail will be controlled by Greenbrier with an approximate 75% interest.&nbsp;Astra Rail&#146;s Chairman Thomas Manns will own
the remainder of the new company.&nbsp;In addition to his ownership stake, Manns will become Chairman of the Supervisory Board of Greenbrier-Astra Rail and will lead the new company&#146;s commercial operations, working closely with its Management
Board and Jim Cowan, President of Greenbrier International.&nbsp;Also serving on the Supervisory Board will be Bill Furman, Chairman and CEO of Greenbrier; Alejandro Centurion, President of Greenbrier Global Manufacturing Operations; and
Cowan.&nbsp;Daily operations will be led by a Management Board including Bernd B&ouml;se, CEO of Astra Rail, who will become CEO and President, and Bogdan Lesnianski, CFO and the head of Greenbrier&#146;s Wagony Swidnica operations, who will become
CFO.&nbsp;The leadership team has decades of experience in railcar markets throughout Eastern and Western Europe, as well as experience in emerging markets around the world. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Greenbrier-Astra Rail will include all European operations of Greenbrier and Astra Rail.&nbsp;Currently, Greenbrier and Astra Rail each
operate three locations performing new railcar manufacturing, engineering and repair services.&nbsp;Greenbrier&#146;s sites are in Poland and Astra Rail&#146;s sites are in Romania.&nbsp;Greenbrier also maintains a sales office in
Germany.&nbsp;Additionally, Astra Rail performs railcar design at a facility in Slovakia.&nbsp;Contracts with GCC-based customers for US-style freight railcars will continue to be designed and manufactured under the direction of Greenbrier&#146;s
senior US manufacturing team. </P>
