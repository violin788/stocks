<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Greenbrier today provided updated and additional preliminary unaudited selected financial
results for its second fiscal quarter ended February&nbsp;28, 2011. These updated results are consistent with
the preliminary results for the quarter previously announced by Greenbrier on March&nbsp;17, 2011.
Based on Greenbrier&#146;s initial closing for the quarter, preliminary revenues are expected to be
approximately $286&nbsp;million and Greenbrier expects a net loss attributable to Greenbrier of
approximately $0.6&nbsp;million, or a loss of $.02 per  share
for the second  quarter.
Greenbrier&#146;s Adjusted EBITDA for the second fiscal quarter is anticipated to be approximately $19.4
million.
</DIV>

<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;These quarterly results are subject to further review by Greenbrier and should be considered
preliminary and subject to change, as Greenbrier is still in the process of preparing its financial
statements for the quarter ended February&nbsp;28, 2011.
</DIV>

<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Greenbrier currently expects to hold its regularly scheduled earnings conference call on April
7, 2011. Greenbrier anticipates filing its Form 10-Q for the second quarter of fiscal 2011 on or
before April&nbsp;11, 2011.
</DIV>

<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Adjusted EBITDA is not a financial measure under generally accepted accounting principles
(GAAP). Greenbrier defines Adjusted EBITDA as earnings (loss)&nbsp;attributable to Greenbrier before
depreciation and amortization, income tax (benefit)&nbsp;expense and interest and foreign exchange. You
should not consider Adjusted EBITDA in isolation or as a substitute for other financial statement
data determined in accordance with GAAP. In addition, because Adjusted EBITDA is not a measure of
financial performance under GAAP and is susceptible to varying calculations, the Adjusted EBITDA
measure presented may differ from and may not be comparable to similarly titled measures used by
other companies. A reconciliation of the loss&nbsp;attributable to Greenbrier to Adjusted
EBITDA is set forth in the supplemental disclosure below.
</DIV>

performance. Adjusted EBITDA is not a financial measure under generally accepted accounting
principles (GAAP). Greenbrier defines Adjusted EBITDA as net earnings (loss)&nbsp;attributable to
Greenbrier before depreciation and amortization, income tax (benefit)&nbsp;expense, and interest and
foreign exchange. Adjusted EBITDA is a performance measurement tool commonly used by rail supply
companies and Greenbrier. You should not consider Adjusted EBITDA in isolation or as a substitute
for net earnings (loss)&nbsp;attributable to Greenbrier or other financial statement data determined in
accordance with GAAP. In addition, because Adjusted EBITDA is not a measure of financial
performance under GAAP and is susceptible to varying calculations, the Adjusted EBITDA measure
presented may differ from and may not be comparable to similarly titled measures used by other
companies. The Adjusted EBITDA estimate for the three months ended February&nbsp;28, 2011 and the
estimated line items used to derive the Adjusted EBITDA estimate are preliminary and are subject
to further review and changes. The final actual results for the second quarter ended February&nbsp;28,
2011 may vary from these estimates.
