</TABLE> <P STYLE="margin-top:12px;margin-bottom:0px" ALIGN="center"><FONT STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2"><B>Greenbrier to retire $52.9 million of Convertible Senior Notes </B></FONT></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:6px;margin-bottom:0px" ALIGN="center"><FONT STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2"><B></B><I></I><B><I>Action furthers strategic initiative to reduce capital employed and pay down debt</I></B><I></I><B> </B></FONT></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12px;margin-bottom:0px; text-indent:4%"><FONT STYLE="font-family:Times New Roman" SIZE="2"><B>Lake Oswego, Oregon, May&nbsp;14, 2013</B> &#150; The Greenbrier Companies, Inc. (NYSE:GBX) (&#147;Greenbrier&#148; or the
&#147;Company&#148;) today announced the expected retirement of $52.9 million of the Company&#146;s $67.7 million outstanding 2.375% Convertible Senior Notes due 2026 (the &#147;Notes&#148;). This action furthers Greenbrier&#146;s strategic
initiatives, previously announced on April&nbsp;4, 2013, which include reducing capital employed in its operations and paying down debt. The Company will use cash generated from operations and borrowings on its existing revolving credit facilities
to purchase the Notes. It is being done pursuant to the terms of the Notes which require Greenbrier to purchase all of the Notes validly tendered (the &#147;Put Option&#148;) at a price equal to 100% of the principal amount of the Notes, plus any
