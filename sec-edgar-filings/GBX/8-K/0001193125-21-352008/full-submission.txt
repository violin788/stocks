</TABLE> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman" ALIGN="center"><B>U. S. Steel, Norfolk Southern and Greenbrier </B></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman" ALIGN="center"><B>collaborate on new, sustainable high-strength steel railcar </B></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman" ALIGN="center"><I>Reduced railcar weight lessens carbon emissions; Improved design extends useful life </I></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>Lake Oswego, Oregon, December</B><B></B><B>&nbsp;9, 2021</B> &#150; Three of America&#146;s leading industrial and transportation companies
&#150; United States Steel Corporation (NYSE: X), Norfolk Southern Corporation (NYSE: NSC) and The Greenbrier Companies, Inc. (NYSE: GBX) &#150; today jointly announced a new, more sustainable steel gondola railcar. Using an innovative formula for
high-strength, lighter-weight steel developed by U. S. Steel, each gondola&#146;s unloaded weight is reduced by up to 15,000 pounds. Gondola railcars transport loose bulk material such as metal scraps, coils, wood chips, steel slabs and ore. Norfolk
Southern will initially acquire 800 of the Greenbrier engineered gondolas. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">The partnership between supplier, builder and <FONT
STYLE="white-space:nowrap">end-user</FONT> is rooted in the recognition that North America&#146;s aging gondola fleet will soon require substantial replacement with a more sustainable design. U. S. Steel&#146;s materials innovation, Norfolk
Southern&#146;s commitment to sustainable freight transportation and Greenbrier&#146;s deep engineering capability have led to a stronger, lighter and more energy-efficient product for the freight rail industry. U. S. Steel is a leader in designing
advanced, lightweight and high-strength steel solutions for the automotive industry. It identified the interconnections between steel advancements for automobiles and adapted these to the rail industry. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Features of the newly designed gondolas provide several benefits: </P> <P STYLE="font-size:6pt;margin-top:0pt;margin-bottom:0pt">&nbsp;</P>

<TD ALIGN="left" VALIGN="top"> <P ALIGN="left" STYLE=" margin-top:0pt ; margin-bottom:0pt; font-family:Times New Roman; font-size:10pt"><B>Extended Lifecycle: </B>The new steel is twice as strong as traditional steel used in the railcar
manufacturing process, potentially extending the useful life of each gondola to 50 years. A stronger external finish also strengthens the railcar body, lowering maintenance costs. </P></TD></TR></TABLE>
<P STYLE="font-size:6pt;margin-top:0pt;margin-bottom:0pt">&nbsp;</P>

steel fabrication process requires less time, leading to improved energy efficiency due to less material being used. The lower weight decreases the fuel needed by locomotives hauling the railcars, reducing energy usage and lowering emissions.
</P></TD></TR></TABLE> <P STYLE="font-size:6pt;margin-top:0pt;margin-bottom:0pt">&nbsp;</P>

high-strength steel requires less structural reinforcement, simplifying the manufacturing process and allowing more total cargo space in each railcar. </P></TD></TR></TABLE>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">&#147;This remarkable collaboration with our partners at Norfolk Southern and Greenbrier is helping realize the full potential of U. S.

<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Moving freight by rail is the most environmentally friendly mode of surface transport, with the average U. S. railroad moving one ton of
