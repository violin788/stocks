</TABLE> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:14pt; font-family:Times New Roman" ALIGN="center"><B>Greenbrier Announces Board of Directors Election Results </B></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:5%; font-size:12pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>Lake Oswego, Oregon, January</B><B></B><B>&nbsp;10, 2022</B> &#150; The&nbsp;Greenbrier Companies, Inc. (NYSE:GBX) announced today the <FONT
STYLE="white-space:nowrap">re-election</FONT> of Admiral Thomas B. Fargo, Ambassador Antonio O. Garza and James R. Huffines to its Board of Directors, bringing Greenbrier&#146;s total number of directors to 10. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:5%; font-size:12pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Admiral Fargo has served on the Greenbrier board since 2015 and as Lead Director since January 2021. He is also the Chair of Greenbrier&#146;s
Compensation Committee. Admiral Fargo provides significant executive leadership, operational and manufacturing expertise to the Board. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:5%; font-size:12pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Ambassador Garza has served as a Board Member since July 2021. He served as the U.S. Ambassador to Mexico from 2002 to 2009 and provides
expertise in the North American rail industry as a Board member of Kansas City Southern. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:5%; font-size:12pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Mr.&nbsp;Huffines has served as a Board Member
since July 2021 and has more than 35 years of experience in the banking and finance sectors. He also brings public company and governmental expertise to the Board. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:5%; font-size:12pt; font-family:Times New Roman">&#147;Tom, Antonio and James each bring unique experience from a range of sectors to our Board,&#148; said William A. Furman, Greenbrier
Chairman and CEO. &#147;Their combined knowledge executing critical business and organizational functions continues to benefit Greenbrier. I am extremely pleased to have these three Directors as Board colleagues during the coming years.&#148; </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:5%; font-size:12pt; font-family:Times New Roman">In addition, two of Greenbrier&#146;s long-term Directors, Duane McDougall and Don Washburn, retired from the Board, effective with the end of
their terms in January 2022. Mr.&nbsp;McDougall joined the Board in 2003 and served as Lead Director from 2014 to 2021. Mr.&nbsp;Washburn joined in 2004, bringing executive management and operational expertise to the Board. Over their tenures,
Greenbrier has grown from a small new railcar builder with under $750&nbsp;million of annual revenue to one of the largest global freight railcar builders with multi-billion-dollar annual revenues. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:5%; font-size:12pt; font-family:Times New Roman">&#147;Over nearly two decades, Duane and Don&#146;s leadership and collaboration has transformed Greenbrier into an international leader in
the rail equipment sector,&#148; said Furman. &#147;We have all benefitted from their proven track record of strategic thinking and focus on execution. On behalf of the Board and Greenbrier&#146;s management, I thank both Don and Duane for their
innumerable contributions to Greenbrier.&#148; </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:30pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:11pt; font-family:Times New Roman" ALIGN="center">- <I>More</I> - </P>
