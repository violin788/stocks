<TD ALIGN="left" VALIGN="top"> <P STYLE=" margin-top:0pt ; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman; " ALIGN="left"><B>Entry into a Material Definitive Agreement. </B></P></TD></TR></TABLE>
<P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">On July&nbsp;26, 2019, The Greenbrier Companies, Inc. (&#147;Greenbrier&#148;) completed its previously disclosed pending acquisition (the &#147;Closing&#148;)
of the railcar and railcar components manufacturing business (the &#147;Business&#148;) from American Railcar Industries, Inc., a North Dakota corporation (&#147;ARI&#148;). </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">In connection with the Closing, Greenbrier issued a $50.0&nbsp;million principal amount unsecured convertible promissory note (the &#147;Convertible
Note&#148;) to ARI as a portion of the purchase price for the Business. The Convertible Note bears interest at an annual rate of&nbsp;2.25% payable in cash semi-annually. The Convertible Note matures five years from the date of issuance, unless
earlier converted in accordance with its terms prior to such date. The Convertible Note is convertible into shares of Greenbrier&#146;s common stock as further described under Item&nbsp;3.02 below.&nbsp;If Greenbrier undergoes certain fundamental
changes prior to its maturity, then the Convertible Note holder will have the option to require Greenbrier to repurchase up to all of the note for cash at 100% of the applicable principal amount, plus accrued and unpaid interest. The Convertible
Note provides for customary events of default as set forth therein. The Convertible Note is a senior unsecured obligation of Greenbrier. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">The foregoing
description of the Convertible Note does not purport to be complete and is subject to, and qualified in its entirety by, the full text of the Convertible Note which is filed as Exhibit 4.1 to this Current Report filed on Form <FONT
STYLE="white-space:nowrap">8-K</FONT> and is incorporated herein by reference. To the extent required by Item 1.01 of Form <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">8-K,</FONT> the information contained in (or incorporated by reference into) Items 2.01, 2.03
and 3.02 of this Current Report filed on Form <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">8-K</FONT> is hereby, respectively, incorporated by reference into this Item 1.01. </P> <P STYLE="font-size:18pt;margin-top:0pt;margin-bottom:0pt">&nbsp;</P>

<TD ALIGN="left" VALIGN="top"> <P STYLE=" margin-top:0pt ; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman; " ALIGN="left"><B>Completion of Acquisition or Disposition of Assets. </B></P></TD></TR></TABLE>
<P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">On July&nbsp;26, 2019, Greenbrier completed its acquisition of the Business from ARI for a purchase price of approximately $410&nbsp;million, subject to
customary post-closing purchase price adjustments. The Purchase Price includes approximately $8&nbsp;million for capital expenditures on railcar lining operations and other facility improvements. In connection with the acquisition, Greenbrier
acquired, among other assets, the manufacturing facilities used in the Business along with certain personal and intangible property primarily used in the Business and assumed certain liabilities related to the acquired assets or primarily related to
the Business. The purchase price was paid for by a combination of cash and the issuance of the Convertible Note. The cash portion of the purchase price of approximately $360&nbsp;million, was funded by a combination of cash on hand and by Greenbrier
borrowing $300&nbsp;million under the term loan further described under Item 2.03 of this Current Report on Form <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">8-K.</FONT> </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">To the extent required by Item 2.01 of Form <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">8-K,</FONT> the information contained in (or incorporated by reference into)

</TABLE> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:14pt; font-family:Times New Roman" ALIGN="center"><B>Greenbrier Completes Acquisition of Manufacturing Business of </B></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:14pt; font-family:Times New Roman" ALIGN="center"><B>American Railcar Industries </B></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman" ALIGN="center"><I>~ Grows Greenbrier&#146;s total U.S. employment base by 66&nbsp;percent ~ </I></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman" ALIGN="center"><I>~ Geographic diversity/broader product portfolio provide efficiencies and access to new markets ~ </I></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman" ALIGN="center"><I>~ ARI&#146;s O&#146;Bryan joins Greenbrier in senior manufacturing role ~ </I></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>Lake Oswego, Oregon,</B><B></B><B>&nbsp;July 29, 2019</B>&nbsp;&#151; The Greenbrier Companies, Inc. (NYSE: GBX) today announced it
completed its acquisition of the manufacturing business of American Railcar Industries (ARI) from ITE Management LP (ITE) on July&nbsp;26, 2019, substantially consistent with the terms of the parties&#146; agreement reached in April. These
operations will be fully incorporated into Greenbrier&#146;s North American business model both from a manufacturing and commercial perspective. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Greenbrier&#146;s U.S. workforce totals approximately 4,000 people, including nearly 1,600 U.S. workers joining from ARI. Prior to the
acquisition, Greenbrier&#146;s U.S. employment included manufacturing and corporate jobs in Oregon and employees located throughout the nation in Leasing&nbsp;&amp; Services and Wheels, Repair&nbsp;&amp; Parts. Greenbrier adds two railcar
manufacturing facilities in Paragould and Marmaduke, Arkansas, as well as manufacturing and administrative employees in St. Charles, Missouri. It adds other operations in Jackson and Kennet, Missouri and Longview, Texas which produce a range of
railcar components and parts, creating enhanced vertical integration for Greenbrier&#146;s manufacturing operations. In addition to substantially increasing Greenbrier&#146;s U.S.-based workforce, the newly-added operations provide us domestic
capacity that insulates Greenbrier from uncertainties related to free trade in North America. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">The combination provides Greenbrier a more
complete product offering in tank cars and covered hoppers through Greenbrier&#146;s integrated business model. Operating lessors, shippers and railroads, all core to Greenbrier&#146;s customer base, can now access Greenbrier&#146;s broad range of
railcar products through either direct sales or creative lease financing solutions, paired with a broad range of after-market services. Geographic expansion provides railcar customers a value proposition that drives efficiencies and lower costs,
such as improved transportation options for finished products. The addition of the ARI manufacturing operations extends Greenbrier&#146;s reputation in design leadership and quality in the North American freight railcar market. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">William A. Furman, Chairman and CEO said, &#147;Acquiring the manufacturing operations of ARI is a major milestone for Greenbrier. The
transaction advances three of our strategic goals: strengthening our presence in the North American rail equipment market, growing at scale and developing a robust talent pipeline. This is Greenbrier&#146;s greatest expansion effort yet. We value
the contributions of employees of both Greenbrier and ARI, our shared customers and other partners who have made this combination possible and who will help us continue to grow.&#148; </P>
