<P STYLE="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:14pt; font-family:Times New Roman" ALIGN="center"><B>Announces Orders for 2,900 Railcars Valued at $470 Million </B></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman" ALIGN="center"><B><I>~ Orders diversified, across multiple railcar types ~ </I></B></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman" ALIGN="center"><B><I>~ Over $130 million of capital returned to shareholders ~ </I></B></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>Lake Oswego, Oregon, October&nbsp;7, 2015</B> &#150; The Greenbrier Companies, Inc. (NYSE:GBX) announced today that it expects to exceed
previously provided diluted EPS guidance of $5.70 to $5.85 (excluding non-recurring costs in the third quarter of $0.16 per share) for the fiscal year ended August&nbsp;31, 2015. These higher expectations are principally driven by increased margins
in the Company&#146;s manufacturing segment, which includes lease syndications and a lower than anticipated tax rate related to geographic mix of earnings. In addition, Greenbrier disclosed it received diversified new orders in its fourth quarter
ended August&nbsp;31, 2015 for 2,900 railcar units valued at $470 million. Orders for the quarter include medium and large cube covered hopper cars, automobile carrying cars, boxcars and tank cars. The tank car orders include a recent award for
1,200 tank cars from Saudi Railway Company (SAR). (More details on the SAR order can be found in a separate news release issued today by the Company.) </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">William A. Furman, Chairman and CEO said, &#147;Our diversified backlog of 41,300 units valued at $4.71 billion as of August&nbsp;31, 2015 is
near all-time highs, giving us visibility well into 2016, 2017 and beyond. Additionally, our recent expansion into Brazil and Saudi Arabia extends our geographical reach into new international markets and further diversifies our business. Production
facilities now include major factories in Mexico to serve North America and Latin America; Brazil which can reach African and Latin American export markets; Poland for Europe, near-Asia, Saudi Arabia and other Middle East markets; as well as our
flagship factory, Gunderson, in the United States.&#148; </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Furman continued, &#147;During our fiscal year 2015, we received orders for
32,400 new railcar units valued at $3.44 billion. The average sales price of $106,000 for the orders received is $11,000 higher than in fiscal year 2014 and is a testament to the value and diversity of railcar types ordered.&#148; </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">&#147;We have recently confirmed production schedules with many of our major customers, including those operating in the energy sector, and
have received no order cancellations. In select </P>
