</TABLE> <P STYLE="margin-top:18pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:12pt; font-family:Times New Roman" ALIGN="center"><B>Admiral Thomas B. Fargo elected to Greenbrier Board of Directors </B></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>Lake Oswego, Oregon, July&nbsp;7, 2015</B> &#150; The Greenbrier Companies (NYSE: GBX) announced today the election of four-star U.S. Navy
Admiral Thomas B. Fargo to the Company&#146;s Board of Directors. The election of Admiral Fargo increases Greenbrier&#146;s Board of Directors to 10 members, eight of whom are independent directors. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Admiral Fargo, who completed his active military service in 2005, brings 35 years of naval leadership and subsequent private sector experience
in maritime and other transportation industries to the Greenbrier Board. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">As commander of the U.S. Pacific Command from 2002 until 2005,
Admiral Fargo led the world&#146;s largest unified command while directing the joint operations of the Army, Navy, Marine Corps and Air Force. In this role Admiral Fargo acted as U.S. military representative for collective defense arrangements in
the Pacific, ultimately responsible to the President and the Secretary of Defense through the chairman, Joint Chiefs of Staff. Admiral Fargo&#146;s naval career included six tours in Washington, D.C. and extensive duties in the Pacific, Indian Ocean
and Middle East including serving as Commander-in-Chief of the U.S. Pacific Fleet and Commander of the Naval Forces of the Central Command. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Admiral Fargo serves as Chairman of Huntington Ingalls Industries, America&#146;s largest military shipbuilder, and on the Boards of Directors
for Hawaiian Electric Industries, Matson and United States Automobile Association. Previously he served on the Boards of Northrop Grumman Corporation and Hawaiian Airlines. </P>
