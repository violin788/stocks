</TABLE> <P STYLE="margin-top:24pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman" ALIGN="center"><B>Greenbrier advances railcar leasing strategy with portfolio acquisition </B></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman" ALIGN="center"><I>GBX Leasing exceeds first-year railcar lease fleet targets </I></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>Lake Oswego, Oregon, October</B><B></B><B>&nbsp;13, 2021 &#150;</B>The Greenbrier Companies, Inc. (NYSE: GBX) (&#147;Greenbrier&#148;), a
leading international supplier of equipment and services to global freight transportation markets, today announced the acquisition of more than 3,600 railcars, a portion of which will be held in GBX Leasing (GBXL), a recently formed special purpose
subsidiary which is approximately 95% owned by Greenbrier. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">GBXL was formed to acquire a minimum of $200&nbsp;million of newly built and
leased railcars in its first year of operations. GBXL serves high credit quality railcar lessees which require leases with <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap"><FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">mid-to-longer</FONT></FONT> term maturities. As of
September&nbsp;30, 2021, GBXL&#146;s fleet totals nearly 4,000 units with a fair market value of $350&nbsp;million, exceeding Greenbrier&#146;s initial investment target for the venture by 75%. The fleet was assembled through a combination of lease
originations on Greenbrier built railcars and the portfolio acquisition announced today. This <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">two-pronged</FONT> acquisition strategy allows GBXL to rapidly scale a diversified portfolio of leased railcars. Railcars
in the GBXL fleet transport a wide variety of commodities and serve a broad range of industries, including agriculture, energy, petrochemical and consumer goods. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Greenbrier CEO and Chairman William A. Furman stated, &#147;By providing <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">tax-advantaged</FONT> cash flows,
lease fleet investments reduce Greenbrier&#146;s exposure to the inherent cyclicality of freight transportation equipment manufacturing. The recent railcar acquisition advances Greenbrier&#146;s strategy to increase the scale of our lease fleet
assets. The mix of railcar types included in the acquisition is an excellent fit for our growing GBXL portfolio. The fleet allows us to better serve our existing customers and engage market participants previously unaddressed by Greenbrier.
Additionally, the transaction will generate attractive returns, delivering further value to our shareholders.&#148; </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:18pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>About Greenbrier </B></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Greenbrier, headquartered in Lake Oswego, Oregon, is a leading international supplier of equipment and services to global freight
transportation markets. Through its wholly owned subsidiaries and joint ventures, Greenbrier designs, builds and markets freight railcars and marine barges in North America, Europe and Brazil. We are a leading provider of freight railcar wheel
services, parts, repair, refurbishment and retrofitting services in North America through our wheels, repair&nbsp;&amp; parts business unit. Greenbrier manages 445,000 railcars and offers railcar management, regulatory compliance services and
leasing services to railroads and other railcars owners in North America. GBX Leasing (GBXL) is a special purpose subsidiary that owns and manages a portfolio of leased railcars that originate primarily from Greenbrier&#146;s manufacturing
operations. Together, GBXL and Greenbrier own a lease fleet of nearly 12,500 railcars. Learn more about Greenbrier at www.gbrx.com. </P>
