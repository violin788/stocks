</TABLE> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman" ALIGN="center"><B>Greenbrier Adds Patrick Ottensmeyer as Independent Director to Board of Directors </B></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman" ALIGN="center"><B><I>Former KCS CEO Patrick J. Ottensmeyer elected to Board of Directors </I></B></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman" ALIGN="center"><B><I>Greenbrier&#146;s Board of Directors increases to 11 members </I></B></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman" ALIGN="center"><B><I>New director brings substantial rail industry expertise to Greenbrier </I></B></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>Lake Oswego, Oregon, June</B><B></B><B>&nbsp;28, 2023</B> &#150;&nbsp;The Greenbrier Companies, Inc. (NYSE:GBX) announced today the
election of Patrick J. Ottensmeyer to the Company&#146;s Board of Directors. The election of Ottensmeyer increases Greenbrier&#146;s Board from 10 to 11 members, nine of whom are independent directors. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Ottensmeyer served as President and CEO of Kansas City Southern (KCS), a Class&nbsp;I railroad, from 2015 to 2023 until the completion of the
merger creating Canadian Pacific Kansas City (CPKC) in 2023. From 2008 to 2015, he was Executive Vice President of Sales and Marketing at KCS. Earlier, from 2006 to 2008, he was Executive Vice President and Chief Financial Officer at the railroad.
</P> <P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Ottensmeyer is the U.S. Chairman of the U.S. Chamber of Commerce&#146;s U.S.-Mexico Economic Council (USMXECO). In this role, he presides
over the U.S.-Mexico CEO Dialogue, which occurs twice annually. He oversees the Council&#146;s extensive agenda of engagement with public- and private-sector leaders in the U.S. and Mexico to strengthen bilateral commercial ties. As leader of the
U.S.-Mexico CEO Dialogue, Ottensmeyer was instrumental in representing business interests during the formation of the United States-Mexico-Canada Agreement (USMCA) from 2017-2020. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Ottensmeyer is the <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">Co-Chair</FONT> of the Brookings Institute USMCA Initiative, a group focused on building
more integrated and resilient supply chains. He serves as Chair of the Truman Library Institute, the member-supported, nonprofit partner of the President Harry S. Truman Library&nbsp;&amp; Museum. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">In 2023, Ottensmeyer received the NARS Edward R. Hamberger Lifetime Achievement Award and in 2022, he was the recipient of Ingram&#146;s
Executive of the Year Award. Also in 2022, he and Keith Creel won <I>Railway Age&#146;s</I> <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">Co-Railroaders</FONT> of the Year Award, in connection with the CP and KCS merger. He received the <I>Railway Age</I>
Railroader of the Year recognition for the first time in 2020 and in 2019 he was the recipient of <I>Progressive Railroading&#146;s</I> Railroad Innovator Award. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">&#147;Pat&#146;s experience in operating international businesses with strong regulatory oversight will be invaluable to Greenbrier. In
addition to his unique experience leading a company that connects the entire North American economy, he is committed to continuous improvement and collaboration. Pat&#146;s leadership role in the CPKC merger demonstrates his vision for economic
growth and supply chain integration. Through CPKC, Pat helped create the first fully-integrated rail freight route spanning all three nations of North America. The Board is confident he will provide important perspectives as we continue to execute
our strategy, drive profitability and enhance value for all Greenbrier shareholders,&#148; said Thomas Fargo, Greenbrier Board Chair. </P>
 <p STYLE="margin-top:0pt;margin-bottom:0pt ; font-size:8pt">&nbsp;</P> <P STYLE="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman" ALIGN="center"><B><I>-More- </I></B></P>
