<TD WIDTH="4%" VALIGN="top" ALIGN="left"><B>(b)</B></TD>
<TD ALIGN="left" VALIGN="top"><B>Departure of Certain Officers.</B> </TD></TR></TABLE> <P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">On May&nbsp;10, 2016, The Greenbrier Companies, Inc.
(the &#147;Company&#148;) entered into a Separation and Consulting Agreement with Mr.&nbsp;James&nbsp;T. Sharp, the Company&#146;s Executive Vice President and President of Greenbrier Leasing Company LLC. Pursuant to the Separation and Consulting
Agreement, Mr.&nbsp;Sharp will retire as an employee of the Company on December&nbsp;31, 2016 or such earlier date of termination of employment (the &#147;Employment Term&#148;) and following the Employment Term, will transition to a consulting role
until June&nbsp;30, 2018 or 18 months following termination of the Employment Term, whichever is earlier (the &#147;Consulting Term&#148;). </P> <P STYLE="font-size:18pt;margin-top:0pt;margin-bottom:0pt">&nbsp;</P>

<TD WIDTH="4%" VALIGN="top" ALIGN="left"><B>(e)</B></TD>
<TD ALIGN="left" VALIGN="top"><B>Compensatory Arrangements of Certain Officers.</B> </TD></TR></TABLE> <P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">On May&nbsp;9, 2016, the Company&#146;s
Compensation Committee approved the terms of a Separation and Consulting Agreement with Mr.&nbsp;Sharp pursuant to which: (i)&nbsp;Mr. Sharp will continue to be paid his current base salary during the Employment Term; (ii)&nbsp;any performance-based
cash bonuses for the fiscal year ended August&nbsp;31, 2016 will be as earned and paid in accordance with the terms of the bonus plan in effect for fiscal 2016; (iii)&nbsp;time-based vesting RSUs will become fully vested at the end of the Employment
Term; (iv)&nbsp;performance-based RSUs for the performance period ending August&nbsp;31, 2016 shall continue to vest according to their terms during the Employment Term; (v)&nbsp;performance-based RSUs for the performance period ending
August&nbsp;31, 2017 will immediately vest at the target performance level at the end of the Employment Term, pro-rated for the portion of the performance measurement period during which Mr.&nbsp;Sharp was employed at the Company; and
(vi)&nbsp;Mr.&nbsp;Sharp is eligible to receive a one-time contribution of twice the amount the Company otherwise would have contributed to the Company&#146;s Nonqualified Deferred Compensation Plan in respect of Mr.&nbsp;Sharp&#146;s service during
2016. In addition, following the Employment Term, Mr.&nbsp;Sharp will receive a lump sum severance payment equal to the sum of (y)&nbsp;an amount equal to one times Mr.&nbsp;Sharp&#146;s annual base salary, plus (z)&nbsp;an amount equal to one times
the average of the annual cash bonuses received by Mr.&nbsp;Sharp for fiscal 2015 and fiscal 2016. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">In addition, under the terms of the
Separation and Consulting Agreement, following the Employment Term, Mr.&nbsp;Sharp will provide consulting services to the Company during the Consulting Term in order to provide an orderly transition of his duties. Under the arrangement,
Mr.&nbsp;Sharp will be paid nominal monthly compensation during the Consulting Term. During the Consulting Term, Mr.&nbsp;Sharp will continue to receive certain benefits he received as an employee of the Company, including health insurance, an
automobile allowance and life insurance. A non-competition and non-solicitation period will be in effect through the end of the Employment Term and for a period of two years after the end of the Employment Term, inclusive of any Consulting Term.
</P>
