<TD ALIGN="left" VALIGN="top"> <P STYLE=" margin-top:0pt ; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman; " ALIGN="left"><B>Departure of Directors or Certain Officers; Election of Directors; Appointment of Certain Officers;
Compensatory Arrangements of Certain Officers. </B></P></TD></TR></TABLE> <P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>(c) Appointment of Certain Officers </B></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Effective August&nbsp;27, 2018, Lorie L. Tekorius was promoted to Executive Vice President and Chief Operating Officer (&#147;COO&#148;) of The
Greenbrier Companies, Inc. (&#147;Greenbrier&#148; or the &#147;Company&#148;). In her newly-created COO role, Ms.&nbsp;Tekorius steps into leadership for Greenbrier&#146;s wheels, parts and repair operating unit and takes on additional
responsibility for human resources and corporate safety&nbsp;&amp; security. Ms.&nbsp;Tekorius will continue to serve as the Principal Financial Officer of the Company, overseeing Greenbrier&#146;s financial team and investor relations, as well as
managing corporate strategy. Ms.&nbsp;Tekorius will also continue to chair Greenbrier&#146;s executive committee. Adrian J. Downes has been promoted to Acting Chief Financial Officer in addition to his current role as Senior Vice President and Chief
Accounting Officer, and will continue to report to Ms.&nbsp;Tekorius. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Ms.&nbsp;Tekorius, 51, has been with Greenbrier for 23 years in
various financial capacities. She has served as Chief Financial Officer (Principal Financial Officer) since February 2016, and was promoted to Executive Vice President and chair of Greenbrier&#146;s executive committee in April 2017. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:18pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>(e) Compensatory Arrangements of Certain Officers </B></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><U>Executive Officer Compensation Adjustments </U></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">In connection with the promotion described above, effective August&nbsp;27, 2018, the Compensation Committee approved an increase in
Ms.&nbsp;Tekorius&#146; annual base salary to $585,000 and set Ms.&nbsp;Tekorius&#146; target bonus payout under the 2019 Short-term Incentive Cash Bonus Program (described below) at 90% of base salary. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:18pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><U>2019 Short-term Incentive Cash Bonus Program </U></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">On August&nbsp;27, 2018, the Greenbrier&#146;s Compensation Committee adopted a 2019 Short-term Incentive Cash Bonus Program (the &#147;Bonus
Plan&#148;), pursuant to which the Company&#146;s executive officers, including its named executive officers (&#147;NEOs&#148;), may earn bonuses based on Company financial performance and achievement of strategic objectives during fiscal 2019. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">For each of William A. Furman, President and Chief Executive Officer; Martin R. Baker, Senior Vice President, General Counsel and Chief
Compliance Officer; Alejandro Centurion, Executive Vice President and President of Greenbrier Manufacturing Operations; Mark J. Rittenbaum, Executive Vice President of Commercial and Leasing; and Lorie L. Tekorius, Executive Vice President and Chief
Operating Officer, 85% of the bonus opportunity is based on achievement of the Company-level financial performance goal, adjusted EBITDA, and 15% of the bonus opportunity is based on achievement of two strategic objectives: (i)&nbsp;integration of
new businesses and continued integration of Europe, Brazil, and the repair shop network; and (ii)&nbsp;talent development and succession planning across all business units. </P>
 <p STYLE="margin-top:0pt;margin-bottom:0pt ; font-size:8pt">&nbsp;</P> <P STYLE="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman" ALIGN="center">2 </P>

<Center><DIV STYLE="width:8.5in" align="left">
 <P STYLE="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Target bonus payouts are established for each executive officer at a different percentage of
base salary. The target bonus payout percentages for each of the named executive officers other than Ms.&nbsp;Tekorius (whose target bonus payout was set by the Compensation Committee at 90% of base salary) will be determined by the Compensation
Committee at a later date. Of the 85% of the bonus opportunity based on financial performance goals, 100% is based on achievement of the Company&#146;s adjusted EBITDA goal. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Adjusted EBITDA is defined as net earnings before interest and foreign exchange, income tax expense, depreciation and amortization, and before
Short-term Incentive Plan, and excluding significant non-cash charges such as goodwill impairment. Each of these metrics is adjusted for special and non-recurring items listed in the Company&#146;s Umbrella Performance Based Plan for Executive
Officers in the discretion of the Compensation Committee. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Bonus payouts resulting from achievement of financial performance goals will
vary depending upon achievement of threshold, goal or stretch performance levels. At threshold performance, the bonus payout will equal 75% of an individual&#146;s target. At goal performance, the bonus payout will equal 100% of an individual&#146;s
target. At stretch performance, the bonus payout will equal 200% of an individual&#146;s target. Bonus amounts will be interpolated between the threshold, goal and stretch levels. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">With respect to the goals based on achievement of strategic objectives, the Compensation Committee will determine the level of achievement of
such goals by all executive officers as a group, on a scale of 0 &#150; 150%. The percentage of an individual&#146;s bonus payout will match the percentage of achievement. For example, if the Compensation Committee determines that the executive
officers achieved 80% of their strategic goals, the payout percentage for each executive officer would be 80% of the individual&#146;s target. </P> <P STYLE="font-size:18pt;margin-top:0pt;margin-bottom:0pt">&nbsp;</P>

</TABLE> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman" ALIGN="center"><B>Greenbrier Promotes Lorie Tekorius to Chief Operating Officer </B></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman" ALIGN="center"><I>~ Recognizes contributions to Greenbrier and advances succession planning ~ </I></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman" ALIGN="center"><I>~ Greenbrier&#146;s strategic goal is to increase scale, growth and talent pipeline ~ </I></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>Lake Oswego, Oregon, August</B><B></B><B>&nbsp;28, 2018</B>&#151;The Greenbrier Companies, Inc. (NYSE: GBX) today announced that its
Executive Vice President (EVP) and Chief Financial Officer (CFO), Lorie Tekorius, has been appointed EVP and Chief Operating Officer (COO) by its Board of Directors, with expanded responsibilities, continuing to report to William A. Furman,
Chairman, President and Chief Executive Officer. In her newly-created COO role, Tekorius steps into leadership for Greenbrier&#146;s wheels, parts and repair operating unit and takes on additional responsibility for human resources and corporate
safety&nbsp;&amp; security. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Rick Turner, Senior Vice President for Wheels, Parts&nbsp;&amp; Repair, will report to Tekorius. Turner will
manage 24 wheels, parts and repair shops, double the number of locations in the Greenbrier shop network, after the dissolution of the GBW Railcar Services joint venture and the return of Greenbrier&#146;s shops announced August 20. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Adrian Downes has been promoted to Acting CFO and remains reporting to Tekorius. Downes joined Greenbrier in 2013 as Chief Accounting Officer
and has over 30 years of accounting and finance experience in a variety of industries, both with publicly held and privately held companies. He currently directs all Greenbrier&#146;s global corporate and operational accounting activities and
oversees Greenbrier&#146;s information technology and tax planning functions. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Tekorius continues her management of corporate strategy and
oversight of Greenbrier&#146;s financial team as well as investor relations and planning led by Justin Roberts, Vice President, Corporate Finance and Treasurer. Additionally, she chairs Greenbrier&#146;s executive committee comprised of senior
management. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Tekorius has been with Greenbrier for 23 years in various financial capacities. Tekorius was appointed CFO in February 2016
and Executive Vice President and chair of the executive committee in April 2017. In May 2018, she was recognized by the Portland Business Journal for her corporate and community work as Oregon&#146;s &#147;CFO of the Year&#151;Public Company
Category&#148;. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Furman stated, &#147;Lorie is a highly skilled executive with more than two decades of service to Greenbrier. Her
promotion and enhanced responsibilities reflect the reality of the increased duties she has accepted through her successive advancements in management at Greenbrier. Lorie has demonstrated a strong sense of our values, including integrity, respect
for people and customers, along with an ability to collaborate across the organization to achieve shared success. Lorie&#146;s promotion to COO today demonstrates Greenbrier&#146;s emphasis on growing from within as we continue to plan our future.
In her enhanced role, Lorie will help Greenbrier focus our energies on management&#146;s strategic plan to emphasize growth and scale while building our bench of talent and sharpening our succession plans throughout the company.&#148; </P>
