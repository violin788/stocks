<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>Lake Oswego, Oregon, July</B><B></B><B>&nbsp;30, 2024</B> &#150; The&nbsp;Greenbrier Companies, Inc. (NYSE:GBX) today remembers Board
member Patrick J. Ottensmeyer, who passed away this past weekend at age 67. The Company extends its deepest condolences to his family and friends. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Ottensmeyer had served on Greenbrier&#146;s Board since 2023, bringing decades of industry and international relations expertise. Ottensmeyer
served as President and CEO of Kansas City Southern (KCS), a Class&nbsp;I railroad, from 2015 to 2023, until the completion of the merger creating Canadian Pacific Kansas City (CPKC) in 2023. Before becoming CEO, Ottensmeyer served in various roles
at KCS since 2006. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Ottensmeyer had deep expertise working across the U.S.-Mexico border and was instrumental in representing business
interests during the creation of the United States-Mexico-Canada Agreement (USMCA). He served as the U.S. Chairman of the U.S. Chamber of Commerce&#146;s U.S.-Mexico Economic Council (USMXECO) during this pivotal time. He was <FONT
STYLE="white-space:nowrap">Co-Chair</FONT> of the Brookings Institute USMCA Initiative, a group focused on building more integrated and resilient supply chains. He served as Chair of the Truman Library Institute, the member-supported, nonprofit
partner of the President Harry S. Truman Library&nbsp;&amp; Museum. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">In 2023, Ottensmeyer received the NARS Edward R. Hamberger Lifetime
Achievement Award; in 2022, he received Ingram&#146;s Executive of the Year Award. Also in 2022, was Railway Age&#146;s <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">Co-Railroader</FONT> of the Year Award, in connection with the CP and KCS merger. He received
the Railway Age Railroader of the Year recognition for the first time in 2020, and in 2019, he received Progressive Railroading&#146;s Railroad Innovator Award. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">&#147;Pat demonstrated a remarkable passion for this industry. From day one, he became an invaluable member of our Board of Directors. We are
forever grateful for his contributions to Greenbrier, its shareholders and the broader rail industry. He will be greatly missed, as he was a wonderful friend to his Board colleagues and many at the company,&#148; said Board Chair Admiral Thomas B.
Fargo (USN Retired). </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">&#147;Working alongside Pat has been a privilege for the other board members and me. He was a visionary leader who
left a lasting legacy for the North American rail industry and the broader trade policy landscape. The Greenbrier family offers our deepest condolences to his friends and family. Pat will be deeply missed,&#148; said Lorie Tekorius, CEO and
President. </P>
