<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 12pt"><U><B>Second Quarter Results</B></U>
</DIV>


<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Revenues for the second quarter of 2010 were $200.0&nbsp;million, down from $287.1&nbsp;million in the
prior year&#146;s second quarter. Gross margin for the quarter was 12.1% of revenues compared to 5.6% of
revenues in the prior comparable period. EBITDA was $15.9&nbsp;million, or 8.0% of revenues for the
quarter, compared to $9.4&nbsp;million, or 3.3% of revenues in the prior year&#146;s second quarter.
</DIV>


<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Company&#146;s net loss was $4.8&nbsp;million, or $0.28 per diluted share, for the quarter, compared
to a net loss of $7.5&nbsp;million, or $0.45 per diluted share for the same period in 2009. Net loss for
the quarter includes noncash charges of $1.3&nbsp;million, net of tax, or $0.08 per diluted share for
warrant amortization expense and amortization of convertible debt discount. Net loss for the prior
year&#146;s second quarter included a noncash charge of $0.6&nbsp;million, net of tax, or $0.03 per diluted
share, for amortization of convertible debt discount. In addition, the prior period was negatively
impacted by a $9.9&nbsp;million obligation under a new railcar contract, wherein the Company guaranteed
the purchaser minimum earnings.
</DIV>


<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 12pt"><U><B>Discussion of Quarterly Results and Outlook</B></U>
</DIV>


<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;William A. Furman, president and chief executive officer, said, &#147;As anticipated, the second
quarter was weaker than our first quarter. Similar to prior years, we expect that the second half
of the year will be significantly stronger than the first half.&#148;
</DIV>


<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Furman added, &#147;We are currently experiencing early signs of increased activity and demand in
all of our business segments. While overall visibility remains limited, we are seeing improvements
in railcar loadings, declining railroad velocity, and declines in the quantity of railcars in
storage. Assuming these trends continue, we anticipate a positive impact on each of our business
segments in the second half of 2010.&#148;
</DIV>


<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Furman concluded, &#147;We are cautiously optimistic that the macro economy has begun a modest
recovery. Regardless of the path this recovery takes, we remain focused on several key objectives
in 2010. These include improving the operational efficiency of our facilities while maintaining
the flexibility to respond to market demand. Further, we will continue to manage for cash flow and
liquidity, and will work to fully leverage our integrated business model. With these objectives in
mind, we believe we are well positioned to capture new opportunities as economic conditions
improve.&#148;
</DIV>


<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 12pt"><U><B>Segment Details</B></U>
</DIV>


<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Refurbishment &#038; Parts segment, consisting of a network of 37 locations, repairs and
refurbishes railcars, and provides wheel services and railcar parts across North America. Revenue
for this segment in the current quarter was $94.3&nbsp;million, compared to $121.7&nbsp;million in the second
quarter of 2009. The revenue decline was due to lower sales volumes across all product and service
types, as a result of the current economic environment. Gross margin for the Refurbishment &#038; Parts
segment was 11.6% of revenues, compared to 11.7% of revenues in the prior comparable period.
</DIV>

<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 12pt"><U><B>Business Outlook</B></U>
</DIV>


<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Based on current industry trends, Greenbrier expects business visibility in fiscal 2010 to
remain limited. Management continues to anticipate that revenues will be lower in 2010 compared to
2009. EBITDA excluding special charges, however, is expected to be modestly higher in 2010 compared
to 2009, due in part to higher expected gross margins in Greenbrier&#146;s Manufacturing segment.
Similar to previous years, financial results for the second half of the year are anticipated to be
significantly stronger than the first half. While the outlook remains cautious in the near term,
the Company continues to be optimistic about the long-term fundamentals that support rail and
marine transportation.
</DIV>


<DIV align="left" style="font-size: 10pt; margin-top: 6pt">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Company believes it has adequate liquidity to weather the economic downturn, with
favorable debt covenants, no significant term debt maturing until 2012, and much of its term debt
maturing in 2015. The Company plans to manage for cash flow and liquidity in both the short- and
long-term to ensure a strong capital position prior to key debt maturity dates.
