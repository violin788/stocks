</TABLE> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman" ALIGN="center"><B>Greenbrier announces new railcar orders and preliminary results for fiscal second quarter </B></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman" ALIGN="center"><B><I>~ Orders for 3,800 units valued at nearly $450&nbsp;million during fiscal Q2 ~ </I></B></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman" ALIGN="center"><B><I>~ New railcar backlog of 26,000 units valued at $2.7&nbsp;billion ~ </I></B></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman" ALIGN="center"><B><I>~ Unadjusted EPS range of $0.07 to $0.09 includes <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">one-time</FONT> charges of $0.14 ~ </I></B></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>Lake Oswego, Oregon, March</B><B></B><B>&nbsp;22, 2019 &#150; </B>The Greenbrier Companies, Inc. (NYSE: GBX) today announced that it had
received orders for 3,800 new railcars with an aggregate value of nearly $450&nbsp;million during its fiscal second quarter ended on February&nbsp;28, 2019. Orders for the quarter include tank cars, automobile-carrying railcars and covered hopper
cars, and were consistent with Greenbrier&#146;s expectations for the period. New railcar backlog of 26,000 units valued at $2.7&nbsp;billion continues to be diversified across railcar types. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Separately, Greenbrier today announced preliminary financial results for its fiscal second quarter. Greenbrier expects revenue of
approximately $650&nbsp;million and unadjusted EPS of $0.07 to $0.09 per share. Challenges in Romania, Gunderson and railcar repair operations impacted results by $0.29, including <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">one-time</FONT> charges of $0.14
related to loss contingencies on certain railcar contracts, primarily in Romania, along with facility closure costs in the railcar repair operations. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">William A. Furman, Chairman and CEO, said, &#147;In the quarter, railcar order demand was solid overall. However, we are disappointed with our
earnings. This was largely driven by three factors. First, poor manufacturing execution in Romania compounded by supplier delivery failures and railcar contract loss contingencies created losses in the quarter at our European railcar manufacturing
operations. Next, lower overhead absorption due to increased labor costs and lower railcar build activity at our Gunderson manufacturing facility weakened results. Finally, continued challenges in railcar repair operations, including extreme winter
weather and closure costs, impacted earnings. Collectively, these items negatively impacted results by $0.29 per share. Greenbrier is actively addressing these performance issues and will resolve them.&#148; </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Furman continued, &#147;In Europe, effective February&nbsp;1, 2019, William Glenn <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">re-joined</FONT> Greenbrier
as Chair of the Management Board and CEO of Greenbrier-Astra Rail with a mandate to improve operating profitability. A railcar production line <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">re-opens</FONT> at Gunderson in the fiscal third quarter, improving the
overhead absorption and production volumes.&#148; </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Furman concluded, &#147;Since regaining direct control of our North American railcar
repair shops in September following the dissolution of GBW Railcar Services, the Greenbrier Rail Services (GRS) management team has been strategically reviewing the network. After first ensuring fair treatment was given to our employees, in the
second quarter GRS announced plans to conclude operations at two railcar repair locations. Although the site evaluation by GRS leadership continues, we are optimistic about the progress we have made with our railcar repair shop network. We will have
the network solidified by the end of the fourth quarter resulting in improving operating performance during the balance of this fiscal year.&#148; </P>
