</TABLE> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:14pt; font-family:Times New Roman" ALIGN="center"><B>Greenbrier announces orders for 15,300 new railcars valued at $1.9 billion </B></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:12pt; font-family:Times New Roman" ALIGN="center"><I>Broad demand across all railcar types leads to highest quarterly orders in nearly a decade </I></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>Lake Oswego, Oregon, September 21, 2023 &#150; </B>The Greenbrier Companies, Inc. (NYSE: GBX) today announced that it had received orders
for 15,300 new railcars with an aggregate value of $1.9 billion during its fiscal fourth quarter ended on August&nbsp;31, 2023. Orders for the quarter demonstrate Greenbrier&#146;s lease origination capabilities balanced with its direct sale
expertise. The 15,300 units were ordered by customers operating in various commercial sectors and spanned a range of railcar types, yielding Greenbrier&#146;s highest quarterly order count in nearly a decade. Railcar orders announced today do not
include any multi-year order activity. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Lorie L. Tekorius, CEO and President, said, &#147;Greenbrier continued its solid operational
execution, and our Commercial team performed exceptionally in our fiscal fourth quarter. Our backlog and this order activity provide clear visibility through fiscal 2024, increasing our confidence in our operating expectations. Further, our leasing
and aftermarket services businesses will provide additional stability and balance through economic cycles.&#148; </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">&#147;The diverse range
of orders across railcar types exceeded Greenbrier&#146;s expectations for the quarter. It also indicates Greenbrier&#146;s strength in our North American and European markets. Order levels are distinct from the high demand seen during the <FONT
STYLE="white-space:nowrap"><FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">crude-by-rail</FONT></FONT> era of the 2010s. Today, one or two railcar types are not powering the market. Although railcar loadings are not fully back at
<FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">pre-pandemic</FONT> levels, we believe that growth in key commodity markets will keep railcar demand steady and above replacement levels in future periods,&#148; said Brian J. Comstock, EVP Chief Commercial and
Leasing Officer. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Greenbrier expects to announce fiscal fourth-quarter and fiscal year 2023 earnings on Wednesday, October 25. Certain
orders referenced in this release are subject to customary documentation and completion of terms. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:18pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>About Greenbrier </B></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Greenbrier, headquartered in Lake Oswego, Oregon, is a leading international supplier of equipment and services to global freight
transportation markets. Through its wholly-owned subsidiaries and joint ventures, Greenbrier designs, builds and markets freight railcars in North America, Europe and Brazil. We are a leading provider of freight railcar wheel services, parts,
maintenance and retrofitting services in North America through our maintenance services business unit. Greenbrier owns a lease fleet of approximately 12,500 railcars that originate primarily from Greenbrier&#146;s manufacturing operations.
Greenbrier offers railcar management, regulatory compliance services and leasing services to railroads and other railcar owners in North America. Learn more about Greenbrier at <U>www.gbrx.com</U>. </P>
