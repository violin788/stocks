</TABLE> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman" ALIGN="center"><B>Greenbrier Elects New Board Chair </B></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman" ALIGN="center"><I>Admiral Thomas B. Fargo, USN (Ret.) will succeed William A. Furman as Chair </I></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>Lake Oswego, Oregon, August</B><B></B><B>&nbsp;30, 2022</B> &#150; The Greenbrier Companies, Inc. (NYSE:GBX) today announced the election
of retired four-star Admiral Thomas B. Fargo as Independent Chair of its Board of Directors, effective at Greenbrier&#146;s close of business on August 31. A board member since 2015, Fargo serves as Chair of Greenbrier&#146;s Compensation Committee
and a member of the Nominating and Corporate Governance Committee. Fargo has served as the Company&#146;s Lead Director since January 2021, a position Greenbrier&#146;s Board will eliminate with the election of an independent Board Chair. Effective
with the appointment of Fargo, Greenbrier&#146;s Board policy is that members identified as Chair must meet the standards of the SEC and NYSE for an independent director. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Fargo will succeed William A. Furman, Greenbrier Executive Chair since March 2022. Previously, Furman was Chairman and CEO since 2014. Furman
served Greenbrier and its predecessors as a <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">co-founder,</FONT> CEO, President and director beginning in 1974. Furman will continue to serve as a board member until January 2024, completing 50 years with Greenbrier
and its affiliated businesses. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Lorie Tekorius, President&nbsp;&amp; CEO said, &#147;On behalf of Greenbrier&#146;s Board of Directors, I
am pleased to announce Admiral Fargo&#146;s election to Independent Chair. Greenbrier, our management team and the Board are committed to leading in corporate governance and enhancing shareholder value. Having an independent board chair reflects
best practices in corporate governance. We carefully considered the qualifications of all directors before electing Admiral Fargo as Chair. His broad experience in the transportation industry and service on public company boards is substantial and
he will lead Greenbrier boldly into the future.&#148; </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Fargo joined Greenbrier&#146;s Board of Directors as a former military commander
with subsequent private sector experience in defense, maritime and other transportation industries. As Commander of the U.S. Pacific Command (2002-2005), he led the world&#146;s largest unified command while directing the joint operations of the
Army, Navy, Marine Corps and Air Force in the Asia-Pacific Theater. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Fargo also serves as Board Chair at Hawaiian Electric Industries and
on the board of directors at shipping and logistics provider Matson, Inc. He was previously Board Chair at USAA, a $37 billion-dollar financial services company supporting the U.S. military and their families and Huntington Ingalls Industries, the
nation&#146;s largest shipbuilder. He has also served on the boards of Northrop Grumman Corporation, Alexander&nbsp;&amp; Baldwin, Inc. and Hawaiian Airlines. </P>
