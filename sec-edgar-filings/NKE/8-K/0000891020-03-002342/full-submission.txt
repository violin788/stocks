<P align="left"><FONT size="2"><B>Beaverton, OR (September&nbsp;18, 2003) </B>&#151; NIKE, Inc. (NYSE:NKE) today reported
revenues and earnings for the Company&#146;s first quarter ended August&nbsp;31, 2003.
First quarter revenues increased eight percent to $3.0&nbsp;billion, versus $2.8
billion for the same period last year. First quarter net income totaled $261.2
million, or $0.98 per diluted share, compared to a loss of $48.9&nbsp;million, or
$0.18 loss per diluted share, and $217.2&nbsp;million, or $0.81 per diluted share
before accounting change in the prior year.
</FONT>

<P align="left"><FONT size="2">Philip H. Knight, Chairman and CEO, said, &#147;Nike&#146;s first quarter results reflect
another great quarter in which global brand strength and superior product,
combined with a favorable currency exchange environment, drove worldwide
business results to record levels. This quarter, Nike&#146;s sales topped $3
billion, the highest single quarter revenue in our history. Equally important,
we again demonstrated our commitment to managing our business for long-term
profitability, achieving the highest gross margin percentage in our recent
history and delivering 21&nbsp;percent growth in earnings per share before last
year&#146;s accounting change. As Nike&#146;s global portfolio of businesses continues
to expand, we will remain focused on managing both our brand and our business
for long-term, profitable growth.&#148;*
</FONT>


<P align="left"><FONT size="2"><B>Futures Orders</B>
</FONT>

<P align="left"><FONT size="2">The Company reported worldwide futures orders for athletic footwear and
apparel, scheduled for delivery from September 2003 through January 2004,
totaling $3.7&nbsp;billion, 10.5&nbsp;percent higher than such orders reported for the same
period last year. Approximately three points of this growth were due to
changes in currency exchange rates. In addition, approximately four
to five points of
this growth can be attributed to an earlier start of the spring season for
footwear sales in Europe.*
</FONT>

<P align="left"><FONT size="2">By region, futures orders for the USA
were down three percent; Europe increased
28&nbsp;percent; Asia Pacific grew 19&nbsp;percent; and the Americas increased nine
percent. In Europe, seven points of the
</FONT>

<P align="left"><FONT size="2">increase were due to currency exchange rates. Currency exchange contributed to
Asia Pacific and the America&#146;s growth by two points and three points,
respectively.*
</FONT>

<P align="left"><FONT size="2">&#147;Once again, momentum in Nike&#146;s international markets continued to fuel the
business as international futures increased 23&nbsp;percent,&#148; Knight added. &#147;While
Nike&#146;s USA futures declined three percent, we are confident that our largest
business, USA footwear, is on the right track based on positive footwear
futures growth. In addition, our profitability has grown and consumers are
responding to our brand and the full range of Nike&#146;s performance and active
life product offerings.&#148;*
</FONT>


<P align="left"><FONT size="2"><B>Regional Highlights</B>
</FONT>


<P align="left"><FONT size="2">USA
</FONT>

<P align="left"><FONT size="2">During the first quarter, U.S. revenues declined two percent to $1.25&nbsp;billion
versus $1.28&nbsp;billion for the first quarter of 2003. U.S. athletic footwear
revenues declined five percent to $822.4&nbsp;million. Apparel revenues increased
five percent to $346.5&nbsp;million. Equipment revenues fell four percent to $85.0
million.
</FONT>


<P align="left"><FONT size="2">Europe
</FONT>

<P align="left"><FONT size="2">Revenues for the European region (which includes the Middle East and Africa)
grew 17&nbsp;percent to $1.01&nbsp;billion, up from $869.3&nbsp;million for the same period
last year. Nineteen points of this growth were the result of changes in
currency exchange rates. Footwear revenues increased 20&nbsp;percent to $590.0
million, apparel revenues increased 10&nbsp;percent to $341.9&nbsp;million and equipment
revenues increased 24&nbsp;percent to $81.6&nbsp;million.
</FONT>


<P align="left"><FONT size="2">Asia Pacific
</FONT>

<P align="left"><FONT size="2">Revenues in the Asia Pacific region grew 13&nbsp;percent to $348.3&nbsp;million compared
to $307.8&nbsp;million a year ago. Four points of this growth were the result of
changes in currency exchange rates. Footwear revenues were up 12&nbsp;percent to
$202.8&nbsp;million; apparel revenues increased 15&nbsp;percent to $113.3&nbsp;million and
equipment grew 13&nbsp;percent to $32.2&nbsp;million.
</FONT>


<P align="left"><FONT size="2">Americas
</FONT>

<P align="left"><FONT size="2">Revenues in the Americas region increased eight percent to $153.7&nbsp;million, an
improvement from $142.3&nbsp;million in the first quarter of 2003. This growth rate
reflected a two percent increase due to changes in currency exchange rates.
Footwear revenues were up 11&nbsp;percent to $103.1&nbsp;million, apparel revenues
increased two percent to $39.4&nbsp;million and equipment improved five percent to
$11.2&nbsp;million.
</FONT>


<P align="left"><FONT size="2">Other Revenues
</FONT>

<P align="left"><FONT size="2">Other revenues, which include Nike Golf, Bauer Nike Hockey Inc., Cole Haan&#174;,
and Hurley International LLC, grew 32&nbsp;percent to $255.5&nbsp;million from $194.2
million last year.
</FONT>


<P align="left"><FONT size="2"><B>Income Statement Review</B>
</FONT>

<P align="left"><FONT size="2">Gross margins were 43.0&nbsp;percent compared to 41.4&nbsp;percent last year. Selling and
administrative expenses were 28.7&nbsp;percent of first quarter revenues, compared
to 28.8&nbsp;percent last year. The effective tax rate for the first quarter was
34.8&nbsp;percent.
</FONT>


<P align="left"><FONT size="2"><B>Balance Sheet Review</B>
</FONT>

<P align="left"><FONT size="2">At quarter end, global inventories stood at $1.5&nbsp;billion, an increase of four
percent from August&nbsp;31, 2002. Cash and short-term investments were $997.8
million at the end of the quarter, compared to
</FONT>

</FONT>


<P align="left"><FONT size="2"><B>Share Repurchase</B>
</FONT>

<P align="left"><FONT size="2">During the quarter, the Company purchased a total of 1,706,500 shares for
approximately $89.5&nbsp;million in conjunction with the Company&#146;s second four-year,
$1&nbsp;billion share repurchase program that was approved by the Board of Directors
in June 2000.
</FONT>

<P align="left"><FONT size="2">NIKE, Inc. based in Beaverton, Oregon is the world&#146;s leading designer, marketer
and distributor of authentic athletic footwear, apparel, equipment and
accessories for a wide variety of sports and fitness activities. Wholly owned
Nike subsidiaries include Converse Inc. (as of September&nbsp;4, 2003), which
designs, markets and distributes athletic footwear, apparel and accessories;
Bauer NIKE Hockey Inc., a leading designer and distributor of hockey equipment;
Cole Haan&#174;, which designs, markets, and distributes fine dress and casual
shoes, accessories, and outerwear; and Hurley International LLC, which designs,
markets and distributes action sports and youth lifestyle footwear, apparel and
accessories.
</FONT>
