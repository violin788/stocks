<div style="font-family:Times New Roman;font-size:10pt;font-variant:normal;margin:0pt;">Zion Oil and Gas, Inc., a Delaware corporation, which has an active petroleum exploration operation onshore Israel on the Megiddo-Jezreel area,&#160;announces the resumption of the recompletion of the Megiddo-Jezreel 1 (MJ-01) well in Israel. Zion&#8217;s rig crew arrived in Israel on February 15, 2025, and has commenced critical maintenance and preparatory work. The rig, which was safely &#8220;warm stacked&#8221; in September, is undergoing necessary checks for maintenance, including fluid changes, lubrication and greasing, and mechanical, electrical, and safety audits. Following maintenance, the team will begin drilling out the temporary plug at approximately 1,100 meters. This phase is expected to take 2-3 weeks, paving the way for the subsequent well completion and testing operations. Once the plug is removed, Zion will proceed with setting a permanent plug at the deeper part of the well, allowing for isolating targeted zones of interest for testing.</div>

<div style="font-family:'Times New Roman';font-size:10pt;font-variant:normal;margin:0pt;">&#160;</div>

<div style="font-family:'Times New Roman';font-size:10pt;font-variant:normal;margin:0pt;">&#160;</div>

<div style="font-family:Times New Roman;font-size:10pt;font-variant:normal;margin:0pt;">Zion Oil &amp; Gas has successfully navigated complex logistical challenges to ensure the timely delivery of essential equipment. Resources are currently enroute from across the globe, including India, Romania, Germany, the Netherlands, the UAE, the United States, and Tanzania. This unprecedented international cooperation underscores the dedication and perseverance of Zion&#8217;s team and partners.</div>

<div style="font-family:'Times New Roman';font-size:10pt;font-variant:normal;margin:0pt;">&#160;</div>

<div style="font-family:Times New Roman;font-size:10pt;font-variant:normal;margin:0pt;">Zion has maintained continuous security at the MJ-01 site, ensuring a stable and secure operational environment. Additionally, commercial air travel into Israel has steadily resumed, further supporting logistical operations. With all necessary equipment expected to be on-site by March, Zion anticipates progressing through the well completion and testing operations in Q2 2025.</div>

<p style="font-family:Times New Roman;font-size:18pt;font-variant:normal;margin:0pt;">Zion Oil &amp; Gas Announces Renewed Operations Underway for MJ-01 Well Testing &amp; Recompletion Project</p>

<p style="font-family:'Times New Roman';font-size:10pt;font-variant:normal;margin:0pt;">&nbsp;</p>

<p style="font-family:Times New Roman;font-size:12pt;font-variant:normal;margin:0pt;">Current Unit Program Ends on February 28</p>

<p style="font-family:'Times New Roman';font-size:10pt;font-variant:normal;margin:0pt;">&nbsp;</p>

<p style="font-family:Times New Roman;font-size:10pt;font-variant:normal;margin:0pt;">DALLAS, Texas, and CAESAREA, Israel, February 18, 2025 &#8211; Zion Oil &amp; Gas, Inc. (OTC: ZNOG) is pleased to announce a significant operational milestone as work resumes on the recompletion of the Megiddo-Jezreel 1 (MJ-01) well in Israel. This marks a crucial phase in Zion&#8217;s ongoing efforts to unlock Israel&#8217;s onshore energy potential.</p>

<p style="font-family:'Times New Roman';font-size:10pt;font-variant:normal;margin:0pt;">&nbsp;</p>

<p style="font-family:Times New Roman;font-size:10pt;font-variant:normal;margin:0pt;"><b>Operations Update: Crews on the Ground and Progress Underway</b></p>

<p style="font-family:Times New Roman;font-size:10pt;font-variant:normal;margin:0pt;">Zion&#8217;s rig crew arrived in Israel on February 15, 2025, and has commenced critical maintenance and preparatory work. The rig, which was safely &#8220;warm stacked&#8221; in September, is undergoing necessary maintenance procedures, including fluid changes, lubrication and greasing, and mechanical, electrical, and safety audits to ensure peak functionality.</p>

<p style="font-family:'Times New Roman';font-size:10pt;font-variant:normal;margin:0pt;">&nbsp;</p>

<p style="font-family:Times New Roman;font-size:10pt;font-variant:normal;margin:0pt;">Following maintenance, the team will begin drilling out the temporary plug at approximately 1,100 meters. This phase is expected to take 2-3 weeks, paving the way for the subsequent well recompletion and testing operations. Once the plug is removed, Zion will proceed with setting a permanent plug at the deeper part of the well to isolate the targeted zones of interest for testing.</p>

<p style="font-family:'Times New Roman';font-size:10pt;font-variant:normal;margin:0pt;">&nbsp;</p>

<p style="font-family:Times New Roman;font-size:10pt;font-variant:normal;margin:0pt;"><b>Global Coordination &amp; Logistics</b></p>

<p style="font-family:Times New Roman;font-size:10pt;font-variant:normal;margin:0pt;">Zion Oil &amp; Gas has successfully navigated complex logistical challenges to ensure the timely delivery of essential equipment. Resources are currently enroute from across the globe, including India, Romania, Germany, the Netherlands, the UAE, the United States, and Tanzania. This unprecedented international cooperation underscores the dedication and perseverance of Zion&#8217;s team and partners.</p>

<p style="font-family:'Times New Roman';font-size:10pt;font-variant:normal;margin:0pt;">&nbsp;</p>

<p style="font-family:Times New Roman;font-size:10pt;font-variant:normal;margin:0pt;">&#8220;As we move forward with this phase of operations, we are witnessing God&#8217;s provision in extraordinary ways,&#8221; said Monty Kness, VP of Operations. &#8220;The ability to coordinate a project of this scale, with equipment and expertise sourced from multiple continents, is a testament to both our team&#8217;s determination and the global support behind our mission.&#8221;</p>

<p style="font-family:'Times New Roman';font-size:10pt;font-variant:normal;margin:0pt;">&nbsp;</p>
