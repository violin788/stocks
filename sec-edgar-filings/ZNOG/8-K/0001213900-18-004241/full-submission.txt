<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0; text-align: justify; text-indent: 0.5in">On April 9, 2018, memorial
services in Jerusalem, Israel were held for Dr. Yehezkel &ldquo;Charlie&rdquo; Druckman, age 79, a beloved member of the Board
of Directors of Zion Oil and Gas, Inc. (the &ldquo;Company&rdquo;) since November 2005. Dr. Druckman&rsquo;s service on the Zion
Board of Directors included serving as Zion&rsquo;s Liaison to the Israeli Energy Ministry and advising the Company regarding his
extensive Israeli geologic expertise. Charlie loved traveling and exploring the outdoors from mountain climbing in Africa to scuba
diving in the Red Sea.</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0; text-align: justify; text-indent: 0.5in">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0; text-align: justify; text-indent: 0.5in">Previously, Dr. Druckman
was the Petroleum Commissioner for the State of Israel from 1995 until his retirement in 2004, where he supervised the licensing
of petroleum rights both onshore and offshore Israel. These efforts led to the discovery of 1.5 trillion cubic feet of gas in the
Israeli offshore Mari B and other smaller fields during 1999-2000. Since 1965, he was a member of the professional staff of the
Geological Survey of Israel, where he headed the Mapping, Stratigraphy and Oil Division during 1982-1985 and 1991-1994. He was
also affiliated with Louisiana State University at Baton Rouge as Research Associate in Geology during 1978-1980 and 1989-1990.
He was awarded in 1974 the Israel Geological Society&rsquo;s Perez Grader award. He was an active member of the American Association
of Petroleum Geologists and the Geological Society of Israel (where he served as president in 1982 and for a number of years on
the Society's editorial board). He also served as member of the Israeli National Petroleum Commission and Board of Directors of
Oil Exploration (Investments) Ltd., an Israeli government company. Dr. Druckman graduated from the Hebrew University in Jerusalem,
where he was awarded BSc, MSc and PhD degrees in geology.</P>
