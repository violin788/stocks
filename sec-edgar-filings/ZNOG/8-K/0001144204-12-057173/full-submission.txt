<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0; text-align: justify"><B>ITEM 1.01. Entry into a Material Definitive
Agreement</B></P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0; text-align: justify">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0; text-align: justify; text-indent: 0.5in">On October 22, 2012,
Zion Oil &amp; Gas, Inc. (the &ldquo;Company&rdquo;) implemented a reduction in the exercise price of all of its publicly traded
warrants trading on the NASDAQ Global Market under the symbols <B>ZNWAW </B>and<B> ZNWAZ. </B>Under the exercise price reduction
program, these warrants can be exercised, through their scheduled expiration date of December 31, 2012, at the reduced warrant
exercise price of $1.75. Each warrant so exercised produces one share of the Company&rsquo;s common stock, trading on the NASDAQ Global
Market under the symbol <B>ZN.</B></P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0; text-align: justify; text-indent: 0.5in">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0; text-align: justify; text-indent: 0.5in">Except for the temporary
reduction in the warrant exercise prices as herein provided, all of the terms and conditions contained in the applicable Warrant
instruments will continue in full force and effect.</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0; text-align: justify; text-indent: 0.5in">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0; text-align: justify; text-indent: 0.5in">A copy of the press

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0; text-align: center"><B>Zion Oil Reduces the Exercise Price of
Outstanding Warrants </B></P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0; text-align: center">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0; text-align: justify"><B>Dallas, Texas and Caesarea, Israel -
October 22, 2012:</B> Zion Oil &amp; Gas, Inc. (NASDAQ GM: <B>ZN, ZNWAW, ZNWAZ</B>) today announced that it has reduced the exercise
price to $1.75 of both series of its outstanding publicly traded warrants quoted under the symbols &ldquo;<B>ZNWAW</B>&rdquo; and
&ldquo;<B>ZNWAZ</B>&rdquo; until 5:00 p.m. eastern time on December 31, 2012, their scheduled expiration date (the &ldquo;Expiration
Time&rdquo;). Any and all warrants properly exercised in accordance with their respective terms prior to the Expiration Time will
be accepted by Zion at the reduced exercise price and one share of registered common stock per warrant will be issued to the exercising
warrant holder. The original exercise price for the <B>ZNWAW</B> Warrants was $7.00 and for the <B>ZNWAZ</B> Warrants was $4.00.</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0; text-align: justify">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0; text-align: justify">After the Expiration Time, warrants under
the symbols <B>ZNWAW</B> and <B>ZNWAZ</B> will expire with no further value. Except for the reduced exercise price of the warrants
through their scheduled expiration date, the terms of the Warrants remain unchanged. Holders of Warrants that desire to exercise
their Warrants should contact their brokers and instruct them to exercise the Warrants prior to the Expiration Time.</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0; text-align: justify">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0; text-align: justify">As of today&rsquo;s date, approximately
471,347 <B>ZNWAW</B> Warrants and 2,953,595 <B>ZNWAZ</B> Warrants are outstanding. Zion&rsquo;s common stock is listed on the NASDAQ
GLOBAL Market under the symbol <B>ZN</B>. Zion recommends that Warrant holders obtain current market quotations for Zion&rsquo;s
securities before deciding whether or not to exercise their Warrants.</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0; text-align: justify">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0; text-align: justify">Mr. John Brown, Zion&rsquo;s Executive
Board Chairman and Chief Executive Officer said: &ldquo;For the shareholders who have supported Zion through our past stock and
warrant offerings, we want to substantially reduce the warrant exercise price to provide a benefit to you before the warrants expire
as a way of expressing our gratitude for your ongoing support.&rdquo;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0; text-align: justify">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0; text-align: justify">ZION&rsquo;S BOARD OF DIRECTORS HAS APPROVED
THE WARRANT EXERCISE PRICE REDUCTION. HOWEVER, NEITHER ZION NOR ANY OF ITS DIRECTORS, OFFICERS OR EMPLOYEES MAKES ANY RECOMMENDATION
AS TO WHETHER TO EXERCISE WARRANTS. EACH HOLDER OF A WARRANT MUST MAKE ITS OWN DECISION AS TO WHETHER TO EXERCISE A WARRANT.</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0; text-align: justify">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0; text-align: justify">The information above does not constitute
an offer to buy or exchange securities or constitute the solicitation of an offer to sell or exchange any securities in Zion.</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0; text-align: justify">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0; text-align: justify">Zion Oil &amp; Gas, a Delaware corporation,
explores for oil and gas in Israel in areas located onshore between Haifa and Tel Aviv. It currently holds three petroleum exploration
licenses: the Joseph License (on approximately 83,272 acres) and the Asher-Menashe License (on approximately 78,824 acres) between
Netanya, in the south, and Haifa, in the north, and the Jordan Valley License (on approximately 55,845 acres), just south of the
Sea of Galilee. The total license area amounts to approximately 218,000 acres.</P>
