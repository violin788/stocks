    <div style="DISPLAY: block; TEXT-INDENT: 0pt"><br></div>
    <div style="DISPLAY: block; MARGIN-LEFT: 0pt; TEXT-INDENT: 0pt; MARGIN-RIGHT: 0pt" align="left"><font style="DISPLAY: inline; FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman">The open
hole test was conducted first and was performed in the deepest geologic zone of
interest.&#160;&#160;Following the conclusion of this test, we moved uphole and
performed a third test on a separate interval, this time in a cased hole section
of the well.&#160;&#160;In each case, the test procedure consisted of two
separate flow and shut-in periods in which the geologic interval was evaluated
for productivity and reservoir pressure.&#160;&#160;We again used Schlumberger
Oilfield Services equipment and personnel for this testing work.</font></div>
    <div style="DISPLAY: block; TEXT-INDENT: 0pt"><br></div>
    <div style="DISPLAY: block; MARGIN-LEFT: 0pt; TEXT-INDENT: 0pt; MARGIN-RIGHT: 0pt" align="left"><font style="DISPLAY: inline; FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman">Currently,
we are performing a &#8216;swabbing&#8217; operation on this latest test interval as a final
means to gauge hydrocarbon potential.&#160;&#160;Swabbing is an operation by
which the heavier fluid column from the wellbore is manually withdrawn from the
well.&#160;&#160;This has the effect of lowering the hydrostatic pressure in the
well and allowing potentially lighter fluid (hydrocarbons) from the rock to be
produced through natural flow.</font></div>
