    <div style="DISPLAY: block; MARGIN-LEFT: 0pt; TEXT-INDENT: 36pt; MARGIN-RIGHT: 0pt" align="left"><font style="DISPLAY: inline; FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman">Zion Oil
&amp; Gas, Inc. ("Zion" or the "Company") previously disclosed in a current
report on Form 8-K filed on April 16, 2010, that following an analysis of the
results of the production testing of&#160;&#160;the Ma'anit-Rehoboth # 2 well,
it has decided to forego further production testing with respect to the well and
has begun plans to temporarily suspend this well.&#160;Following a subsequent
&#8216;swabbing&#8217; operation, as a final means to gauge hydrocarbon potential,
management has concluded that commercial quantities of hydrocarbons are not
present in the Ma'anit-Rehoboth # 2 well. The Company is in the process of
suspending operations on the well.</font></div>
    <div style="DISPLAY: block; MARGIN-LEFT: 0pt; TEXT-INDENT: 0pt; MARGIN-RIGHT: 0pt" align="justify">&#160;</div>
    <div style="DISPLAY: block; MARGIN-LEFT: 0pt; TEXT-INDENT: 36pt; MARGIN-RIGHT: 0pt" align="left"><font style="DISPLAY: inline; FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman">As a
result of the actions described above, the Company&#8217;s board of directors
concluded, on April 23, 2010, that under generally accepted accounting
principles applicable to the Company, the Company will have to record an
estimated non-cash impairment charge of approximately $18,000,000 to its
unproved oil and gas properties that will be included in Zion's unaudited
statements of operations for the quarter ending March 31, 2010. The Company
estimates that, with respect to the impairment charge, no future cash
expenditures will be required.</font></div>
    <div style="DISPLAY: block; MARGIN-LEFT: 0pt; TEXT-INDENT: 36pt; MARGIN-RIGHT: 0pt" align="left">&#160;</div>

that the Ma'anit-Rehoboth # 2 well may have further utility as a potential
offset well to the next well planned to be drilled (designated by us, the
Ma'anit-Joseph #3 well) which will target a completely different geologic
formation in Permian aged rocks.&#160;&#160;Preliminary planning of this next
well is currently under way.</a></font></div>
