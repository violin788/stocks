<div style="DISPLAY: block; MARGIN-LEFT: 0pt; TEXT-INDENT: 0pt; MARGIN-RIGHT: 0pt" align="center"><font style="DISPLAY: inline; FONT-WEIGHT: bold; FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman">ZION OIL WARRANT EXERCISE RESULTS</font></div>

<div style="DISPLAY: block; TEXT-INDENT: 0pt"><br>
</div>

<div style="DISPLAY: block; MARGIN-LEFT: 0pt; TEXT-INDENT: 0pt; MARGIN-RIGHT: 0pt" align="justify"><font style="DISPLAY: inline; FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman"><font id="TAB1" style="MARGIN-LEFT: 45.8pt"></font>Dallas, Texas and Caesarea, Israel &#8211; March 11, 2011: Zion Oil &amp; Gas, Inc. (NASDAQ GM: <font style="DISPLAY: inline; FONT-WEIGHT: bold">ZN, ZNWAW, ZNWAZ</font>) today announced that 100,600 of its publicly traded warrants quoted under the symbol <font style="DISPLAY: inline; FONT-WEIGHT: bold">ZNWAW</font> were exercised under the program whereby holders could exercise warrants at $4, versus the $7 exercise price provided by the original terms of the warrants, generating cash of $402,400 for Zion. The reduced exercise price program, which was in effect since January 3, 2011, expired on March 7, 2011, and the original exercise price of $7 has been
reinstituted for all remaining unexercised warrants with the symbol <font style="DISPLAY: inline; FONT-WEIGHT: bold">ZNWAW</font>. Warrants (with symbol <font style="DISPLAY: inline; FONT-WEIGHT: bold">ZNWAW</font>) for 506,079 shares remain outstanding and continue to be exercisable through January 31, 2012.</font></div>

<div style="DISPLAY: block; TEXT-INDENT: 0pt"><br>
</div>

<div style="DISPLAY: block; MARGIN-LEFT: 0pt; TEXT-INDENT: 0pt; MARGIN-RIGHT: 0pt" align="justify"><font style="DISPLAY: inline; FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman"><font id="TAB1" style="MARGIN-LEFT: 45.8pt"></font>Concurrently, Zion received an additional $301,040 in cash from warrant exercises of its other publicly traded warrants trading under the symbol <font style="DISPLAY: inline; FONT-WEIGHT: bold">ZNWAZ</font>, which were issued in connection with the closing of Zion&#8217;s most recent rights offering and were first quoted in December 2010. Warrants (with symbol <font style="DISPLAY: inline; FONT-WEIGHT: bold">ZNWAZ</font>) for 3,567,561 shares, which are exercisable at $4 through December 31, 2012, remain outstanding.</font></div>

<div style="DISPLAY: block; TEXT-INDENT: 0pt"><br>
</div>

<div style="DISPLAY: block; MARGIN-LEFT: 0pt; TEXT-INDENT: 36pt; MARGIN-RIGHT: 0pt" align="left"><font style="DISPLAY: inline; FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman">Richard Rinberg, CEO of Zion, stated, "We temporarily reduced the exercise price on our <font style="DISPLAY: inline; FONT-WEIGHT: bold">ZNWAW</font> warrants in order to both raise funds for our exploration work in Israel and be fair to all of Zion&#8217;s investors. We are glad to have afforded this opportunity to our investors.&#8221;</font></div>

<div style="DISPLAY: block; TEXT-INDENT: 0pt"><br>
</div>

<div style="DISPLAY: block; MARGIN-LEFT: 0pt; TEXT-INDENT: 36pt; MARGIN-RIGHT: 0pt" align="left"><font style="DISPLAY: inline; FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman">Zion is currently drilling its Ma&#8217;anit-Joseph #3 well in Northern Israel and has reached a drilling depth of approximately 14,576 feet (4,443 meters). The Ma&#8217;anit-Joseph #3 well has targets in both the Triassic and the Permian geological zones. The primary target is a Permian age formation, expected at a depth of over 19,000 feet (5,790 meters), making the Ma&#8217;anit-Joseph #3 well one of the deepest wells ever drilled onshore Israel.</font></div>

<div style="DISPLAY: block; TEXT-INDENT: 0pt"><br>

<div style="DISPLAY: block; MARGIN-LEFT: 0pt; TEXT-INDENT: 36pt; MARGIN-RIGHT: 0pt" align="left"><font style="DISPLAY: inline; FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman">For updates on the drilling activity please visit Zion&#8217;s website <font style="DISPLAY: inline; FONT-WEIGHT: bold; TEXT-DECORATION: underline">www.zionoil.com</font>.</font></div>

<div style="DISPLAY: block; MARGIN-LEFT: 0pt; TEXT-INDENT: 36pt; MARGIN-RIGHT: 0pt" align="left"><font style="DISPLAY: inline; FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman">Zion&#8217;s common stock trades on the NASDAQ Global Market under the symbol &#8220;ZN&#8221; and Zion&#8217;s warrants trade under the symbols &#8220;ZNWAW&#8221; and &#8220;ZNWAZ&#8221;.</font></div>

<div style="DISPLAY: block; MARGIN-LEFT: 0pt; TEXT-INDENT: 36pt; MARGIN-RIGHT: 0pt" align="left"><font style="DISPLAY: inline; FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman">Zion Oil &amp; Gas, a Delaware corporation, explores for oil and gas in Israel in areas located on-shore between Haifa and Tel Aviv. It currently holds two petroleum exploration licenses, the Joseph and the Asher-Menashe Licenses, between Netanya, in the south, and Haifa, in the north, covering a total of approximately 162,000 acres.</font></div>
