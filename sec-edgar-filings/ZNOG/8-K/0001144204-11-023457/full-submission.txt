<div style="DISPLAY: block; TEXT-INDENT: 0pt"><br>
</div>

<div style="DISPLAY: block; MARGIN-LEFT: 0pt; TEXT-INDENT: 36pt; MARGIN-RIGHT: 0pt" align="justify"><font style="DISPLAY: inline; FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman">In connection with his appointment as Chief Financial Officer of Zion Oil &amp; Gas, Inc. (the &#8220;Company&#8221;), the Company&#8217;s Board of Directors, at the recommendation of the Compensation Committee, on April 15, 2011, has determined to adjust the compensation payable to Ilan Sheena.&#160;&#160;Mr. Sheena has been employed by the Company since October 2009 and is currently employed under the terms of an Amended and Restated Employment Agreement dated as of January 1, 2010 (the &#8220;Employment Agreement&#8221;). Prior to his appointment on March 31, 2011, as Chief Financial Officer, he served as Vice President and Managing Director of the Company's Israel Branch. Mr. Sheena will continue to reside in Israel.</font></div>

<div style="DISPLAY: block; TEXT-INDENT: 0pt"><br>
</div>

<div style="DISPLAY: block; MARGIN-LEFT: 0pt; TEXT-INDENT: 36pt; MARGIN-RIGHT: 0pt" align="justify"><font style="DISPLAY: inline; FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman">Effective April 1, 2011, Mr. Sheena&#8217;s base salary under the Employment Agreement has been increased to $144,000 per annum and, in connection with his appointment, Mr. Sheena is entitled to options (the &#8220;Options&#8221;) under the Company&#8217;s 2005 Stock Option Plan to purchase, in respect of each employment year, up to 7,500 shares of the Company&#8217;s common stock, par value $0.01 per share, which options are scheduled to vest in quarterly installments of 1,875 Options. The Employment Agreement also contains customary non-solicitation provisions.</font></div>

<div style="DISPLAY: block; TEXT-INDENT: 0pt"><br>
</div>

<div style="DISPLAY: block; MARGIN-LEFT: 0pt; TEXT-INDENT: 36pt; MARGIN-RIGHT: 0pt" align="justify"><font style="DISPLAY: inline; FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman">Mr. Sheena can terminate the employment agreement and the relationship thereunder at any time upon 60 business days' notice. The Company is entitled to terminate the agreement at any time, for any reason other than "Cause" (as defined the employment agreement), in which case, Mr. Sheena will be entitled to the greater of three months&#8217; salary (and related social benefits) or severance payments then payable under Israeli law.</font></div>

<div style="DISPLAY: block; TEXT-INDENT: 0pt"><br>
</div>

<div style="DISPLAY: block; MARGIN-LEFT: 0pt; TEXT-INDENT: 36pt; MARGIN-RIGHT: 0pt" align="left"><font style="DISPLAY: inline; FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman">The Company and Mr. Sheena are in the process of finalizing the terms of a new employment agreement to reflect his new position.</font></div>
