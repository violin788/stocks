<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0; text-align: justify">I am happy to announce some excellent news.
This past week, Zion Oil &amp; Gas (Zion Oil) and Lapidoth Israel Oil Prospectors Corp. Ltd. (Lapidoth) signed a Memorandum of
Understanding (MoU) that I believe will not only be good for both Zion Oil and Lapidoth, but also for the State of Israel. (Lapidoth,
a forerunner of onshore oil prospecting in Israel, was incorporated in 1959 and its securities are traded on the Tel Aviv Stock
Exchange.)</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0; text-align: justify">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0; text-align: justify"><FONT STYLE="font-size: 10pt">The MoU outlines
plans to establish a company, tentatively named &ldquo;Zion-Lapidoth Drilling&rdquo;, which is to purchase a drilling rig suitable
for drilling wells to a depth of up to 25,000 feet. The MoU contemplates that Zion-Lapidoth Drilling will be 50% owned by Zion
Oil and 50% by Lapidoth. The anticipated cost of the drilling rig is up to US$ 15 Million and each party will share equally in
the financing of Zion-Lapidoth Drilling. The MoU provides that Zion Oil will retain Zion-Lapidoth Drilling for its drilling program
and when not required by Zion Oil, Zion-Lapidoth Drilling may lease the drilling rig to third party oil and gas drilling entities</FONT>.</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0; text-align: justify">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0; text-align: justify">The MoU is subject to certain standard
conditions, including the execution of definitive purchase agreements, required approvals and the completion of acceptable due
diligence by the parties. Additionally, the implementation of the MoU is subject to Zion raising at least $10 million within the
next 12 months. The MoU also provides that for the next 12 months Lapidoth will have a right of first refusal to drill wells, as
needed, in accordance with Zion Oil&rsquo;s work program.</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0; text-align: justify">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0; text-align: justify">The establishment of Zion-Lapidoth Drilling
will help alleviate a longstanding concern about our ability to continue to drill exploratory wells in Israel without dependence
on an outside third party. Zion will also benefit from Lapidoth's significant experience in operating drilling projects in Israel.
We believe that the ultimate establishment of Zion-Lapidoth Drilling will take Zion Oil, as a business, to a completely new level.</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0; text-align: justify">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0; text-align: justify">&nbsp;&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0">You can read the full text of the MOU at <U>www.zionoil.com</U>.</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0; text-align: center">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0; text-align: justify">In order to address some of the recent
regulatory changes relating to drilling wells in Israel, this past week, we attended a meeting in Tel Aviv at the Chamber of Commerce
(Oil &amp; Gas Exploration Group) with Minister Dr. Uzi Landau.</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0; text-align: justify">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0; text-align: justify">Dr. Landau was generous with his time and
we, along with others in the local oil &amp; gas industry, were given the opportunity to present both our problems and our recommendations.</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0; text-align: justify">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0; text-align: justify">Last month's update detailed the work we
are doing on our various exploration licenses. This past month we have been progressing that work and are looking forward to further
field operations in the coming weeks, including re-entering the Elijah #3 well and acquiring additional seismic data.</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0; text-align: justify">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0">Some time ago, 'Globes' (one of Israel's leading business publications)
published an interview with Fred Zeidman, who has been chairman of the board or a director of various Texas based oil &amp; gas
drilling / exploration / refining companies.</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0">Fred Zeidman made a number of comments regarding the (very bright)
future for oil exploration in Israel, including:</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0">&nbsp;</P>
