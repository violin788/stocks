    <div style="DISPLAY: block; MARGIN-LEFT: 0pt; TEXT-INDENT: 0pt; MARGIN-RIGHT: 0pt" align="center"><font style="DISPLAY: inline; FONT-WEIGHT: bold; FONT-SIZE: 8pt; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman">The

    <div style="DISPLAY: block; MARGIN-LEFT: 0pt; TEXT-INDENT: 0pt; MARGIN-RIGHT: 0pt" align="justify">&#160;</div>
    <div style="DISPLAY: block; MARGIN-LEFT: 0pt; TEXT-INDENT: 0pt; MARGIN-RIGHT: 0pt" align="left"><font style="DISPLAY: inline; FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman"><font style="DISPLAY: inline; FONT-WEIGHT: bold; TEXT-DECORATION: underline">Drilling
Operations at the Ma'anit-Joseph #3 Wellsite</font></font></div>
    <div style="DISPLAY: block; MARGIN-LEFT: 0pt; TEXT-INDENT: 0pt; MARGIN-RIGHT: 0pt" align="justify">&#160;</div>
    <div style="DISPLAY: block; MARGIN-LEFT: 0pt; TEXT-INDENT: 0pt; MARGIN-RIGHT: 0pt" align="left"><font style="DISPLAY: inline; FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman">On August
26, 2010, drilling operations began on the Ma'anit-Joseph #3 well, in our Joseph
License area, onshore Northern Israel.</font></div>
    <div style="DISPLAY: block; MARGIN-LEFT: 0pt; TEXT-INDENT: 0pt; MARGIN-RIGHT: 0pt" align="justify">&#160;</div>
    <div style="DISPLAY: block; MARGIN-LEFT: 0pt; TEXT-INDENT: 0pt; MARGIN-RIGHT: 0pt" align="left"><font style="DISPLAY: inline; FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman">Further
to our last update, drilling of the 6-1/8&#8221; hole continued uneventfully to a
depth of 5,023 meters (16,480 feet) when it became necessary to perform a
routine bit change.</font></div>
    <div style="DISPLAY: block; MARGIN-LEFT: 0pt; TEXT-INDENT: 0pt; MARGIN-RIGHT: 0pt" align="justify">&#160;</div>
    <div style="DISPLAY: block; MARGIN-LEFT: 0pt; TEXT-INDENT: 0pt; MARGIN-RIGHT: 0pt" align="left"><font style="DISPLAY: inline; FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman">In
keeping with oilfield "best practice" and as done periodically during the course
of drilling this well, prior to the bit change, an inclination survey was
conducted in order to determine the angle from which the bit has deviated from
the vertical during drilling. However, after the inclination survey was
completed, we were unable to retrieve the drill pipe from the hole.</font></div>
    <div style="DISPLAY: block; MARGIN-LEFT: 0pt; TEXT-INDENT: 0pt; MARGIN-RIGHT: 0pt" align="justify">&#160;</div>

    <div style="DISPLAY: block; MARGIN-LEFT: 0pt; TEXT-INDENT: 0pt; MARGIN-RIGHT: 0pt" align="left">&#160;</div>
    <div style="DISPLAY: block; MARGIN-LEFT: 0pt; TEXT-INDENT: 0pt; MARGIN-RIGHT: 0pt" align="left"><font style="DISPLAY: inline; FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman">A
&#8220;free-point&#8221; tool was run in the hole and we determined that the pipe was stuck
at a depth of about 4,770 meters (15,650 feet), at a point approximately 230
meters (755 feet) above the drill bit.&#160;&#160;The drill string was
&#8220;backed-off&#8221; at this point allowing us to recover all but the approximately 230
meters (755 feet) of pipe which remained stuck in the hole.</font></div>
    <div style="DISPLAY: block; MARGIN-LEFT: 0pt; TEXT-INDENT: 0pt; MARGIN-RIGHT: 0pt" align="justify">&#160;</div>
    <div style="DISPLAY: block; MARGIN-LEFT: 0pt; TEXT-INDENT: 0pt; MARGIN-RIGHT: 0pt" align="left"><font style="DISPLAY: inline; FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman">The
back-off operation was accomplished by applying reverse torque on the drill pipe
and detonating a small explosive charge inside a selected threaded connection
(at its &#8220;free&#8221; point).&#160;&#160;The force of the explosion together with the
reverse torque typically results in an instantaneous uncoupling of the threaded
connection.</font></div>
    <div style="DISPLAY: block; MARGIN-LEFT: 0pt; TEXT-INDENT: 0pt; MARGIN-RIGHT: 0pt" align="justify">&#160;</div>
    <div style="DISPLAY: block; MARGIN-LEFT: 0pt; TEXT-INDENT: 0pt; MARGIN-RIGHT: 0pt" align="left"><font style="DISPLAY: inline; FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman">With a
long section of &#8216;fish&#8217; remaining in a small diameter (6-1/8&#8221;) hole at great
depth, conventional fishing options are extremely problematic.&#160;&#160;After
careful consideration, it was determined that the best course of action was to
forego the fish remaining in the hole and sidetrack the well.</font></div>
    <div style="DISPLAY: block; MARGIN-LEFT: 0pt; TEXT-INDENT: 0pt; MARGIN-RIGHT: 0pt" align="justify">&#160;</div>
    <div style="DISPLAY: block; MARGIN-LEFT: 0pt; TEXT-INDENT: 0pt; MARGIN-RIGHT: 0pt" align="left"><font style="DISPLAY: inline; FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman">Sidetracking
a well means to use directional drilling tools to start a new wellbore section
from a point in an existing well.&#160;&#160;It will enable us to bypass the
'problem' in our well i.e. the uncased portion of 8-1/2&#8221; hole that was drilled
but operationally could not be cased.&#160;&#160;So, we will start a new 6-1/8&#8221;
sidetrack hole at a point just below the last section of cased hole, at
approximately 4,000 meters (13,120 feet).</font></div>
    <div style="DISPLAY: block; MARGIN-LEFT: 0pt; TEXT-INDENT: 0pt; MARGIN-RIGHT: 0pt" align="justify">&#160;</div>
    <div style="DISPLAY: block; MARGIN-LEFT: 0pt; TEXT-INDENT: 0pt; MARGIN-RIGHT: 0pt" align="left"><font style="DISPLAY: inline; FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman">We plan
to redrill approximately 1,000 meters (3,280 feet), however, as we will be
drilling through what are now &#8216;known&#8217; rock layers, the rate of redrilling this
section should be much improved as compared to the rate in which this section
was drilled in the original hole.</font></div>
    <div style="DISPLAY: block; MARGIN-LEFT: 0pt; TEXT-INDENT: 0pt; MARGIN-RIGHT: 0pt" align="justify">&#160;</div>
    <div style="DISPLAY: block; MARGIN-LEFT: 0pt; TEXT-INDENT: 0pt; MARGIN-RIGHT: 0pt" align="left"><font style="DISPLAY: inline; FONT-SIZE: 10pt; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman">As of
Friday, February 18, we have already set the cement plug in the existing hole
and are waiting for it to harden prior to restarting drilling. We are waiting
for directional drilling equipment to arrive (via sea) from Italy and expect to
resume drilling operations next week.</font></div>
