<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0"><B>ITEM 1.01. Entry into a Material Definitive Agreement</B></P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0; text-indent: 0.5in">On June 6, 2012, Zion Oil &amp; Gas, Inc.
(the &quot;Company&quot;) implemented a temporary reduction in the exercise price of all of its publicly traded warrants trading
on the NASDAQ Global Market under the symbols <B>ZNWAL</B>, <B>ZNWAW </B>and<B> ZNWAZ. </B>Under the temporary exercise price reduction
program, these warrants can be exercised, through August 15, 2012, at the reduced warrant exercise price of $1.75. Each warrant
so exercised produces one share of the Company's common stock, trading on the NASDAQ Global Market under the symbol <B>ZN.</B></P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0; text-indent: 0.5in">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0; text-indent: 0.5in">The <B>ZNWAL</B> warrants are scheduled
to expire on August 15, 2012. Following the end of the reduced exercise price period, the original warrant exercise price of $4
and $7 for each of the <B>ZNWAZ</B> Warrants and the <B>ZNWAW</B> Warrants, respectively, will be reinstituted through December
31, 2012, the scheduled expiration date of these warrants.</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0; text-indent: 0.5in">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0; text-indent: 0.5in">Except for the temporary reduction in the
warrant exercise prices as herein provided, all of the terms and conditions contained in the applicable Warrant instruments will
continue in full force and effect.</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0; text-indent: 0.5in">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0; text-align: center"><B>Zion Oil Reduces the Exercise Price of
Outstanding Warrants </B></P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0; text-align: center">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0; text-align: justify"><B>Dallas, Texas and Caesarea, Israel &ndash;
June 6, 2012: Zion Oil &amp; Gas, Inc.</B> (NASDAQ GM: <B>ZN, ZNWAL, ZNWAW, ZNWAZ</B>) today announced that it has temporarily
reduced the exercise price to $1.75 of all of its outstanding publicly traded warrants quoted under the symbols &ldquo;ZNWAL&rdquo;,
&ldquo;ZNWAW&rdquo; and &ldquo;ZNWAZ until 5:00 p.m. eastern time on August 15, 2012 (the &ldquo;Expiration Time&rdquo;). Any and
all warrants properly exercised in accordance with their respective terms prior to the Expiration Time will be accepted by Zion
at the reduced exercise price and one share of registered common stock per warrant will be issued to the exercising warrant holder.
The original exercise price for the ZNWAL Warrants is $3.50; for the ZNWAZ Warrants, $4.00 and for the ZNWAW Warrants is $7.00.</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0; text-align: justify">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0; text-align: justify">After the Expiration Time, warrants under
the symbol ZNWAL will expire with no further value and the exercise prices included in the original terms of the warrants quoted
under the symbols ZNWAW and ZNWAZ will be reinstituted. Except for the reduced exercise price of the warrants prior to the Expiration
Time, the terms of the Warrants remain unchanged. Holders of Warrants that desire to exercise their Warrants should contact their
brokers and instruct them to exercise the Warrants prior to the Expiration Time.</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0; text-align: justify">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0; text-align: justify">As of today&rsquo;s date, approximately
9,820,000 ZNWAL Warrants, 504,000 ZNWAW Warrants and 3,295,000 ZNWAZ Warrants are outstanding. Zion&rsquo;s common stock is listed
on the NASDAQ GLOBAL Market under the symbol ZN. Zion recommends that Warrant holders obtain current market quotations for Zion&rsquo;s
securities before deciding whether or not to exercise their Warrants.</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0; text-align: justify">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0; text-align: justify">Richard Rinberg, CEO of Zion, stated, &ldquo;In
order to progress our drilling program and establish our new drilling company (tentatively named Zion-Lapidoth Drilling), we evaluated
different ways to raise capital. We felt that it was important to allow our warrant holders the opportunity to invest in Zion through
a temporary warrant reset program. Consequently, we are pleased to offer our warrant holders the opportunity to exercise their
warrants at a reduced exercise price of $1.75 and hope that a substantial number of these warrants will be exercised.</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0; text-align: justify">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0; text-align: justify">We want to be fair to all of Zion&rsquo;s
warrant holders and believe that a reduction to a uniform warrant exercise price for an identical period achieves that aim. In
addition to potentially raising a significant amount of capital for Zion, we believe that a reduction in the number of our outstanding
warrants will simplify our capital structure and reduce the overhang of those warrants on the Company&rsquo;s common stock.&rdquo;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0; text-align: justify">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0; text-align: justify">ZION&rsquo;S BOARD OF DIRECTORS HAS APPROVED
THE WARRANT EXERCISE PRICE REDUCTION. HOWEVER, NEITHER ZION NOR ANY OF ITS DIRECTORS, OFFICERS OR EMPLOYEES MAKES ANY RECOMMENDATION
AS TO WHETHER TO EXERCISE WARRANTS. EACH HOLDER OF A WARRANT MUST MAKE ITS OWN DECISION AS TO WHETHER TO EXERCISE A WARRANT.</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0; text-align: justify">&nbsp;</P>
